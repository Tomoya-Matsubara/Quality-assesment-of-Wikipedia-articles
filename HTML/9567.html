Jessica Fox (born 11 June 1994) is a French-born, Australian slalom canoer who has competed since 2006.

Personal

Fox was born on 11 June 1994 in Marseilles, France.123 She currently lives in Penrith, New South Wales.4 Fox's parents are Richard and Myriam, who both competed at the Olympic, her father for Great Britain at the 1992 Games and her mother at the 1996 Games where she won a bronze medal for France.5678 Her father is Second Vice President of the International Canoe Federation.910 Her father is Australian Canoeing's current high-performance manager,11 and a five time world champion.121314

She attended Blaxland High School, and finished her HSC year by being first in New South Wales in the PDHPE in 2011 and had an ATAR score of 99.1.1516 She is currently studying at the University of Sydney where she is working on a degree in media/communications.1718

She is bilingual in English and French.1920

She had had a back injury.21

In May 2012, she spoke at the Chullora Public School.22

She celebrated her 18th birthday on 11 June 2012 in Cardiff while competing at a World Cup event.23

Canoeing

Fox races in K1 and C1 events.24 Her coach is her mother.2526 She started in the sport in 2005 by canoeing on the Nepan River.2728 She has a scholarship and is affiliated with the Australian Institute of Sport and the New South Wales Institute of Sport.29 In club competitions, she represents the Penrith Valley Canoe Club.30

In September 2009, Fox made her Australian senior national team bid.31 She competed at the 2009 World Championships in Seu in the C1 event.32 She competed at the ICF World Ranking in Merano, Italy in 2009 where she finished third in the K1 event.33 At the 2009 AYOF event in Penrith, New South Wales, she finished first in the women's K1 event.34

Fox won a bronze medal in the C-1 event at the 2010 ICF Canoe Slalom World Championships in Tacen.35 At the 2010 Summer Youth Olympics in Singapore, she won the gold in the girls' K-1 slalom event.3637 In 2010, she finished 1st in the women's C1 event at the ICF Slalom World Cup in Seu, Spain. In 2010, she finished 1st in the women's K1 event at the Junior World Championships in Foix, France. In 2010, she finished 1st in the women's C1 event at the Junior World Championships in Foix, France. In 2010, she finished 3rd in the women's C1 event at the World Championships in Sloka, Slovakia. In 2010, she finished 5th in the women's K1 event at the World Championships in Sloka, Slovakia. In 2010, she finished 1st in the women's K1 event at the Oceania Open in Penrith, New South Wales. In 2010, she finished 1st in the women's C1 event at the Oceania Open in Penrith, New South Wales. In 2010, she finished 1st in the women's C1 event at the National Championships in Eildon, Victoria. In 2010, she finished 3rd in the women's K1 event at the National Championships in Eildon, Victoria.38

In 2011, she won gold medals in the C1 women's event at World Cups 2 and 3.39 At the 2011 World Championships in Szeged, Hungary, she finished 19th, which gave Australia an automatic spot in the event at the 2012 Summer Olympics.40

She won silver medals at Australian Open and the Oceania Championships in the women's K1 event in 2011.41

She earned a spot in the women<U+2019>s K1 event representing Australia at the 2012 Summer Olympics, in what will be her Olympic debut.42

In April 2012, she trained on the Olympic course in London.43

She left Australia for overseas in advance of the 2012 Summer Olympics in May 2012. Her pre-Olympic schedule included World Cup competitions in Wales, France and Spain, and the Junior World Championship in the United States.44

In June 2012, she finished seventh in a World Cup race in Cardiff.45 She was the only Australian to make the finals, with her Australian teammates having been knocked out earlier.46 She finished in the top three in the women's C1 event.47

Honours

She was the 2010 Penrith Press Junior Sports Star of the year and NewsLocal Medal winner.48 She has also served as the ambassador for the Premier<U+2019>s Sporting Challenge.49 In 2010, she was also named the Cumberland Courier Junior Sport Star, NSWIS Junior Athlete of the Year and the Pierre de Coubertin AOC award.5051 In 2009, 2010 and 2011, she was named the Junior Canoeist of the Year Australian Canoeing.52 In 2011, she was named the Australian Canoeing Athlete of the Year.53 She earned the AIS Secondary Education award in 2011.54
