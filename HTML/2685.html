"Forever Is Over" is a pop song performed by British girl group The Saturdays. The song was written by: Louis Biancaniello, Sam Watters, Kahmarl Gordon, James Bourne and produced by: Sam Watters and Louis Biancaniello. It was released as the lead single from the group's second studio album Wordshaker on 4 October 2009.

The song peaked at number two and number nine in the UK and Ireland respectively becoming their highest charting single to date (excluding their charity release "Just Can't Get Enough").

Background

"Forever Is Over" was revealed as the title for the group's second album's lead single on July 25. A sixty-second preview of track was premiered on August 22 with the full song premiering on radio the following day.1

"Forever Is Over" was originally written for former American Idol winner Kelly Clarkson by former Busted star James Bourne, who is well known for his writing for the likes of Jordin Sparks' single "Battlefield". Group member Mollie King explained to the Daily Star how the group came to record it instead: The song was also wanted by American pop rock artist P!nk.2 The track was co-written and produced by Louis Biancaniello and Sam Watters, famed for their writing of Keri Hilson' single "Energy" and work with Jordin Sparks.

Una Healy opens the song and sings the first verse and the song's bridge. Rochelle Wiseman sings the calls in the second verse and the third verse. Mollie King sings three responses in the second verse. Vanessa White sings one response in the second verse, the chorus' solos vocals, backing vocals during third verse, the four responses in the fourth verse, and the end of the song. Frankie Sandford sings the calls in the fourth verse and backing vocals during the second and third chorus.

The single was accompanied by a B-side entitled "I Can't Wait" which was co-written by The Saturdays together with Hannah Robinson and producers Ben Macklin, Julian Peake and Richard Stannard.3 David Balls of entertainment website Digital Spy said of the b-side "Another decent B-side from the girls [...] A laid-back electro poppy offering that recalls the more subdued moments from their debut album."4

Critical reception

The song received positive reviews from music critics: Nick Levine from Digital Spy said "'Forever Is Over' finds them shifting away from glossy electropop and taking a few stiletto steps into pop rock territory," and further describing the song as having a "Big, beltalong chorus that hits its target, and that Una Healy shines on the guitary middle 8."5 The BBC Chart Blog elaborated on the single as being in the "Highest realms of the UK pop league up there with the Pixie Lotts and Girls Alouds of the world," further describing it as sounding "Slick" and "Big" with a "Mahoosive, dancealong, brilliant chorus."6 Musosguide reviewed "Forever Is Over" as a "Deliciously catchy tune that marks a welcome return to the electric guitar to a pop market that is saturated beyond recognition with electro-sounds."7 OK! Magazine labelled the song as "An infectious synth pop tune".8 TeenToday.co.uk mentioned the track as having "A killer soar of a chorus, which Vanessa is all over like a gorgeously-voiced rash" and as "Radio-friendly as pop can get."9 Muumuse.com had the following to say: "Radio-friendly doesn't even begin to describe it - the chorus is simply massive."10 TheChemistryIsDead.com described "Forever Is Over" as "Rockier than their other stuff but it's very sleek and polished."11 WessexScene.co.uk reviewed the single as "A sophisticated more pop rock breakaway from the girls' previous pure pop style and has a more transatlantic feel to it" further saying that "It is vocally impressive. Una's attitudey solo stands out as well as Vanessa's impressive warbling throughout the chorus."12 Entertainment-focus.com reviewed the lead single as "A slow-building pop stormer and that all five girls sharing vocals adds a harder edge to their normally polished pop sound."13 Whilst Yahoo! Music's view of track included it having "Sultry verses and a full throttle chorus which suggests a new, more sophisticated direction to put them on par with their rivals."14

Music Video

"Forever Is Over" was accompanied by a music video for its single release. The music video was shot on July 29, 2009.17 The music video was directed by Trudy Bellinger.

The music video takes on an urban theme to it and sees the girls in a house and around a city singing. The video involves no dance routines and ends with the girls leaving a nightclub. During Una Healy's middle eight of the song the girls can be seen bathed in atmospheric blue lighting whilst wearing black outfits. Other scenes include Mollie King licking her thumb and smearing her heart-shaped tattoo saying 'Forever' as a metaphor for saying that "Forever Is Over". In another seen Una can be seen deleting the L from 'lover' to spell 'over' on her laptop, also as a metaphor to contrast with the song's title.

Promotion

The single was also performed at various British music festivals and on various television shows as promotion during its release period. On September 12, "Forever Is Over" was played live for the first time at the Annual Freedom Festival held in Hull.18 Later that month, on September 28 the group was interviewed on The Paul O'Grady Show where they performed "Forever Is Over" for the first time on live television.19 On October 3, a day before the release of "Forever Is Over," the girls performed at G-A-Y in London.20 A day after the single's physical CD release, the band performed the song on ITV1's This Morning. On the same day the girls were interviewed on The 5:19 Show where an exclusive acoustic performance of "Forever Is Over" was recorded.21 The group then appeared on October 12 for a performance of the song on The Alan Titchmarsh Show.22 October 17, the group performed at the Hollyoaks Music Festival which saw the group perform "Forever Is Over" as well the title track from their second album.23 The group then performed the single along with the album track "Deeper" and their previous single "Issues" as part of 'The Biz Sessions' for British newspaper The Sun on October 19.24 On November 6, the group performed at the premiere of Disney film "A Christmas Carol" alongside R&B singer songwriter Taio Cruz and on November 14 the girls performed at "The Christmas Lights" at Cheshire Oaks.25 On November 29 the girls performed "Forever Is Over" on T4's Stars of 2009 alongside British hip hop trio N-Dubz at Earl's Court in London.26

"Forever Is Over" debuted and peaked on the Irish Singles Chart, issued October 8, at number nine. It was the second highest new entry of the week. The single spent a total of five weeks within the chart29 and gave the group their second Irish top ten hit after Comic Relief single "Just Can't Get Enough" peaked at number six in March 2009. On October 11, "Forever Is Over" debuted and peaked at number two on the UK Singles Chart.30 The single became the group's fifth top ten hit and second single to reach a peak of number two in the United Kingdom.
