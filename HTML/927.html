Arlen F. Gregorio (born 1931 in San Francisco, California, United States) is an American attorney, politician and commercial mediator. He was a member of the California State Senate from 1970 to 1978. As a Senator he chaired the Senate Health and Welfare Committee from 1979 to 1984 and was an elected member of the San Mateo County Board of Supervisors from 1979 to 1984. After leaving politics in 1985 he began a career in mediation.

Early life and education

Gregorio was born in San Francisco in 19311 to a family who has lived in California for four generations.23 He grew up in the Bernal Heights district until his family moved to Burlingame, California, where he attended Hoover School, Burlingame High School and the College of San Mateo.4 Gregorio served as a U.S. Naval Air Officer for three years in the 1950s5 receiving the China Service Medal for combat duty.6 He received his bachelor's and law degrees from Stanford University in 1955.7 He was an adjunct faculty member at Stanford University, Notre Dame University in Belmont, and College of San Mateo.89

Career

Gregorio became a member of the California State Bar in 1955.10 He was a partner in general law practice from 1958 to 1970 and held the position of Assistant City Attorney for San Bruno from 1962 to 1970.11 He also chaired the San Mateo County Democratic Central Committee from 1964 to 1970.1213

Politics

In 1970, he was elected to represent San Mateo County in the California State Senate.14 According to fellow Senator Alan Robbins, Gregorio "refused special-interest money" and "contributions over $100".15 During his two terms in the Senate (1970 - 1978),16 Gregorio authored laws dealing with legislation and campaign finance reform, alcohol and drug abuse, the arts,17 environment, education and health.1819202122 He was chairman of the Senate Health and Welfare Committee23 from 1973 to 1978.24 Gregorio had a particular interest in reforming campaign financing and the political process in general.25 His political reforms included allowing the public to attend state budget conference committee meetings and to access legislator committee voting results.26

In 1975, Gregorio challenged then Governor Edmund G. Brown Jr. to a debate after the Governor vetoed his "alcohol tax bill."27 In 1976 he created legislation that would give members of the public the "voting majority on most regulatory boards."28 Gregorio lost his November 1978 Senate bid by a margin of only 90 votes after a vote recount.29 In 1979, he won a special election to the San Mateo County Board of Supervisors and was re-elected in 1980, serving through 1984.30 Gregorio's last political race was in 1984, when he left the Board of Supervisors to run again for the state Senate.3132 In that political contest, Gregorio and his opponent, Becky Morgan, set a "new Senate campaign spending record" of $1.6 million.33

Mediation

After leaving politics, Gregorio began an attorney mediation practice in San Francisco in 1985.34 He founded the non-profit, Peninsula Conflict Resolution Center in San Mateo, California in 1986.3536 In 1989, he created the first law partnership that focused exclusively on the mediation of civil trial matters.37 The firm is known as Gregorio, Haldeman & Rotman and is located in San Francisco.38

Personal life

Gregorio learned the Transcendental Meditation technique after reading a Wall Street Journal article about it in 1971.3940 He appeared with Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, Clint Eastwood and others on an episode of the Merv Griffin show in 1975.41

He has three sons by his first marriage and began his second marriage in 1980.4243
