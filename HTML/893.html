Robert White (May 28, 1876 <U+2013> August 15, 1935) was an American lawyer and politician in the U.S. state of West Virginia. White served four consecutive terms as the Prosecuting Attorney for Hampshire County (1912<U+2013>1928) and served one term in the West Virginia Senate (1930<U+2013>1934) representing the state's Fifteenth Senatorial District in the 40th and 41st Sessions of the West Virginia Legislature. During the 1933 legislative year, White served as the floor leader for the Democratic Party members of the West Virginia Senate.

White was a member of the White political family of Virginia and West Virginia and was the son of Hampshire County Clerk of Court and West Virginia Fish Commission President Christian Streit White (1839<U+2013>1917) and the grandson of Hampshire County Clerk of Court John Baker White (1794<U+2013>1862). White was a nephew of West Virginia Attorney General Robert White (1833<U+2013>1915).

Early life and education

Robert White was born on May 28, 1876 in Romney, West Virginia.123 He was the second child of Hampshire County Clerk of Court Christian Streit White (1839<U+2013>1917) with his second wife Catharine Steele White (1837<U+2013>1869)45 and the third eldest of his father's five children.6 White was a grandson of Hampshire County Clerk of Court John Baker White (1794<U+2013>1862)7 and a great-grandson of the prominent Virginia judge Robert White (1759<U+2013>1831).8 His uncle Robert White (1833-1915) served as Attorney General of West Virginia and his brother John Baker White (1868<U+2013>1944) was a military officer and later served as a Charleston city councilman and president of the West Virginia Board of Control.

White received his early education in the public schools of Romney, and attended Romney's Potomac Academy.9 He commenced his career in public service at the age of 1610 when he began serving as the Deputy Clerk of Court in the office of his father, Christian Streit White, who was then serving as Clerk of Court for Hampshire County.11 In 1894, White graduated from Potomac Academy at the age of 18.1213 Two years later, he began his studying jurisprudence in the West Virginia University College of Law in Morgantown.1415 He graduated from the West Virginia University College of Law in 1899,161718 earning a Bachelor of Laws degree.19 White cast his first vote for Democratic Party presidential nominee William Jennings Bryan in the United States presidential election of 1900.20

Law career

Following his graduation in 1899 from West Virginia University,21 White returned to Romney and established a law practice.2223 In 1903, he became associated with his father Christian Streit White in a law practice, following the conclusion of his father's term as Hampshire County Clerk of Court.24 White continued to practice law with his father until 1912, when he was elected to his first term as Prosecuting Attorney for Hampshire County.2526

Political career

Before the age of 21, White represented Hampshire County as a delegate to a West Virginia Democratic Party state convention.27

In 1912, won election to serve as the Prosecuting Attorney for Hampshire County.2829 He was elected to the position four times, and served four four-year terms from 1912 to 1928.303132 While serving as the county's prosecuting attorney, White was a strong proponent of the Good Roads Movement33 and under his leadership, the first concrete bridge was constructed in Hampshire County by the County Court.34

Between 1917 and 1921,353637 in addition to serving as the county's prosecuting attorney, White served as the Commissioner of School Lands383940 and as one of the Chancery Commissioners for Hampshire County, along with Joshua Soule Zimmerman and James Sloan Kuykendall.414243

In 1930, White was elected to serve in the West Virginia Senate representing the state's Fifteenth Senatorial District.444546 He served in the 40th and 41st Sessions of the West Virginia Legislature and completed his term in 1934.474849 White served as the floor leader for the West Virginia Senate's Democratic Party members during the 1933 legislative year.50

Affiliations

White served as a master of the Masonic Lodge,51 and was affiliated with the Odd Fellows.52
