H<U+00E9>l<U+00E8>ne Darroze (born 23 February 1967), is a French chef. She has held two Michelin stars at one time or another in each of her two restaurants, H<U+00E9>l<U+00E8>ne Darroze at The Connaught in London, and Restaurant H<U+00E9>l<U+00E8>ne Darroze in Paris.

Career

In 1990 she began working for Alain Ducasse in the office of the Louis XV restaurant in Monaco,1 after graduating from University. She was convinced to enter the kitchen by Ducasse;2 it was her only time working elsewhere than her family restaurant, and later said that she was working in the office about half the time.3 After working for Ducasse for three years, she returned to her family's restaurant and kept it's existing Michelin star. After the restaurant closed in 1999 after suffering from financial difficulties,4 she opened Restaurant H<U+00E9>l<U+00E8>ne Darroze in Paris in 1999, and won her first Michelin star in 2001, and picked up a second in 2003.5 The second star was lost in the 2010 version of the guide.6

In 2008, she was named as the new chef at The Connaught in London, replacing Angela Hartnett who had operated Angela Hartnett at The Connaught there on behalf of Gordon Ramsay Holdings. Darroze took over as part of a <U+00A3>70m refurbishment of the hotel, and in addition to the restaurant, she is responsible for all catering for guests at the restaurant including room service.7 H<U+00E9>l<U+00E8>ne Darroze at The Connaught, was panned by restaurant critics when it first opened.8 Food critic Jay Rayner described said of the food there, "Two of the very worst dishes ever to be served to me at this level".9 Despite this, the restaurant was awarded a Michelin star in 2009,10 and then a second star two years later in 2011.11

In 2012, she was admitted into the French Legion of Honour as a Chevalier (Knight) by President Nicolas Sarkozy.12

Personal life

Darroze is a fourth generation chef, the previous three having run a family restaurant in Villeneuve-de-Marsan, France. Before becoming a professional chef, she took a degree in business.13 She was also the inspiration for the character Colette in Pixar Animation Studios' 2007 film Ratatouille.14

She has two adopted daughters from Vietnam,15 Charlotte and Quiterie.16 She alternatives on a weekly basis between staying in London and in Paris, in order to split her time between her two restaurants.17
