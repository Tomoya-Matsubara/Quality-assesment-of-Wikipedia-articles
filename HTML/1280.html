Micro<U+00EF>ds (also known as MC2-Micro<U+00EF>ds) is a French software company who publishes and develops video games. Since 2003 they have been part of MC2 France, and the Micro<U+00EF>ds brand was relaunched in late 2007. Since the beginning of 2010 Micro<U+00EF>ds has been a part of Anuman Interactive.

History

Micro<U+00EF>ds was founded in 1985 by Elliot Grassiano. For the first 10 years, Micro<U+00EF>ds was a development house, but in 1995 they expanded into publishing and distribution as well.1 Micro<U+00EF>ds France at this time was located in V<U+00E9>lizy (near Paris), France. The company also then expanded worldwide with a publisher in Milan, Italy (Micro<U+00EF>ds Italy), distributor in Milton Keynes, United Kingdom (Micro<U+00EF>ds Ltd) and a developer in Montr<U+00E9>al, Canada (Micro<U+00EF>ds Canada Inc.).

MC2 France

In 2003, Emmanuel Olivier (founder of Index+) created a new company called MC2 France which subsequently merged with Micro<U+00EF>ds. In September 2003 MC2-Micro<U+00EF>ds acquired Wanadoo Edition as part of a deal with the French government for Micro<U+00EF>ds to emerge from bankruptcy protection. As part of the deal, Wanadoo also became a 12% shareholder of Micro<U+00EF>ds.2 Emmanuel Olivier became CEO when Elliot Grassiano left the company.

In March 2005 Ubisoft announced their intention to acquire MC2-Micro<U+00EF>ds (Micro<U+00EF>ds Canada) and to integrate it into Ubisoft's Montreal studios.3 This deal only included fifty of MC2-Micro<U+00EF>ds' employees and technical equipment; however, the agreement did not include any transfer of intellectual property.4 Micro<U+00EF>ds Italy split off from the company in 2005, turning into the Italian publisher Blue Label Entertainment.5

On November 28, 2007 MC2 France officially relaunched the Micro<U+00EF>ds brand6, with all of the company's future productions to be released either under the MICRO<U+00CF>DS Montreal or MICRO<U+00CF>DS Paris brands. The headquarters of the company are in Montrouge a suburb of Paris and they have a development studio in Montreal, Canada, hence the two brands. In December 2007 they opened an online store, powered by Metaboli.

On 20 October 2008 Micro<U+00EF>ds acquired the brands and intellectual property of Cryo Interactive, who had been declared bankrupt in 2002.7 On 5 November the Micro<U+00EF>ds online store was improved and relaunched.8

Anuman Interactive

On 23 November 2009 Anuman Interactive announced that they were acquiring the mark of and all the associated licences of Micro<U+00EF>ds.9 The Micro<U+00EF>ds deal between Anuman and MC2 was completed on 1 January 2010.1011

Games

MC2-Micro<U+00EF>ds is well known for the development of the adventure games including Syberia, Syberia II, Post Mortem and its sequel Still Life. Other notable games that Micro<U+00EF>ds has produced include Far Gate and the Nicky Boum series of action games for the Commodore Amiga.

In 2007 Micro<U+00EF>ds announced that it was working on a number of games in collaboration with various development studios such as Elektrogames, Kheops Studio, Mzone Studio, TOTM Studio, and White Birds Productions. Still Life 2 was also announced to be developed by GameCo Studios. New games are also being developed for Apple Macintosh computers.12 In 2008 Micro<U+00EF>ds announced they had entered into a publishing agreement with Encore (part of Navarre Corporation), where Encore would publish Micro<U+00EF>ds entire catalogue in North America.13 This deal was extended in August where Legacy Interactive would distribute Micro<U+00EF>ds adventure video games as digital downloads in North America.14 A deal with cdv Software Entertainment to distribute games such as Still Life 2 in France was announced in April 2009.15

In September 2009 the company announced a long-term distribution deal with Iceberg Interactive for the United Kingdom and Benelux.16

Corporate identity

Since the creation of Micro<U+00EF>ds in 1984 the company has had a number of different logos, utilising two main designs. There is the original curvy stylised letter M logo, which may look like a simple drawing of a bird; initially this had a background depicting a 3D scene.17 Fellow French video game developer with links to Micro<U+00EF>ds, White Birds, also use similar simple drawings of birds as their logo. In the mid-1990s the company changed their logo slightly to contain the colours of the rainbow, but without a background. This logo remained until the merger with MC2 France, when a red/grey logo was used. By the time of Still Life being released the company was using a logo containing a blocky stylised letter M and dot below it.18 In 2005 this had a smooth typeface and no dots above the letter i. By the relaunch of the brand in 2007 the stylised M logo had dots placed above the letter i and a different typeface.19
