George Washington Memorial Park is located at the center of Jackson, Wyoming. More generally known as "Town Square", the park is notable for its elk-antler arches at each corner of the park, collected from the nearby National Elk Refuge by boy scouts and periodically rebuilt. The park existed as an open space in the center of town that was made into a park in 1934. The park center also contains a stone memorial to John Colter.1
