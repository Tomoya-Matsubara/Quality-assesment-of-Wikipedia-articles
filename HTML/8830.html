Abdurahim Laajab (born 21 May 1985), commonly known as Ibba, is a Norwegian football and futsal player of Moroccan descent who currently plays for V<U+00E5>lerenga as a winger.

Ibba played eight season at second tier in Norway with Skeid, Notodden, Mj<U+00F8>ndalen and Str<U+00F8>mmen before he signed with the Tippeligaen-side V<U+00E5>lerenga in 2012. Ibba is one of the key players in the Norwegian futsal team.

Football career

Ibba was born to Moroccan parents and moved to Norway when he was one month old.1 He played at V<U+00E5>lerenga and Moss during his youth,23 and later joined Skeid because his friends were playing there.4 At Skeid he was teammate with Moa, Mos, Birger Madsen and Dawda Leigh.5

Ibba didn't get too much playing time with Skeid, but during the second half of the 2004-season he played 248 minutes in nine matches in the First Division and scored two goals. Ahead of the 2005-season, Ibba and his teammate Moa joined Rosenborg BK on their pre-season tour to La Manga.6 Ibba was sent on a short loan-spell to Dr<U+00F8>bak-Frogn in 2005,7 and left Skeid after the season and signed a one-year contract with S<U+00F8>rumsand which had been relegated from the Third Division.8

Before he played a match for S<U+00F8>rumsand, Ibba moved to Borussia M<U+00F6>nchengladbach9 where J<U+00F8>rn Andersen was the assistant coach. Ibba and Andersen communicated with eachother in Norwegian. A year later Andersen was fired together with the head coach, and the new coach only talked to Ibba in German, a language Ibba did not try to learn, and eventually Ibba requested to terminate his contract and moved home to Norway.10 He only played one match for Borussia M<U+00F6>nchengladbach II.11

In 2007 Ibba was back in Skeid, before he signed a contract with Notodden ahead of the 2008-season.12 Ibba played regularly in the first-team until head coach Jan Halvor Halvorsen was fired halfway through the 2009-season. The new coach, Arne Sandst<U+00F8> placed him on the bench and Ibba left the club after the season. Ahead of the 2010-season, Ibba was on a trial with the Spanish second-tier club Elche and was offered a contract there, but Ibba rejected the offer and decided to move to Mj<U+00F8>ndalen instead.13 Mj<U+00F8>ndalen's coach Vegard Hansen stated that Ibba was a risky signing due to his poor reputation, but that the 24-year had a such an unredeemed potential that he couldn't ignore him.14

Ibba played for Str<U+00F8>mmen in 2011, where he scored seven goals in 24 matches in Adeccoligaen,15 and ahead of the 2012-season he signed for V<U+00E5>lerenga.1617 After Ibba's transfer to V<U+00E5>lerenga, his former team mate from Skeid and former V<U+00E5>lerenga-player, Moa, said in an interview with Norwegian TV2 that the supporters of V<U+00E5>lerenga should be excited about their new signing and dubbed Ibba as one of the best newcomers of the 2012 Tippeligaen.18 Ibba made his first-team debut for V<U+00E5>lerenga in the friendly match against the Second Division-side Nesodden on 11 January 2012.19

Futsal career

Ibba is known as one of the biggest futsal-star in Norway.202122 In his debut for the Norwegian futsal team, he scored three goals when Norway won 7-2 against Malta on 13 January 2011.23 The next day he again scored three goals against Malta, before he scored another hat-trick when Norway won against Israel in the qualifying match for the 2012 UEFA Futsal Championship on 20 January, which made a total of nine goals in his first three matches for Norway.24

Ahead of the qualifying match against Spain on 15 December 2011, Norway's national team coach Espen O. S<U+00E6>ther called Ibba a "top international futsal player", and that if their chances against Spain was depending on Ibba's performance and that he had to play at his very best.25 Three days later, Ibba scored the goal that secured a 3-3 draw against Belgium and sent Norway to the playoff round of the 2012 FIFA Futsal World Cup qualifier.26

Style of play

Laajab has a good physique, a good temperament and good one-on-one skills and has a great breakthrough force.27
