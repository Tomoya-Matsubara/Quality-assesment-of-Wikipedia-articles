Lewis Archer is a fictional character from the British soap opera Coronation Street. The character was created and introduced as a love interest for Audrey Roberts (Sue Nicholls). Executive producer Kim Crowther revealed the team wanted an actor who was slightly younger than Audrey and who was very charming. Various actors were considered for the part, but the casting director suggested Havers as she knew he was a big fan of the show. The actor said could not turn down a chance to appear in Coronation Street as his character had an interesting story arc. Havers was was contracted until July 2010 and he made his debut as Lewis in the episode broadcast on 18 December 2009.

Lewis is a gentleman escort, who is described as being suave, charming and charismatic. He is first seen accompanying Claudia Colby (Rula Lenska) to a Christmas ball. He is introduced to Audrey, who falls for his charm and she later hires him to be her companion to another event. In March 2010, Lewis was seen advertising his services in The Lady, as part of a crossover between the society magazine and Coronation Street. Rita Sullivan (Barbara Knox) sees the advert and books Lewis for a date, forming a love triangle storyline between them and Audrey. Lewis eventually declares his feelings for Audrey and they plan to leave Weatherfield together.

As their plans to emigrate step up a gear, Lewis forges a plan to steal money from Peter Barlow's (Chris Gascoyne) bookies by cashing in fraudulently franked betting slips. He sucessfully pulls off his scam and cheats on Audrey when he kisses Deirdre Barlow (Anne Kirkbride). Lewis flees the country and he made his last appearance on 13 August 2010. Just over a year later, it was announced Havers would reprise his role as Lewis and return to Coronation Street in February 2012. The actor believed Lewis would be a changed man and said he just wants to apologise to those he hurt. The character and Havers' first turn in the show was well received by television critics.

Creation and casting

On 14 October 2009, Daniel Kilkelly of Digital Spy announced Havers had joined the cast of Coronation Street as Lewis, a potential love interest for Audrey Roberts (Sue Nicholls).1 A writer for What's on TV said Havers was to make a one-off appearance in December, before continuing in the role throughout 2010.2 The shows executive producer, Kim Crowther told Gareth McLean of the Radio Times that while the team was planning the Audrey and Lewis storyline, they decided they wanted to introduce someone slightly younger than Audrey, who was very charming.3 Casting director June West considered various actors for the part, including Anthony Head, but she suggested Havers for the role as she knew he was a big fan of the show.4 Crowther said "When you think of Nigel, you think of someone suave enough to charm you out of your high heels. Half the work is done."5 Havers revealed he could not say no to Coronation Street as his character was fascinating and he had an interesting story arc. The actor said being on the show had always been an ambition of his.6 Havers was was contracted until July 2010 and he made his debut as Lewis in the episode broadcast on 18 December 2009.7 In March 2010, Havers said that he was keen to renew his contract, as he was enjoying his time with the soap.8

Development

Characterisation and Audrey Roberts

Gareth McLean of the Radio Times revealed Lewis was a gentleman escort and he deemed him a "suave companion."9 A show spokeswoman said that there is no suggestion that Lewis is anything more than a companion for his clients.10 A writer for the Metro described Lewis as "charming" and "charismatic", while a reporter from the Daily Record said he was "Weatherfield's answer to American Gigolo."1112 Havers believed Lewis would display "a real dark side", though he was unaware of what that was.13 Lewis is first seen accompanying Claudia Colby (Rula Lenska) to a Christmas ball.14 He is introduced to Audrey Roberts and Daniel Kilkelly of Digital Spy said she was "immediately bowled over by his charming ways."15 Claudia informs Audrey that she booked Lewis through an agency and Audrey decides to hire Lewis to be her companion to another event.16 Lewis goes to the Rovers Return Inn and pretends not to know Audrey.17 He then proceeds to chat her up in front of her friends, causing them to wonder how she attracted him.18 Havers said the scenes were funny and called the storyline between Lewis and Audrey fantastic.19 In March 2010, Lewis was seen advertising his escort service in society magazine, The Lady.20 A reporter for the The Belfast Telegraph said the character's classified advert would then be seen in the real life magazine in a crossover with the show.21 A spokeswoman for Coronation Street said "The Lady heard the magazine was going to feature in an episode, and they approached us with the idea of using the advert in the actual magazine."22 In the advert, Lewis claimed to have "years of experience" and was "suitable to accompany ladies in any social or corporate event", two fictional phone numbers were also included.23 In the show, Audrey's friend Rita Sullivan (Barbara Knox) notices the advert and books Lewis for a date.24 A love triangle storyline between Audrey, Rita and Lewis then formed and played out over Easter 2010.25 Rita hires Lewis to accompany her to a ball, despite knowing that Audrey has fallen for him.26 Audrey becomes jealous when she tries to book Lewis herself and he explains someone has already hired him.27 Audrey attends the ball with a friend and is shocked to see Rita with Lewis. The two woman insult each other and Lewis and Rita leave.28 Susan Hill, writing for the Daily Star said despite Lewis' apology for attending an event with Rita, Audrey is furious and confronts her friend.29 Rita tells Audrey she is "acting like an old fool as Lewis can never be hers" and Audrey slaps Rita across the face.30 Of Lewis' take on the situation, Havers commented "I think Lewis is genuinely concerned about Audrey. When Rita gets involved, he has to box clever, but he's very good at that. Things move on, but I don't want to give anything away. Put it like this - all is not what it seems."31 Audrey decides to stop all contact with Lewis after realising she has fallen for him. 32 Audrey's decision is prompted by her falling out with Rita and the fact she has read Lewis' diary, which contains details on his clients.33 Not wanting to get hurt by him, Audrey tells Lewis that she wants end their arrangment. Lewis is shocked and cannot understand why Audrey would want to stop seeing him.34 A show spokesperson said "In the end she has to come clean and tell him how she really feels <U+2013> which prompts Lewis to kiss her. He then tells her he<U+2019>s developed feelings for her too."35 Audrey is shocked by the kiss and Lewis' declaration of love.36

As their relationship progresses, Audrey invites Lewis to move in with her. Nicholls told Suzanne Byrne of RT<U+00C9> Ten that Lewis appears to brush Audrey off, before eventually agreeing to move in.37 Nicholls said "And then in his usual charming style he brings her breakfast in bed on her birthday, which she adores him for. He's clever like that, he knows how to bring the balance back to the relationship."38 In May 2010, Brian Roberts of the Daily Mirror reported Audrey and Lewis would leave Weatherfield together for a new life in Greece.39 The couple visit a Greek island on holiday and they plan to purchase a hotel out there.4041 Nicholls said Audrey is taken with Lewis' "romantic" idea of opening a hotel and living a glamorous life.42 Roberts likened the plot development to the 1989 film, Shirley Valentine.43 Roz Laws of the Sunday Mercury noted that Lewis seemed uncomfortable with the plan, while Nicholls said Audrey is unaware that he is having doubts.4445 Laws also compared the plot to a similar one involving Diane Sugden (Elizabeth Estensen) and Charlie Haynes (George Costigan) in Emmerdale, predicting both storylines would end in heartache.46 Nicholls stated that she wanted Audrey and Lewis to leave together and have a happy ending. She also revealed that she would have liked to have seen a spin-off focusing on the two characters and their new life in Greece.47

Departure

Olivia Buxton of the Daily Mirror reported two endings to Lewis' story arc had been filmed, one of which saw him making a dramatic exit from the show.48 As their plans to emigrate to Greece step up a gear, Lewis forges a plan to steal money from Peter Barlow's (Chris Gascoyne) bookies by cashing in fraudulently franked betting slips.49 Lewis starts flirting with Deirdre Barlow (Anne Kirkbride), who works in the shop, and successfully tries out his scam with one stolen betting slip first.50 While Audrey arranges to have a large sum of money transferred into an account for them to purchase their Greek hotel, Lewis goes to the bookies and convinces Deirdre to open a bottle of wine.51 When she is out of the room, Lewis takes his fake betting slips and places them in the till.52 Deirdre returns and notices the shop door is locked and Lewis kisses her to distract her. Kate Woodward of Inside Soap said Deirdre likes Lewis, but she ends their kiss and tells him to return to Audrey.53 While Audrey is holding a farewell party in the Rovers, Lewis slips out and collects his money from the bookies. He chooses not to take Audrey's money as well and breaks up with her, before deciding to flee the country. Havers filmed his final scenes on 21 June 2010 at a Manchester hotel, which doubled for an airport bar.54 While he is at the airport, Lewis meets businesswoman, Lydia Radcliffe (Lysette Anthony).55 Chris Johnson of the Daily Mail said Lydia is a welcome distraction for Lewis who is feeling guilty about robbing Peter and leaving Audrey.56 Lewis begins flirting with Lydia and "rolls out some of his well-known chat-up lines", which she falls for.57 Lydia mentions she is heading to Barbados and Lewis reveals he is going there too. They then board the plane together.58 Lewis departed on 13 August 2010.59

Return

While appearing on Loose Women in February 2011, Havers revealed that he had been in talks with Coronation Street producers about a return to the show.60 Havers said he adored his role as Lewis and that he had to return to apologise and Audrey.61 Later that year, ITV announced Havers would be reprising his role as Lewis and returning to Coronation Street in 2012.62 Series producer Phil Collinson revealed Havers had started filming his scenes in January 2012 and he would begin appearing as Lewis from February.63 Collinson said Lewis would have a lot of ground to cover to win Audrey back.64 He added the character is "a great hoot" and that it was wonderful to have Havers back.65 Peter Dyke and Katie Begley of the Daily Star revealed Lewis' return storyline would see Audrey and her daughter, Gail Platt (Helen Worth), run into Lewis at a country pub.66 Havers told Allison Jones of Inside Soap that Lewis then tries to convince them he is his own twin brother, Hugh.67 Havers explained "Audrey is totally floored to see him again - as you can imagine. And if I said that Gail is angry when they bump into Lewis, it would be a massive understatement!"68 Lewis begs Audrey and Gail not to call the police and he decides to return to Weatherfield.69

Havers deemed Lewis a changed man and said he wants to apologise and make amends with those that he hurt.70 Lewis returns the money he took to Peter and he adds a thousand pounds to the amount as compensation. Havers explained that Lewis feels "desperately bad" about what he did.71 Upon entering the Rovers, Lewis is punched by Deirdre.72 Havers opined that Lewis still "has an eye" for Audrey and he finds her charming and honest. The actor said he believed Lewis did fall for her while they were together and that it why he did not take her money.73 Havers added "There's always that danger Lewis could fall back into his old ways, and he is down to his last penny. But at the moment, Lewis is trying his hardest and has no ulterior motive - just an awful lot of ice to break instead!"74 Havers will make his return to Coronation Street as Lewis on 17 February 2012.75

Storylines

Lewis is hired by Claudia Colby to accompany her to the Weatherfield Traders' Association Christmas party. Claudia introduces Lewis to her friend, Audrey Roberts, and he flirts with her. Audrey learns Lewis is a male escort and she later hires him to be her date for the Stylists' Dinner. Audrey contacts Lewis again and invites him to have dinner with her, but she gets cold feet and pretends to be ill when he arrives. Lewis persuades Audrey to let him in and he listens while she talks about her late husband. They then go out for dinner. Lewis visits the Rovers Return on Coronation Street and pretends not to know Audrey. Norris Cole (Malcolm Hebden) recognises Lewis from the Christmas party and when he sees Lewis kissing Audrey, he jumps to the conclusion that they are having an affair.

Rita Sullivan contacts Lewis and they attend the theatre together. Lewis charms Rita and she books him for the Chamber of Commerce Easter Dinner Dance. Lewis is surprised when Audrey turns up at the dance with Claudia. Audrey becomes jealous of Rita and the women insult each other, until Lewis steps in. Lewis leaves his diary containing his client's details in Audrey's salon. When he goes to collect it, he finds Audrey has read it. Audrey tells Lewis that they cannot continue to be together, but Lewis kisses Audrey and tells her he has feelings for her. They begin dating, despite Rita telling Audrey it will end in tears. After noticing Audrey is not happy with his occupation, Lewis quits telling Audrey that he is beginning to feel too old for the job. Audrey invites Lewis to move in with her and although he hesitates at first, Lewis eventually accepts.

Lewis and Audrey go on holiday to Greece together and Lewis suggests they move out there and open a hotel. Audrey agrees and gets a <U+00A3>100,000 loan towards the purchase of the hotel. Needing money of his own, Lewis decides to steal from Peter Barlow's bookies with fraudulently franked betting slips. He begins flirting with Deirdre Barlow, an employee at the bookies. Lewis sends Deirdre out of the shop to get a corkscrew, so they can share a bottle of wine. He places his fraudulent betting slips in the till and when Deirdre returns, he kisses her to distract her. Lewis slips out of Audrey's farewell party to collect his money and he leaves for the airport. Peter discovers Lewis' has stolen from him and Audrey is devastated his disappearance. She discovers Lewis left her money alone and she finds a photograph of them, with the words 'I tried' written on the back. While he is in the airport bar waiting for his plane to Barbados, Lewis introduces himself to Lydia Radcliffe and offers to sit with her during the flight.

Reception

Jaci Stephen of the Daily Mail called Havers' casting "inspired", saying he had become "synonymous with upper-class cads" following his role as Ralph Ernest Gorse in The Charmer.76 Of the character, Stephen said "It's not often that someone like Lewis strays onto the cobbles. Suave and charming, he's in a different class to Jack Sugden and co. Although at least none of Weatherfield's men folk have charged ladies for their company, as, despite his plummy veneer, Lewis is just a gigolo."77 A writer for the Liverpool Daily Post said "Since Lewis (Nigel Havers) waltzed into the street, things with the over fifty club (Rita, Audrey and now Deirdre) haven't been the same."78 Steve Pratt, writing for The Northern Echo, said "As love rat scumbags go, Lewis Archer is up there with that chap who tried to push Rita under a tram in Blackpool.79

Sue Crawford of the Daily Mirror said "playing a ladies' man comes naturally to Nigel Havers so it's hardly surprising he's playing a flirt in Coronation Street."80 Crawford's colleague, Simon Boyle, branded Lewis "charming but devious."81 Havers' turn in the soap proved popular with female viewers. The actor told entertainment website Digital Spy that he had been sent sackfuls of fan mail and underwear.82 However, other viewers were unhappy with his character's treatment of Audrey and the actor received abuse in the street.83 In November 2011, Jon Horsley of Yahoo! said viewers should be excited about Lewis' return to Coronation Street.84 Horsely added "It'll be great to see the old smoothy back on the cobbles. We're really looking forward to Audrey's reaction to him, too."85
