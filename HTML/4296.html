John Elmer "Jack" Stivetts (March 31, 1868 <U+2013> April 18, 1930), was a professional baseball pitcher who played 11 seasons in Major League Baseball (MLB) spanning from 1889 to 1899. He played in the American Association (AA) with the St. Louis Browns, and in the National League (NL) with the Boston Beaneaters and Cleveland Spiders. "Happy Jack" (nicknamed due to his pleasant demeanor} was born to German immigrants and raised in Ashland, Pennsylvania.12 He initially followed his father into the coal mining industry before playing professional baseball. After playing two and half seasons in minor league baseball (MiLB), he was signed to the Browns. Over the next few seasons, he was regarded as one of the best pitchers in baseball.

In his first season, he led the AA in earned run average (ERA), and in strikeouts two seasons later. In the years spanning from 1890 through 1896, he posted 20 or more victories in a season six times; two of which he won more than 30. In 1892, he won a career-high 35 games, and on August 6, he threw a no-hitter. During his seven seasons with the Beaneaters, he was part of four league championships, and pitched along-side future Hall of Fame members John Clarkson and Kid Nichols. He was among the best hitting pitchers of his era, and was often used at other positions when he was not pitching. He became the first pticher to hit two home runs in one game, and his season total of seven in 1890 stood as the record for pitchers until 1931. He compiled a .298 lifetime batting average, hit 35 home runs, and delivered 357 runs batted in (RBIs).

His last season in the major leagues, he played for the Spiders, widely considered to be one of the worst teams in MLB history. After his baseball career, he returned to his hometown of Ashland. He worked for the area coal mines as a brewery wagon driver and carpenter, and died there at 62 years of age.

Early life

John Elmer Stivetts was born on March 31, 1868 in Ashland, Pennsylvania to German immigrants Adam, a coal worker, and Amelia (n<U+00E9>e Cooper).34 He was raised in a family of Lutherans, receiveing his baptism one month after his birth,5 and his confirmation on April 2, 1882 at the age of 14 from Christ's United Lutheran Church in Ashland.6 He attended public schools for eight years, before following his father in coal mining trade.78 He married Margaret Ann "Maggie" Thomas in June 1896, and together they had one son, John, and five daughters, Ida, Mary, Leona, Margaret, and Gladys.91011

When Stivetts was 19 years of age, he began his professional baseball career in June 1897 for the Ashland representative in the Central Pennsylvania League.12 He compiled a 9<U+2013>5 win<U+2013>loss record (W<U+2013>L) that season with a 1.35 earned run average (ERA). In addition to Ashland in 1888, he played for the Allentown Peanuts of the Central League.13 He began the 1889 season with the York representative of the Middle States League.14 An umpire, Tim Hurst, noticed Stivetts' skills, and recomended him to Charles Comiskey, the manager of the St. Louis Browns of the American Association (AA).15 Comiskey was impressed the pitcher's speed, and made him an offer. The Philadelphia Athletics soon made an offer of their own, but St. Louis was able to sign him for $275 a month salary with a $200 signing bonus.16

Career

St. Louis

1889 season

When Stivetts joined the team, he became their third starting pitcher in the rotation behind Silver King and Ice Box Chamberlain.17 He made his major league debut for the Browns on June 26, 1889 against the Cincinnati Reds.18 He pitched complete game and struck out nine batters, but lost the game by the score of 6<U+2013>1, behind several errors made by his team. His performance was noted in the press to have "made a good impression."19

In 26 games pitched during the season, his W<U+2013>L record was 12<U+2013>7 as St. Louis finished two games behind the Brooklyn Bridegrooms for the AA championship.2021 He started 20, completed 18, struck out 143, and had a league-leading 2.25 ERA in 191<U+2154> innings pitched.22 It was reported on November 13, 1889 that he had re-signed with the Browns for the 1890 season.23

1890 season

During the off-season, the up-start Union Association (UA) began its operations, and the ensuing departure of players from both AA and the NL teams, the Browns roster was depleted of many their best players.24 Among these included Comiskey, their player-manager, and King the team's top pitcher.25 Chamberlain re-signed with the team, but he was sold to the Columbus Solons after pitching in just five games.2627 These movements left Stivetts and Toad Ramsey as the franchise's top two starters, with rookie Billy Hart cast as the occasional relief starter.28

The Browns opened the regular season on April 18 with a four-game series on the road versus the Louisville Colonels, and Stivetts started games two and four.29 In game two, he recorded 13 strikeouts, but lost the game 5<U+2013>3 on a tenth-inning, two run double by Tim Shinnick.30 In game four on April 21, Stivetts was again credited with the loss by allowing 17 runs to score, seven of which were earned.31 He fared better in his third start however, allowing just one run as the Browns defeated the Solons by a 14<U+2013>1  score. He struck out 12 batters, including the first seven he faced, and hit the first home run of his career.3233

On June 10, against Fred Smith and the Toledo Maumees, he became the first pitcher on record to hit two home runs in one game.34 The first was a two-run home run in the fifth inning, and the second came with his team down by three runs in the bottom half of ninth inning and the bases loaded.35 It was the first, and only, grand slam of his career, and the second "ultimate grand slam" in history.363738{{#tag:ref|Although the box score of the game shows the Browns's scoring line first, it was the custom for the home team to bat second.39 Eventually, this practice became codified as an official rule on December 21, 1949 during the Professional Baseball Official Playing Rules Committee meeting.40|group=notes}} In a game versus the Brooklyn Gladiators on July 6, manager Chris Von Der Ahe removed the Browns' starting pitcher Ramsey in the third inning and replaced him with Stivetts. Though the crowd momentarily interrupted the game in protest, the move proved successful. Stivetts hit a home run in the fifth inning to give the Browns a 3 runs to 1 advantage, leading the team to an eventual 7<U+2013>2 victory.41 The home run was his sixth of the season, and he added another on August 9 for number seven: his final season total. His seven home runs in a season by a pitcher was neither broken nor tied until 1931, when Wes Ferrell hit nine for the Cleveland Indians.42 St. Louis finished in third-place among the nine AA teams, 12 games behind the Colonels.43 Stivetts had a W<U+2013>L record of 27<U+2013>21, a 3.52 ERA, and 289 strikeouts in 419<U+2153> innings pitched, along with three shutouts.

1891 season

On January 25, 1891, it was reported that he had re-signed with the Browns for the up-coming season.44 The season opened April 8, and Browns faced Kelly's Killers at home.45 Stivetts was the team's opening day starting pitcher. The game ended in the bottom of ninth inning when the umpire, former player and St. Louis native Bill Gleason, determined that Cincinnati was intentionally allowing St. Louis to score in an effort to delay the game. St. Louis was awarded the forfeited when Cincinnati refused to play.4647 AA president, Louis Kramer, declared the forfeit illegal a short time later, and the game was re-played on April 12, with Stivetts the winning pitcher.48 This was Gleason's second and last career game as an umpire.49 Over the course of the season, Stivetts was the Browns' ace, pitching mostly every second or third game the team played.50 When not pitching, or when he occasionally played in right field, the Browns used Joe Neale, Willie McGill, and future Hall of Fame executive Clark Griffith as part of the starting rotation.5152 On May 30, Stivetts started the both games of a doubleheader in a home series versus the Philadelphia Athletics. Due to large leads, he was pulled from each game; lasting two innings in the first and six in the second.53

Stivetts won each of his next three starts, hitting a home run in the third game, on June 6, against the Boston Reds.5455 He hit two more in a game on August 6, against Gus Weyhing of the Athletics, the second two-homer game of his career.56 He connected for another off of Weyhing in a game on September 16, during a four-run ninth inning that gave the Browns a 10<U+2013>7 victory.5758 This was his seventh home run of the season, fourth as a pitcher, adding to three that he hit while playing right field.59 Again, he was among the best pitchers in the AA, and among the league leaders in many pitching categories. His 259 strikeouts led the league, while his 33 victories were third, and his 2.86 ERA ranked fifth.60 St. Louis completed the season second in the AA among the nine teams, 8.5 games behind the champion Reds.61

Boston

1892 season

Though Stivetts initally denied it, it was reported on September 28, 1891 that he signed with the Boston Beaneaters of the National League (NL) for the 1892 season.62 Stivetts joined a pitching rotation that already included future Baseball Hall of Fame members John Clarkson and Kid Nichols, as well as veteran Harry Staley.63 Along side Boston's solid pitching, their position players were a potent mix of future hall of fame members Hugh Duffy, Tommy McCarthy, and King Kelly, and established veterans Herman Long, Bobby Lowe, and Tommy Tucker.64 With this roster, Boston was expected to compete for a second consecutive NL championship.65 When analyzing the 12 National League teams, New Yor Giants captain, Buck Ewing, remarked about Boston, "I think Clarkson has seen his better days. Jack Stivetts will strengthen them wonderfully. He is one of the best pitchers in the country to-day."66 In Stivetts' first start of the season on April 23, his pitching was described as "wild", as he issued six bases on balls. He prevailed, however, and Boston won by the score of 11<U+2013>7.6768 Four days later, he was victorious again, and Boston's W<U+2013>L record stood at 10<U+2013>1 after their first 11 games.69 On June 23, he hit his first home run of the season, along with a double, in a 13<U+2013>6 victory over the Baltimore Orioles.70 The Pittsburg Dispatch reported on July 1 that Stivetts was leading Boston hitters in batting at that point in the season.71{{#tag:ref|Stivetts' .298 batting average finished second among Boston's hitters behind Hugh Duffy's .302.72|group=notes}}

With Stivetts now established, Clarkson became expendible and was released from the team on June 30, leaving Boston with a solid three-man pitching rotation for the remainder of the season.737475 On August 6, 1892, Stivetts pitched a no-hitter against the Brooklyn Bridegrooms, an 11<U+2013>0 victory.76 Later, on September 5, he started, completed, and defeated Louisville in both games of a doubleheader.77 The following day, the press credited the victories to his ability to work well with runners on the bases.78 The regular season for the Beaneaters closed on October 15, with a doubleheader against the Washington Senators. Stivetts pitched the second game, and delivered a complete game shutout, his third shutout and his 35th victory of the season.79 Although he finished within the top-ten in several pitching statistical categories, his strikeout total dropped to 180 in 1892. His 3.9 Strikeouts per 9&nbsp;innings pitched (K/9) was significantly lower from the 5.3 the previous season.80

The AA disolved, following the 1891 season, with severl teams merging with the NL, which re-established itself as the lone "major league". The league devised a post-season series to determine a champion between the two half-season victors.81 This series pitted the Beaneaters (the first-half champions) against the Cleveland Spiders (the second-half champions). The first game occured on October 17; Stivetts started for Boston, and future Hall of Famer Cy Young pitched for Cleveland. The pitching duel lasted eleven scorless innings before the game was brought to end by darkness.82 After Boston won game two behind Staley on October 18, Stivetts started and gained the victory in the game on October 19.8384 The Beaneaters were declared the NL champion after winning five straight after the opening tie game; Stivetts gained victories in three.85 This playoff scheme was abandoned after the season.86

1893 season

On January 21, 1893, it was reported that Stivetts re-signed with Boston for the up-coming season.87 In March, Stivetts' name was tied to an "immiment" trade between Boston and Brooklyn that involved pitcher Tom Lovett transferring to Boston. Ultimately, the deal did not transpire, and each pitcher reported to their respective teams.88 One rule change implemented for the 1893 season changed the distance between the pitching mound and home plate from 50 feet to 60 feet 6 inches.89 The change intended to even the competetive balance between the pitchers and hitters. In addition to the distance, pitchers were to pitch from a rubber slab, and not allowed to take a running start before they delivered the ball to the batter.90

Stivetts struggled with the new distance initially; his first four starts of the season were largely ineffective.91 On April 29, with the weather cold and rainy, he issued bases on balls to ten New York Giants batters, and surrendered 15 runs.92 The next two starts were against Baltimore and he allowed seven runs to score the first game depite gaining the victory, and another 16 runs in a loss in the second game.93 Another ten runs scored on May 10, in a loss versus the Washington Senators.94

His troubles adjusting to the new rules, and his athletic conditioning during June and July, prompted the team to sign Hank Gastright for added pitching strength.95 He struck out just 61 batter in 283 2/3 for a 1.6 K/9, and his ERA increased from 3.03 in 1892 to 4.41. Despite this, he helped Boston claim their third consecutive NL championship with a 20<U+2013>12 W<U+2013>L record.9697

1894 season

With the previous season's inconsistencies behind him, he was re-signed and was the team's starting pitcher for Boston season-opening game on April 19; a 13<U+2013>2 victory versus Bridegrooms of Brooklyn.9899 He then lost his next eight starts, however, before beginning a ten-game winning streak that lasted into mid-July.100 On August 21, he started and gained complete game victories in both ends of a doubleheader versus the Cincinnati Reds.101 Two days later, on August 23, he pitched Boston to 12<U+2013>10 victory against the Spiders. That same day in Ashland, his father was killed in a coal mining accident when a load of coal fell on top on him.102103

Boston finished in third place among the 12 NL teams in 1894, and Stivetts' won 26 games, though most of his numbers in important statistical categories failed to improve or became worse.104 His ERA rose to 4.90, and his K/9 moved up slightly to 2.0 when he struck out 76 in 338 innings pitched.105 He issued 127 bases on balls, and surrendered a league-leading 27 home runs.106 It was his hitting skills that was his biggest success in 1894. Beginning on July 5, Boston played a three-game series against Cleveland. While alternating game to game from pitching and playing in right field, Stivetts hit one home run in each game, the last one coming in the sixth inning off of Cy Young, completing the sweep.107 He hit a career-high season total of eight home runs for the season. He also achieved career-highs with 64 RBIs, seven triples, and 55 runs scored, while batting .328 in 68 games played.108

1895 season

In early November, due to the threat that a new American Association was near formation, the National League clubs were quick to sign their players without much negotiating. Many of the players' salary demands were met, including Stivetts'.109 In March, shortly before the 1895 season began, Stivetts claimed he was in "excellent phycial condition", and that he was "many pounds lighter than he was at this period last year."110 The season opener was on April 19 against Brooklyn, and Stivetts got the start over Nichols for the second consecutive season.111 Despite being down early in the game, Boston won the game 11<U+2013>6 behind a seven-run seventh inning. Stivetts scored thrice and gathered two hits in the game, including a double.112

During the 1893 season, Stivetts (a protestant) involved himself in a dispute between two catholic players, Tommy Tucker and Hugh Duffy, and their manager Frank Selee. The resulting rift caused strife, on occasion, among team members. Factions formed, with Duffy, Tucker, and McCarthy on one side and Herman Long, Bobby Lowe taking the side of Stivetts. These disagreements, in part, led to an incident a Louisville, Kentucky hotel dining room between Stivetts and McCarthy. In repsonse to something Stivetts had said, McCarthy retaliated by punching him once in the face and then calmly left the restaurant.113114

His W<U+2013>L record slid to 17<U+2013>17, while his ERA improved to 4.64 in 291 inning pitched, as Boston fell to fifth place, 16.5 games behind the NL champions from Baltimore.115 His hitting suffered as well; his batting average dropped to a career-low .190, and he failed to hit a home run for the first time since his rookie season.116 Shortly after Boston has completed its schedule, Stivett re-signed for the 1896 season.117

1896 season

During the off-season, trade talks were conducted, but not completed, between the Beaneaters and the Philadelphia Quakers. The deal reportedly involved Stivetts and Tucker, two players involved in Boston's religious quarrel from the previous season.118 As the season progress, the press noted that his season had begun better than the previous season.119 Stivetts claimed that it was his weight loss (now just over 200 lbs), that was the main reason why he had been more of an asset to the team than in past years.120

Though he lost his first start of season, the rebounded to win six starts in seuccession from April 25 though May 20, including a 7<U+2013>0 shutout victory on May 8 versus Louisville.121 On June 12, he pitched Boston to a 15-3 victory over the Reds at home, and connected for two home runs against Chauncey Fisher.122123 This was the third, and last, time he hit two home runs in a single game.124 Hit three home runs total for the season, as well as improving his batting average to .347 in 222 at bats. He increased his win total to 22, and his 4.10 ERA was the lowest since 1892.125

On October 3, it was reported that he was reserved by Boston under the reserve clause.126 When interviewed during the off-season, Stivetts claimed that he would like to quit pitching and play first if a team would give him the opportunity. Selee, Boston's manager, disagreed and claimed that the team needed Stivetts' in the pitching rotation instead.127 It was also likely that he would have received less salary playing at first.128

1897 season

Boston re-signed Stivetts for $2100 depite the view of some in the press who believed Boston would continue to sink in the standings if they didn't rid themselves of him and the team's manager.129130 Shortly after Stivetts' first start of the season, a 10<U+2013>5 loss on April 23 in Baltimore, Selee was quoted as saying that Stivetts will not be pitching much into the future, saying that "he is growing rather stiff, and will not let himself out as he did when he was at his best, some years ago."131132 His weight was now at 217 lbs, so as the team departed on May 10 for a western road trip, Selee sent him back to Ashland to get back into proper game condition.133 Although he was told not to report back to the team until his weight was below 200 lbs, he had re-joined the team and played right field in the second game of a doubleheader on May 15.134135 His place in the rotation fell behind Nichols, Fred Klobedanz, and Ted Lewis.136 He made a relief appearance on May 18, before having to take some time off for a sore arm.137138 His next start occured on May 31, when Boston defeated St. Louis by a 25<U+2013>5 score.139 He earned his second consecutive victory on June 11, in his third start, this time a 12<U+2013>4 win versus Louisville.140

While he only pitched 129 1/3 innings in 18 games during the season, he fared well, and helped Boston re-claim the NL championship. He had an 11<U+2013>4 W<U+2013>L record, and his ERA dropped to 3.41. As a hitter, he continued to flourish, with .367 batting average, hit a career-high nine triples, and added two more home runs. At the conclusion of the regular season, Boston faced faced the second place Orioles in a post-season series; the Temple Cup. This was the last year the cup series was to take place, and was Boston's first appearance.

1898 season

Stivetts was again reserved by Boston, and later re-signed for $2000. It was now expected that he play as an extra outfielder, due to the Beaneaters' solid, four-man starting pitching rotation of Nichols, Klobedanz, Lewis, and rookie Vic Willis.141142143 Stivetts claimed that he had never felt better in life and outlook about the coming season was positive.144

In a game versus the Reds on June 9, the score was tied 5<U+2013>5 in the bottom of the ninth inning when Stivetts connected for a pinch-hit, walk-off solo home run. This was his second career walk off home run, his third as a pinch hitter, and the last home run of his career.145 It was reported on July 22, that a deal had been agreed upon to trade Stivetts the St. Louis Browns of the NL for Kid Carsey and cash.146 The deal was contigent on the acceptance of Stivetts.147 In early August, the Boston manager Selee, sent Stivetts home, commenting that the team did not have use of his services any longer.148 Tim Hurst, now managing St. Louis, planned on using Stivetts in center field.149 Stivetts refused to accept the move to St. Louis, and a sale was officially conducted on August 14, the sold him to St. Louis for $2000 and an unnamed player.150151152 True to his word, Stivetts did not report, or play a single game for St. Louis that season.153 Due to his dislike of being sold to St. Louis, he claimed that he would stay in Ashland and go back to coal mining.154

St. Louis/Cleveland

1899 season

During the off-season, the owners of the Cleveland Spiders purchased the St. Louis Browns from Chris Von der Ahe. The changed the team name to Perfectos, and inhereted a number of their players, including Stivetts. In early 1899, Stivetts claimed interest in playing for the St. Louis franchise if his salary were equal to what he was paid with Boston.155 The new owners, to maximize their assets, transferred all the best players to St. Louis, while sending the rest, including Stivetts, to Cleveland. The Spiders released Stivetts on June 13. He was claimed by St. Paul of the Western League a few days later.156 On July 12, 1899, it was reported that Stivetts had retired from playing baseball.157

Post-baseball

After his retirement was professional baseball, he worked for the coal mining industry in various capacities. He was listed as a carpenter in both the 1910 and 1920 Federal Census.158159 In April of 1912, he had been working as a carpenter for the Black Ridge mine in Conyngham, Pennsylvania.160 He occasionally played in on small clubs around the Ashland area, and in May of 1902, he survived a bout with Small Pox.161162 By 1930, he was no longer working.163 He died on April 18, 1930, at the age of 62 in Ashland, and is interred at Brock Cemetery.164

Legacy

He was described in the papers as one of the fastest pitchers in the league, along with Amos Rusie and Ted Breitenstein.165 Kid Nichols remarked in 1902 that Stivetts' speed was as fast as Amos Rusie, and could have extended his career much longer had he taken better care of his body.166 At the time, he was reportedly driving a brewery wagon for a coal company in his hometown of Ashland.167168 Baseball writer O. P. Caylor wrote that Stivetts' issues were entirely his own, blaming his hard-drinking lifestyle and his inability to remain in good condition.169
