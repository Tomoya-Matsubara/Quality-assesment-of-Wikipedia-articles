The Climate of the Los Angeles Basin is most characterized by a Mediterranean climate or Dry-Summer Subtropical (K<U+00F6>ppen climate classification Csb on the coast, Csa inland), and most of the Basin receives just enough annual precipitation to avoid K<U+00F6>ppen's BSh (warm semi-arid climate) classification. The Los Angeles Basin receives plenty of sunshine, with 320 sunny days and only 40 rainy days on average every year.

Summers are warm to hot and dry with average high temperatures of and lows of , however temperatures sometimes exceed during the summer. Winters are mild and somewhat rainy with high temperatures of and lows of . Spring and autumn bring mild days with cool evenings and are generally dry.

The Los Angeles area is also subject to the phenomenon typical of a microclimate. As such, the temperatures can vary as much as 18 <U+00B0>F (10 <U+00B0>C) between inland areas and the coast, with a temperature gradient of over one degree per mile from the coast inland.

Rainfall occurs mainly in the winter and spring months (February being the wettest month), with light rainfall, but sometimes as thunderstorms. Los Angeles averages of precipitation per year, but is lower at the coast and higher at the mountains. Tornadoes are extraordinarily rare downtown, though waterspouts can be seen during severe storms at beaches. Snow is extraordinarily rare in the city basin, but the mountainous slopes within city limits typically receive snow every year. The greatest snowfall recorded in downtown Los Angeles was on January 15, 1932.12

Overview

Fall

The Santa Ana winds are strong, extremely dry offshore winds that characteristically sweep through in Southern California and northern Baja California in late fall into winter. They can range from hot to cold, depending on the prevailing temperatures in the source regions, the Great Basin and upper Mojave Desert. However, the winds are remembered most for the hot dry weather (often the hottest of the year) that they bring in the fall.

Records

In downtown Los Angeles, weather observations were taken for many years at Civic Center before moving to the campus of the University of Southern California. The highest temperature recorded in downtown Los Angeles was on June 26, 1990. The lowest temperature was on December 22, 1944. The wettest year was 1983 with 34.04 inches and the dryest year was 1953 with 3.83 inches. The most rainfall in one month was 14.94 inches in January 1969. The most rainfall in 24 hours was 5.68 inches on March 2, 1938.3

At the Los Angeles International Airport, the highest recorded temperature was on September 26, 1963. The lowest temperature was on January 4, 1949. The wettest year was 1983 with 29.46 inches and the dryest year was 1947 with 2.95 inches. The most rainfall in one month was 13.79 inches in February 1998. The most rainfall in 24 hours was 5.60 inches on November 21, 1967.4

In Long Beach, the highest recorded temperature was on October 16, 1958, and October 15, 1961. The lowest temperature was on January 20, 1922. The wettest year was 1978 with 27.67 inches. The dryest year was 2002 with 2.63 inches. The most rainfall in one month was 14.50 inches in January 1969, including a record 24-hour rainfall of 6.71 inches on January 20. The record snowfall was 1.5 inches in January 1949.5
