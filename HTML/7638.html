__FORCETOC__ Andrei Kharlov (born on November 20, 1968) is a Russian chess player. In 1992, he recieved the title of grandmaster after tying for first place in the 1990 Russian National Championships.1 Since then, Kharlov has won the 1996 Eurocup and the 1998 Russian Club Cup with the Kazan chess team. In 2000, he tied for first in the Russian National Championship in Samara and tied for first in the European Individual Chess Championship in Saint-Vincent.2

Andrei Kharlov vs Vladimir Kramnik

This game between Andrei Kharlov and Vladimir Kramnik lasted only 8 moves before the players agreed to draw (known as a Grandmaster draw). 1. e4 d6 2. d4 Nf6 3. Bd3 e5 4. c3 Nc6 5. Ne2 d5 6. exd5 Qxd5 7. O-O Be7 8. dxe5 1/2-1/2.
