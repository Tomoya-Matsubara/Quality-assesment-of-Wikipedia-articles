Keryn Jordan is a former South African footballer who last played for Auckland City FC in the New Zealand Football Championship. He announced his retirment on March 19, 2010 due to on-going Knee injuries. He has played at the highest level in South Africa and New Zealand. He holds New Zealand citezenship.

Early career

He started his career in his native South Africa at Supersport United, before transferring to Manning Rangers where he won the golden boot during the 1997<U+2013>98 Premier Soccer League season with 11 goals. In the 2003/2004 season he also led with 8 goals.1

Waitakere United

He would move next to New Zealand, with ambitions to play for New Zealand Knights in the newly formed Australian/New Zealand A-League. However this never eventuated, and he signed with new New Zealand Football Championship team Waitakere United. This would ultimately be a successful move, scoring 15 goals in the 21 rounds, and earning the Golden Boot.2

Auckland City FC

At the end of what was a good debut season with Waitakere United, he made the move to Auckland City FC. He made an immediate impact for the Auckland Club, socring frequently for the club in both the NZFC and the Oceania Champions League.

In 2008 he shocked the New Zealand footballing community, when it was revealed that he had been battling with cancer for 3 years, which would explain the 3 months he spent on the sideline. He had initially been worried that it would interfere with his claims for permanent residency and New Zealand Citezenship, but was treated and given the all-clear by doctors and made his comeback in a 4-1 win against Canterbury United.3

His most notable appearances for the club were in the 2006 FIFA Club World Cup & 2009 FIFA Club World Cup, in which Auckland City finished 5th out of 7th, surprising many onlookers wordwide. It was the best result any New Zealand or Oceania club had finished in the tournement, which plays host to each confederations best teams.

On Friday, 19 March 2010, Jordan announced his retirements from all levels of Domestic and International football, citing ongoing knee injuries. Jordan hadn't played a full game since the 2009 Club World Cup, and he felt it the right time to retire, after what he conceded was an ultimately successful career.4

International career

Jordan has represented South Africa twice, both in 2000.
