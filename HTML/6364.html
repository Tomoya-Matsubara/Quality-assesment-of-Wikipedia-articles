Mississippi Highway 350 (MS 350) is a highway in extreme northern Mississippi. Its western terminus is at MS 2, and its eastern terminus is at MS 25. The route was designated in 1981, and no significant changes have been made since.

Route description

MS 350 is located in northeastern Alcorn and northern Tishomingo counties.1 In 2012, Mississippi Department of Transportation (MDOT) calculated as many as 2,900 vehicles traveling east of County Road 159, and as few as 1,500 vehicles traveling east of County Road 363.2 All of the road is maintained by MDOT.3 MS 350 is not included as a part of the National Highway System (NHS), a network of highways identified as being most important for the economy, mobility and defense of the nation.4 MS 792 is legally defined in Mississippi Code <U+00A7> 65-3-3.5

MS 350 starts at a T-intersection with MS 2 and travels eastward. The route goes through small groups of trees, and turns northeast east of County Road 154. The road enters a larger forest and curves back east later. There, MS 350 was less than from the Tennessee state line. The two-lane road intersects a few roads leading to the state border, such as Kendrick Road. MS 350 soon enters Tishomingo County, curving around some hills. It remains parellel from the state line, until at County Road 375, where it starts to travel southeast. The route temporarily travels east at County Road 355, before heading southeast again. MS 350 ends about , at a T-intersection with MS 25.6
