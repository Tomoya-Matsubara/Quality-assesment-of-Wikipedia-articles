Emslie Horniman's Pleasance is a park in the Borough of Kensington and Chelsea in London. Named after the London City Councillor Emslie J. Horniman who created it, and opened in 19141, it is the traditional kicking-off point of the Notting Hill Carnival.23

The Pleasance contains a notable walled garden in the Arts and Crafts style, designed for Horniman by C. F. A. Voysey.45 The garden is a Grade II listed structure.6

It is located on Bosworth Road in Ladbroke Grove, on the southern side of the Grand Union Canal, near to Notting Hill. The nearest tube station is Westbourne Park.

The park also contains tennis courts, five-a-side football pitches, a hard play area and a children's playground.7
