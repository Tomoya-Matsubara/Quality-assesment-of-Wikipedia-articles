"I Love Paris" is a popular song written by Cole Porter and published in 1953. The song was introduced by Lilo in the musical Can-Can. A line in the song's lyrics inspired the title of the 1964 movie "Paris When It Sizzles."

Notable recordings

  • Les Baxter and His Orchestra had a #13 hit in 1953
  • Michel Legrand released a version on his 1954 album, I Love Paris, which included an orchestral arrangement of the song1
  • Ella Fitzgerald released a version on her 1956 album, Ella Fitzgerald Sings the Cole Porter Songbook2
  • The Coasters released a version of the song on their 1958 album, Coasters3
  • Screamin' Jay Hawkins released a version on his 1958 album At Home with Screamin' Jay Hawkins4
  • Stan Kenton - The Stage Door Swings (1958)
  • Frank Sinatra & Maurice Chevalier sang the song in the film Can-Can in 1960 and the song as a bonus track on the album Come Fly with Me5
  • Andy Williams released a version on his 1960 album, Under Paris Skies.
  • Al Hirt released a version on his 1961 album, He's the King and His Band6 and was also featured on his greatest hits album, The Best of Al Hirt7
  • Jack Jones released a version on his 1961 album, I've Got a Lot of Livin' To Do8
  • Peter Cincotti released a version on his 2004 album, On the Moon9
  • Eleni Mandell released a version as a 2005 single; that same year it was used in the soundtrack of a television commercial for the Carl's Jr. fast-food chain, starring Paris Hilton.
  • Vanessa Paradis released a version on her 2009 album, Best of Vanessa Paradis10
  • Les Negresses Vertes covered the tune on the Cole Porter tribute album Red Hot + Blue for AIDS awareness
  • Lana Del Rey covered the song at the Paris stop of her Paradise Tour on April 27, 2013.
