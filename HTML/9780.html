William Carey Crane (March 17, 1816 <U+2013> February 27, 1885) was the President of Baylor University from 1864 to 1885.123

Biography

William Carey Crane was born in Richmond, Virginia, on March 17, 1816.45 He attended the Mount Pleasant Classical Institute in Amherst, Massachusetts and Virginia Baptist Seminary, now known as Richmond College.67 In 1883, he attended the Hamilton Literary and Theological Institute and Madison, now known as Colgate University.89 In 1836, he received a B.A. from Columbian College, now known as George Washington University, followed by an M.A. in 1839.10 From 1937 to 1939, he taught in Talbotton, Georgia and preached in Thomaston and Greenville.1112 He also preached at Mercer University.13 In 1838, he became a Baptist minister in Baltimore, Maryland.14 In 1839, he preached at the First Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama.15

In the 1840s, he served as a pastor in Columbus, Vicksburg, and Yazoo City.1617 In 1844, he was a Professor at Union University in Murfreesboro, Tennessee, and he edited The Baptist with R.B.C. Howell for two years in Nashville.18 He served as President of Mississippi Female College in Hernando from 1851 to 1857, Semple Broaddus College in Centre Hill, Mississippi from 1859 to 1860, and Mount Lebanon College in Louisiana from 1860 to 1863.1920 He was co-editor of the Louisiana Baptist and President of the Louisiana Baptist State Convention.21 He served as a pastor in Centre Hill, Coldwater, Oxford, Mississippi and New Connah, Tennessee.22 He was also an editor of the Mississippi Baptist.23 He was the cofounder and Vice-President of the Mississippi State Historical Society.24 He served as the general agent of the American Tract Society for two years.25 From 1851 to 1863, he served as Secretary of the Southern Baptist Convention and was its Vice-President four times in the 1870s and 1880s.2627

In 1863, he served as Pastor at the First Baptist Church in Houston, Texas.28 From 1864 to 1885, he was the President of Baylor University.29 He served as the pastor of the Independence Baptist Church for eighteen years from 1864 to 1867, and again from 1869 to 1884.30 He was also active in the Texas Baptist State Convention.3132 He was the first President of the Texas State Teachers Association and Chairman of the founding committee for Sam Houston Normal Institute, now known as Sam Houston State University.33 He was a member of the American Philological Association.34

Crane County, Texas is named after him.35
