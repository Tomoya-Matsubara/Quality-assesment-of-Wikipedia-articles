"Broken-Hearted Girl" is a song by American recording artist, Beyonc<U+00E9> Knowles. It was written by Knowles, Kenneth "Babyface" Edmonds, Mikkel Storleer Eriksen and Tor Erik Hermansen, and was produced by Knowles, Eriksen and Hermansen for Knowles' third studio album, I Am... Sasha Fierce (2008). Having previously been announced as the album's sixth single, it was delayed, with "Sweet Dreams" released instead. "Broken-Hearted Girl" later became the album's seventh single, and was released on August 28, 2009.

An R&B and pop ballad, the song features a moderate tempo and uses piano, strings and drums. It received generally positive reviews from critics, who praised Knowles' vocal talent. It appeared on several singles charts, and reached the top twenty in Australia, Brazil, the Czech Republic, Germany and Ireland. The accompanying music video for "Broken-Hearted Girl" reminisces on a former relationship, and uses shows some scenes in reverse.

Background and release

"Broken-Hearted Girl" was written by Beyonc<U+00E9> Knowles, Kenneth "Babyface" Edmonds, Mikkel Storleer Eriksen and Tor Erik Hermansen, and was produced by Knowles, Eriksen and Hermansen.1 It was recorded in 2007 at Roc the Mic Studios, New York City.2

"Broken-Hearted Girl" was originally lined up as the sixth single from I Am... Sasha Fierce, however "Sweet Dreams" was released instead. "Broken-Hearted Girl" was later released as the album's seventh single.3 The single was released in Australia and New Zealand on August 28, 2009,45 and October 30, 2009 saw its release in most of Europe.6 "Broken-Hearted Girl" was released in Germany on November 20, and in Brazil on November 23, 2009.78

Composition

"Broken-Hearted Girl" is a R&B and pop ballad, and uses the piano as its base.91011 It also features tender strings and hollow drums.1213 According to EMI Music Publishing's sheet music published at Musicnotes.com, the song is set in common time with a moderate tempo of 84 beats per minute.14 It is written in the key of D Minor, and follows the chord progression of Dm-B-Fadd9-F-C/E. Knowles' vocal range spans from the low note of F4 to the high note of F6.15

Critical reception

Colin McGuire of Pop Matters said that "The piano-balladry of 'Broken-Hearted Girl' is simply more Celine Dion than Aretha Franklin".16 Nana Ekua Brew-Hammond of Village Voice called the song "romantic-comedy soundtrack fare".17 Jennifer Vineyard of MTV News complimented the song's domination of piano and strings and stated "Her voice shows restraint, and singing in a lower register gives her more vocal power".18 Digital Spy's Nick Levine gave the single a mixed review, commending the use of piano and strings, but criticizing the drums sounds. He praised Beyonc<U+00E9>'s "emotion-filled vocals", but noted it does not compare to her earlier songs "Crazy in Love" and "If I Were a Boy", and singles from Destiny's Child's "still-peerless" Writing's on the Wall and Survivor.19

Chart performance

"Broken-Hearted Girl" debuted on the UK Singles Chart at number fifty-six on October 3, 2009. It rose to its peak position of number twenty-seven on November 14, 2009, and slipped off the chart in December 2009 after twelve weeks.20 It also made it to number twelve on the UK R&B Chart.21 In Australia, the song reached the high point of number fourteen on the ARIA Singles Chart, where it spent two weeks. After nine weeks in the top fifty, it fell off. "Broken Hearted-Girl" peaked on the Irish Singles Chart at number twenty,22 and lasted nine weeks.23

The song found further success in mainland Europe; it reached number thirty-seven on the European Hot 100 Singles.24 It attained top-twenty places on the Czech Republic Airplay Chart,25 the German Singles Chart26 and the Irish Singles Chart,27 while reaching the top forty in Austria and Slovakia.<ref name="aut"/28 "Broken-Hearted Girl" also appeared on the Swiss Singles Chart29 and the Belgian Tip Charts,3031 as well as the Brazilian Hot 100 Airplay.32

Music video

A video for the single was released on June 16, 2009 on the Above and Beyonc<U+00E9> - Video Collection & Dance Mixes.33 The video was directed by Sophie Muller,34 who previously directed "D<U+00E9>j<U+00E0> Vu" and "Ring the Alarm" for Knowles.3536 The video was included on the video album, Above and Beyonc<U+00E9> <U+2013> Video Collection & Dance Mixes.37

The beginning of the video is shot in black-and-white, and shows Knowles in a car crying before walking along a beach. Scenes of memories are displayed of Knowles and her partner spending time there previously. Midway through the song, the video transitions into full color, where she is in a green dress with a red rose. A scene shows Knowles repairing a rose, a reverse cut of her pulling the petals off. The video returns to black and white memories, and ends with Knowles returning to her car, smiling, and driving off.
