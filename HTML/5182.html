Bill Cullen (born 20 May 1942) is an Irish businessman and philanthropist. He is the former owner of the Renault Ireland franchise1 until it was taken under more direct control by Renault in 2007. A relatively well-known figure in Ireland, Cullen has made numerous appearances on RT<U+00C9>'s The Late Late Show and his autobiography became a best-seller.2 He is listed as one of Ireland<U+2019>s top 100 businessmen.3

Biography

Early life

Born into poverty in the Summerhill area of Northside Dublin during The Emergency, Bill Cullen was the eldest of fourteen children, seven boys and seven girls. Cullen was born in a caul which was considered a good luck omen at the time. His mother, Mary Darcy was a fruit seller and Cullen started trading at market stalls from a very young age.45 His father Billy Cullen, her husband was for the most part unemployed, working on the docks and earning a laborers wage. 6

Career

He finished his education with the Christian Brothers at the age of thirteen to work on the markets full-time, selling fruit, vegetables and whatever else he could as a means of supporting his family. In 1956 Bill got a job as a messenger boy at Waldens Ford Dealer and worked his way up through the company to be appointed director general of Waldens in 1965. He went on to set up the Fairlane Motor Company in Tallaght in 1977 which he developed into the biggest Ford dealership in Ireland by 1981. In 1986 he took over the franchise from for Renault car distribution from Waterford Crystal. The initial purchase price of the operation was only a <U+00A3>1 however the deal entailed taking on a debt burden of <U+00A3>18 million. 7 8 This company became known as the Glencullen Group and the Renault distribution arm became known as Glencullen Distributors. Turnover of the business grew to <U+20AC>350 Million at its peak. 9 In April 2006 Bill was awarded the distinction of Chevalier de la L<U+00E9>gion d'honneur by decree of the President of the French Republic for his leadership in the turnaround of Renault Ireland from near bankruptcy. In November 2007, the Renault operation was taken over by Renault S.A.S after achieving sales of 11,900 in 2006. It now operates as a subsidiary of the parent company. 10 He continues to own Ireland's largest Renault retailer, CityGate Motor Group. It has six dealerships around the country - three of which are in Dublin. 11 He also set up and owns the Europa Academy, a training facility in Swords, north County Dublin. 12 He took over the the Muckross Park Hotel in Killarney in 1990 with his partner, Jackie Lavin, Who is also his business partner. The hotel achieved 5 Star status in 2007. 13

Other business positions

As well as his interests in the motor and hotel industries, Cullen also hold executive positions in other areas:-

  • In March 2009 Cullen became an Executive Editor of RSVP business magazine 14
  • Bill Cullen sits on the board of Directors of Dublin Airport Authority 15
  • Cullen also offers his services as a motivational speaker to Schools college and the business community. 16

Homes

He owns a 43-room mansion in County Kildare and residences in Florida and County Kerry. 17

Philanthropological work

Cullen set up and is a director of the charity "The Irish Youth Foundation" which raises <U+20AC>1.5 million a year for youth projects in Ireland. All royalties from Cullen's 2002 autobiography, It's a Long Way from Penny Apples, were donated to this charity.18

Cullen set up the Bill Cullen Sunshine Scholarships Fund in 2001 in order to assist young athletes make the transition to senior athletics while continuing their third level education in Ireland. 19

In February 2005, Bill Cullen brokered a sponsorship deal for Home Farm F.C., a club which he used to play for, worth <U+20AC>1,000,000 in a sponsorship deal with several groups with support from Renault Ireland The aim was to establish the club as a major soccer academy. The Irish Department of Sports, Arts and Tourism, the Irish Youth Foundation and the club president, Tony O'Reilly also contributed to the fund. As part of the arrangement Home Farm jerseys featured the Renault logo alongside the club's distinctive crest. 2021

Recognition of achievements

In 1998, he was a recipient of the Lord Mayor's Award for his work with the disadvantaged young people of Dublin.

In September 2004, he was awarded the inaugural Princess Grace Humanitarian Award.

Bill was named Maxol Irish Motor Industry Person of the Year 2000. 22

He was conferred with an honorary Doctorate of Law of Maynooth University in May 2005 23

Involvement in the Media

Cullen is a popular figure in the irish media and is considered a minor celebrity, appearing on television shows, such as The Late Late Show, Tubridy Tonight and The Restaurant on RTE 24 25 He has been parodied by Tom Dunne for several years on the Today FM Gift Grub comedy segment. This has led to him being affectionately known as "Dr. Bill because of his honorary doctorate. 26 Most recently he has taken on the role as key business "guru" in the Irish version of TV show The Apprentice.27

The Apprentice

In June 2008, TV3 began inviting applications for an Irish version of popular television franchise The Apprentice. In the programme, Cullen takes on the role filled in the U.S. version by Donald Trump, and in the UK version by Alan Sugar. 28 His partner Jackie Lavin also acts as a mentor to the apprentices. The show started its second season in September 2009.

Books

Cullen released his autobiography entitled It's a Long Way from Penny Apples (ISBN 9780765310439) in 2002 detailing his life growing up in inner city Dublin and rising to become one or Irelands most succesful business people.

In 2006 Cullen released his second book Golden Apples: Six Simple Steps to Success (ISBN 03408389810, a self help book in which he details principles to achieving business success.

Politics

Cullen is not affiliated to any political party however, Glencullen Holdings made political donations during 2002 of <U+20AC>13,459. 29

Cullen has been fiercely critical of the the Green Party since it came to power, particularly the effect the change in Vehicle Registration Tax and motor taxation policy in July 2008 has had on the Irish motor industry, blaming them for the collapse in new vehicle sales in 2009, saying that these changes were unnecessary. 30 31

Cullen is one of a number of high profile members along with Packie Bonner and Mick Galwey of the group 'We Belong' who are campaigning for a yes vote in the second Irish constitutional referendum on the Lisbon Treaty. 32

Trivia

Bill Cullen took the Guinness World Record for the largest ever book signing in Easons, O<U+2019>Connell Street on 16th April 2005 by signing 1849 copies of Golden Apples in 10.5 hours. This was 32 copies more then the previous record. 33 34

Bill Cullen is booked to be one of the first passengers to take a flight into space on Richard Bransons Virgin Galactic service at a cost of $200,000. 35
