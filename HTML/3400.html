Maria Neculi<U+0163><U+0103> (born March 30, 1974 in Deva Romania) is a Romanian artistic gymnast who competed in international events between 1988 and 19921. Her best events were the floor and the beam. She won team silver medals at the 1992 Olympics and 1991 World Championships. Individually, she won silver on beam and bronze on floor at the 1992 Worlds and a continental bronze on beam in 1990.

Career

Maria started gymnastics at the age of eight at Cetate Deva Gymnastics Club. In 1987 she was selected for the senior national team trained by coaches Octavian Belu and Mariana Bitang2.

Her international debut as senior was at the 1988 Moscow news where she medaled bronze on the floor exercise and placed sixth all around and on the uneven bars3. In 1990 she competed in her first major international competition, the 1990 European Championships, Athens4. In Athens she won bronze on the balance beam and she placed fifth on floor and sixth all around5.

Neculi<U+0163><U+0103> won the bronze medal with the team at the 1991 World Championships6. The team showed a good performance in the team optionals but, together with Eugenia Popa, Maria was heavily criticized by the press and by coach Belu for having let the crowd interfere with their performance during the team compulsories7. At this competition Maria did not qualify in any of the final events8

However, one year latter she had one of her best meets at the 1992 World Championships, tying for silver on beam with Li Yifan and for bronze on floor with Tatiana Lysenko9. Her floor routine showed musicality, style and world class tumbling, establishing her as one of the medal contenders on floor for the 1992 Olympic Games10. In 1992 she also tied with Gina Gogean for the all around title and won the floor title at the 1992 International Championships of Romania11.

Together with Lavinia Milo<U+015F>ovici, Gina Gogean, Mirela Pa<U+015F>ca, Vanda H<U+0103>d<U+0103>rean, and Cristina Bonta<U+015F>, Maria was a member of the silver winning Romanian team12 at the 1992 Summer Olympics Barcelona, Spain. Unfortunately, in the qualifications for the floor and beam finals she placed seventeenth and sixteenth, respectively13. Therefore, she did not compete in the final events. Instead, her teammates Milo<U+015F>ovici and Bonta<U+015F> won the gold and the bronze in the floor final, respectively.

Her last competition was the 1992 Balkan games were she won the all around (ahead of Gina Gogean), the floor and beam finals and finished second on bars14.

Post retirement

Maria retired in 1992. In 1995 she went to Italy to coach in Neuro at the Centro Sportivo Osaka club15.
