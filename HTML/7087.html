Jos<U+00E9> Luis L<U+00F3>pez Panizo (born Sestao, Vizcaya, January 12 1922), usually shortened to Panizo, was a Spanish/Basque footballer who played for Athletic de Bilbao, SD Indautxu and Spain during the 1940s and 1950s. Panizo first played for Nuevo Porto Arsenal and then joined Sestao Sport Club for the 1938/39 season. He made his debut for Athletic de Bilbao during the 1939/40 season. He was a member of the Atl<U+00E9>tico team that won the La Liga/Copa double in 1942/43 and the Copa del Generalisimo three times in a row between 1943 and 1945. He played 405 games for Atl<U+00E9>tico including 326 in La Liga and scored 126 league goals in sixteen seasons. Panizo made his debut for Spain in 1946 and subsequently played 14 games, scoring twice. He was a member of the Spanish squad during the 1950 FIFA World Cup. He finished his playing career at SD Indautxu
