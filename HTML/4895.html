David Clendon is a New Zealand politician and member of the Green Party of Aotearoa New Zealand. Following the resignation of Sue Bradford, Clendon will become a Member of the New Zealand Parliament on 30 October.

Political Involvement

In both the 1999 elections and the 2005 elections Clendon polled third in the seat of Waitakere.

Clendon was the co-convenor of the Green Party from 2001 to 2004.

In the 2008 general elections Clendon stood as a candidate in the Helensville electorate, coming third with 5.96% of the electorate vote, with Prime Minister-elect, John Key, winning the seat.

Member of Parliament

Following the resignation of Sue Bradford Clendon was declared elected, taking effect on October 30, 2009.1
