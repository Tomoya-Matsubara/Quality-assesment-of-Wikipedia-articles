Camp Paxson Boy Scout Camp (24MO77), located on the shore of Seeley Lake, Lolo National Forest, Montana, is on the National Register of Historic Places and started out as a summer camp for the Boy Scouts of America (BSA). The United States Forest Service granted the Western Montana Council of the BSA to build a summer camp, originally with six small structures and tents, in 1924. More space was needed and in 1939 more facilities were begun by the Works Progress Administration and Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC). They were completed in 1940 under the supervision of Forest Service engineer Clyde Fickes. There are twenty buildings constructed out notched Western Larch logs. Uncut native stone was used for the fireplaces. The site has also been used by the United States Forest Service as a smokejumper school for conscientious objectors in the Civilian Public Service Program during World War II.12 This smokejumper program operated from May 1943 to April 1946 and was known as "CPS Camp No. 103".3 It is the only CCC-constructed youth camp in Montana.4
