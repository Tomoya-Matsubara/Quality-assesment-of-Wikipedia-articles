"Lovers in Japan" is a song by English alternative rock band Coldplay. It was written by all members of the band for their fourth album, Viva la Vida or Death and All His Friends. The song is built around an introductory tack piano sound, then followed by chiming guitars and soaring choruses, supported by the pianos and rhythms that accompany the song's lyrics. "Lovers in Japan" also includes a hidden track "Reign of Love".1

It was released on 4 November 2008 as the fourth single from Viva la Vida or Death and All His Friends and has reached number 10 in Billboard's Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles chart. The song has also appeared on Billboard's Hot Adult Top 40 Tracks on the number 32 peak position. "Lovers in Japan" received positive reception, with music critics noting the track's sound.

Writing and composition

When asked about the development of the song, during a track-by-track reveal, drummer Will Champion said: "We were in a studio in New York, this place called the Magic Shop, and it had this thing called a tack piano there, which sounds like an old honky-tonk piano, where you put little tacks in the hammers, so it sounds like more of a harpsichord almost. And so we wanted to use that kind of sound, but we didn't have a tack piano, so rather than sample it, we went and bought an old piano from the shop up the road from our studio, and we bought a load of tacks, and me and Guy and Jon spent a couple of hours pushing tacks into the piano hammers."23

In an interview with Q magazine, when asked about why "Lovers in Japan" had a hidden track, Guy Berryman explained that the band could not make up their minds, because they had discussed that they did not want an extra song on the album, and instead they wanted to keep the album concise with a total of ten tracks.4 Champion followed with, "We just preferred to have less titles and more stuff. The album as a whole has got the most on it, but it<U+2019>s the shortest. We wanted to make it almost impossible for you to not listen to it all in one go."5

The song's musicscape features an introductory detuned tack piano sound,6 then followed by an upbeat chiming guitar and soaring choruses.78 Towards the end, it builds into a rhythmically soaring, U2 sound.910 Martin sings about runners who must run until the race is won and soldiers who must soldier on in the second and third verse of the song.111213 Martin also sings about the sun coming out one day.14 The song also mentions of time being short.15

Release and reception

Coldplay released "Lovers in Japan" in the US on 4 November 2008 as the fourth single of their fourth album.16 The song is also featured in the band's 2008 EP Prospekt's March, entitled as "Lovers in Japan" (Osaka Sun Mix),17 when it was released on 21 November 2008. Vocalist Chris Martin in discussion about the song, said: "We've been playing it live better than it is on the record. So the live version has informed this Osaka Sun version, which is just a bit more lively."18

"Lovers in Japan" appeared on Billboard's Hot Adult Top 40 Tracks chart in the peak position of 32 on 29 November 2008.19 The track peaked at number 10 on Billboard's Bubbling Under Hot 100 Singles chart on 13 December 2008.20 Also on 13 December 2008, the song appeared in Billboard's Pop 100 chart,21 in the peak position of 65. Although the song did not chart on the UK Singles Chart, as it was released as a radio-only song in the US, the song appeared on Japan Hot 100 Singles at number 45 on 19 December 2008.22

Critics were positive towards the song. Alexis Petridis of The Guardian review of the album, wrote: "Chris Martin's melodies ... shine: even his loudest detractor could hardly deny his way with a tune as evidenced here by '42' and 'Lovers in Japan'."23 Evan Sawdey of PopMatters noted, "On the stunning, jaw-dropping highlight 'Lovers in Japan', Martin pounds away at a bouncy toy-piano melody ... leading into a chorus where guitarist Jonny Buckland gets to unleash what might be the catchiest guitar riff he<U+2019>s written (and yes, he absolutely bathes in the moment)."24 Joey Guerra of the Houston Chronicle added that "Lovers in Japan" was a "joyous, romantic anthem that combines wistful rock riffs and gorgeous piano."25 He also wrote that the song is one of "the disc's best moments".26 Josh Hathaway from The Plain Dealer wrote: "'Lovers in Japan/Reign Of Love' works in just the opposite direction with 'Lovers' playing like an update of vintage Simple Minds, an almost anthem that never fully takes flight, before giving way to the gentle 'Reign of Love'."27

Music video

A music video for the single, directed by Mat Whitecross in London, was released on 31 October 2008 through iTunes Store, where it was available free for a week on iTunes US and iTunes Canada.28 The video utilises the "Osaka Sun Mix" version of the song.2930

The first half of the video shows the band playing in a courtyard while each member writes certain words from the lyrics in glowing neon on the screen. During the chorus, the camera spins around in circles to go along with the lines "They are turning my head out".31 The video ends with butterfly-shaped confetti raining on the band, as happens when each chorus is played at live concerts.32
