Anthony James Keck was a politician in England.

He was Member of Parliament (MP) for Leicestershire from 1755 - 1756, also for the rotten borough of Newton in Lancashire from 1768 - 1774. He lived at the since demolished Stoughton Grange until he moved to Lancashire in 1768 and died aged 42 years, on 28th February 1782, and is buried at St Mary and All Saints Church in Stoughton, Leicestershire.1 along with numerous members of his family and decendants including his son (who was also a politician for Leicestershire),

Family

He married Elizabeth Legh who was the second daughter of Peter Legh of Lyme, and by her had six children of whom Peter Robert Anthony Keck died an infant and was buried in the family church on January 11th 1768. Elizabeth Keck, who was also an infant, died and was buried on February 5th 1773. Anthony Peter Beaumont Keck, who was their third child to died and was buried on March 14th 1777. There are marble plaques in rememberance in the family church in Stoughton. Another son Peers Anthony Keck also died unmarried aged 28, and buried on March 12th 1797. The only son to survive to old age was George Anthony Legh Keck who died at Bank Hall, Lancashire but was brought back to the family church for burial in 1860.2
