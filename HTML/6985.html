Media

Manga

Koharu Biyori is written and illustrated by Takehito Mizuki. The manga was serialised in MediaWorks's monthly Dengeki Daioh before MediaWorks moved the title to the quarterly Dengeki Moeoh. The manga is licensed in North America by ComicsOne. The English license was transferred to DrMaster after ComicsOne's collapse.4 As of February, 2009, MediaWorks has published 4 bound volumes of the manga. ComicsOne released the first volume of the manga on February 16, 2005.5 The manga is licensed in Taiwan by Ever Glory Publishing.6

Anime

The manga was adapted into a 3-episode original video animation by Doumu. The anime is licensed in the United States by Sentai Filmworks and distributed by ADV Films.7 However, with ADV being shut down, distribution was now handled by Section23 Films. It was also distributed in Russia by MC Entertainment.8 The episodes were released in Japan between October 14, 2007 and April 2, 2008.

Geneon released 3 DVDs in Japan. The first DVD was released on November 21, 2007.9 The second DVD was released on February 8, 2008.10 The final DVD was released on April 2, 2008.11 The subtitled series was released on March 10, 2009 in the US, there is no English-dubbed version available.12

The original video animations use three pieces of theme music. by Eri Kitamura is the series' opening theme, while by Eri Kitamura and Satomi Akesaka is the ending theme for the first two episodes. by Eri Kitamura is the ending theme for the third episode.

Reception

Mania.com's Eduardo M. Chavez criticised the manga for having "no plot" and a cast that "lacks personality". He also criticised the manga for the overuse of the moe anthropomorphism.16 Liann Cooper from Anime News Network criticises the manga for having "no plot" and that the manga's "illustrations look like sloppy sketches".17 Anime Fringe's Janet Crooker classifies the manga as a Chobits parody.18

Mania.com's Chris Beveridge commends the OVAs for its "character designs are good, the fanservice just right and it plays things at a slightly different angle because of the androids and the lack of a real crush/interest in the main characters".19 THEM Anime's Carlos Ross criticises the OVA for "very juvenile" writing and further comments that the OVA "" is not a cleanly or adroitly written work in regards to pacing". He also criticises the "very abrupt, kludgy transitions (particularly between episodes two and three) that seem to come clean out of left field".20
