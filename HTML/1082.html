Thi<U+1EC1>n Buddhism (<U+79AA><U+5B97> Thi<U+1EC1>n T<U+00F4>ng) is the Vietnamese name for the Zen school of Buddhism. Thi<U+1EC1>n is ultimately derived from Chinese Ch<U+00E1>n Z<U+014D>ng (<U+79AA><U+5B97>).

History

According to traditional accounts of Vietnam, in 580, an Indian monk named Vin<U+012B>taruci () traveled to Vietnam after completing his studies with Sengcan, the third patriarch of Chinese Ch<U+00E1>n. This, then, would be the first appearance of Vietnamese Thi<U+1EC1>n Buddhism.

The sect that Vin<U+012B>taruci and his lone Vietnamese disciple founded would become known as the oldest branch of Thi<U+1EC1>n. After a period of obscurity, the Vin<U+012B>taruci School became one of the most influential Buddhist groups in Vietnam by the 10th century, particularly so under the patriarch V<U+1EA1>n-H<U+1EA1>nh (died 1018).

Other early Vietnamese Thi<U+1EC1>n schools included that of Wu Yantong, called V<U+00F4> Ng<U+00F4>n Th<U+00F4>ng in Vietnamese, which was associated with the teaching of Mazu Daoyi, and the Th<U+1EA3>o <U+0110><U+01B0><U+1EDD>ng, which incorporated nianfo chanting techniques; both were founded by Chinese monks. A new school was founded by one of Vietnam's religious kings; this was the Tr<U+00FA>c L<U+00E2>m school, which evinced a deep influence from Confucian and Taoist philosophy. Nevertheless, Tr<U+00FA>c L<U+00E2>m's prestige waned over the following centuries as Confucianism became dominant in the royal court.

In the 17th century, a group of Chinese monks led by Nguy<U+00EA>n Thi<U+1EC1>u established a vigorous new school, the L<U+00E2>m T<U+1EBF>, which is the Vietnamese pronunciation of Linji. A more domesticated offshoot of L<U+00E2>m T<U+1EBF>, the Li<U+1EC5>u Qu<U+00E1>n school, was founded in the 18th century and has since been the predominant branch of Vietnamese Thi<U+1EC1>n.

Thi<U+1EC1>n master Th<U+00ED>ch Thanh T<U+1EEB> is credited for renovating Tr<U+00FA>c L<U+00E2>m in Vietnam. He is one of the most prominent and influential Thi<U+1EC1>n masters currently alive. He was a disciple of Master Th<U+00ED>ch Thi<U+1EC7>n Hoa.

The most famous practitioner of syncretized Thi<U+1EC1>n Buddhism in the West is Th<U+00ED>ch Nh<U+1EA5>t H<U+1EA1>nh who has authored dozens of books and founded Dharma center Plum Village in France together with his colleague, Thi<U+1EC1>n Master bhikkhuni Ch<U+00E2>n Kh<U+00F4>ng.
