Thomas Hale Hamilton (August 4, 1914 <U+2013> December 25, 1979) was an American academic administrator who served as president of the State University of New York and the University of Hawaii.1

A native of Marion, Indiana, Hamilton received his A.B. (1936) from DePauw University and his A.M. (1940) and Ph.D. (1947) from the University of Chicago. He headed the State University of New York system from 19592, leaving in 1963 to assume the Presidency of the University of Hawaii.3 Hamilton resigned his presidency in Hawaii over a tenure scandal in 1967.4

Thomas Hamilton married the former Virginia Prindiville on June 1, 1940 and raised a son and a daughter.5 He died in Honolulu at the age of 65. The University of Hawaii's Hamilton Library is named in his honor.
