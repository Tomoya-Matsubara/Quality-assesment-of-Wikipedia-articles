Sisse Graum J<U+00F8>rgensen (born 8 May 1972) is a Danish film producer12 and sits on the board of the film company Zentropa.3 Films she has produced include '<U+201D>H<U+00E6>vnen<U+201D> <U+201C>Jagten<U+201D> and <U+201C>Dear Wendy<U+201D>. She also sits in the advisory board for TrustNordisk, a Scandinavian international film distributor.

Early life

J<U+00F8>rgensen was born in Copenhagen, Denmark where she also received her Bachelor (HA) degree at the Danish Business School Handels h<U+00F8>jskolen.

Career

In 2003 Graum J<U+00F8>rgensen became a member of the European Academy, which promotes European films and the film industry throughout the world.

Graum J<U+00F8>rgensen started her carrier making advertisements. In 1991 she worked on her first movie for Zentropa Productions APS4 as a production trainee5 and assistant producer for the co founder Peter Aalb<U+00E6>k Jensen. Ten years later she debuted as a producer with Niels Arden Opleys movie <U+201C>Chop Chop<U+201D> (Fogsvansen 2001).

Graum J<U+00F8>rgensen worked with Danish directors Lars Von Trier and Thomas Vinterberg, and, beginning in 2002, with Susanne Brier. The first movie they worked on was Biers <U+201D>Open Hearts<U+201D> (Elsker dig for evigt 2002) and J<U+00F8>rgensen was the co producer. After this she and Brier worked together in several movies and she produced <U+201C>Brothers<U+201D> (Br<U+00F8>dre 2004),<U+201D>After the Wedding<U+201D> (Efter brylluppet 2006) that was nominated for an Oscar. They also made Golden Globe, Oscar and EFA-winner <U+201C>In a Better World<U+201D> (H<U+00E6>vnen 2010). Their last collaborations was <U+201C>Love is all you need<U+201D> (Den skaldede fris<U+00F8>r 2012) written by Anders Thomas Jensen and Susanne Bier and a post production drama <U+201C>A second chance<U+201D> (En chance til) will premier in 2015.
In 2006 she helped to organize a film initiative called "Advance Party", designed to promote the film industry in Scotland.67

Personal

Graum J<U+00F8>rgensen is married to Ulrik J<U+00F8>rgensen and have four children.

Awards and Nominations

Sisse Graum J<U+00F8>rgensen has produced many award winning motion pictures. <U+201C>The Hunt<U+201D> (Jagten 2012) directed by Thomas Vinterber was nominated at the 2014 Oscar and Golden Globe for the best foreign film, as well as 29 awards and 37 nominations, including British independent Film Award 2012 (Best international independent film), and European film awards where they won for best screenwriter and nominated in four other categories, including Best Film. Other nominated and winning films are <U+201C>En Famillie<U+201D> (2010),<U+201D>Red Road<U+201D> (2006) , <U+201D>Perfect Sense<U+201D> (2011).

European Film Promotion chose J<U+00F8>rgensen as their <U+201D>Producer on the Move<U+201D> in 2003. Screen International wrote about her in their <U+201C>Talent to Watch<U+201D> and Variety<U+00B4>s Cannes chose her as one of <U+201C>10 Producers to Watch<U+201D>. In 2013 she won the Erik Ballings Rejselegat Prize handed out by the Nordic Film Council8 together with her co worker at Zentropa, Louise Vesth.
