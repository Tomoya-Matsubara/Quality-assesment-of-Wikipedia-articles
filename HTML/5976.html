Izta-Popo, Zoquiapan and Anexas National Park is a national park on the border of the Mexican states of M<U+00E9>xico and Puebla. It covers Mexico's second and third-tallest mountains, Popocat<U+00E9>petl and Ixtacc<U+00ED>huatl, as well as the areas of the Hacienda de Zoquiap<U+00E1>n and its annexed areas (or anexas) of Zoquiapan, Ixtlahuac<U+00E1>n, and the R<U+00ED>o Fr<U+00ED>o river.

The park was established November 7, 1935.1
