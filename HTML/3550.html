The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (Filipino: Suriang Pilipino ng Bulkanolohiya at Sesmolohiya, abbreviated as PHIVOLCS) is a Philippine national institution dedicated to provide information on the activities of volcanoes, earthquakes and tsunamis, as well as other specialized information and services primarily for the protection of life and property and in support of economic, productivity and sustainable development.

Phivolcs "is principally mandated to mitigate disasters that may arise from volcanic eruptions, earthquakes, tsunami and other related geotectonic phenomena." It was created in 1982 as successor to the Commission on Volcanology which was created in 1952.1

Phivolcs monitors volcano, earthquake, and tsunami activity, and issues warnings as necessary.

This agency is headed by Renato Solidum.
