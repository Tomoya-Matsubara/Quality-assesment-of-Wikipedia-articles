The UCL Eastman Dental Institute is a dental school located in London, United Kingdom and an academic department of the Faculty of Medical Sciences of University College London (UCL).1 It is based on Gray's Inn Road in the Bloomsbury district of Central London adjacent to the Eastman Dental Hospital, with which it is closely associated.234

The Institute is the largest postgraduate dental centre in Europe, and together with the Eastman Dental Hospital forms one of the largest concentrations of oral health care specialists and research in Europe.56 The Institute runs a CPD programme with the British Dental Journal which is one of the largest of its type, with around 14,000 dental care professionals enrolled.7

History

In 1926 George Eastman donated <U+00A3>200,000 to fund the establishment of a dental clinic in London, after being approached by the Chairman of the Royal Free Hospital, Lord Riddell, with donations of <U+00A3>50,000 each also made by Lord Riddell and the Royal Free honorary treasurer.8 On 20 November 1931, the Eastman Dental Clinic opened as part of the Royal Free Hospital. In 1948, the Eastman became independent of the Royal Free and became the postgraduate dental institute of the Postgraduate Medical Federation.9 In 1949 the Eastman opened a department of pathology and microbiology and the first civilian oral hygiene school in the UK.1011

During the Directorship of Professor Frank Wilson (1950<U+2013>1959) a full department range was established at the Eastman, including children<U+2019>s, oral surgery, radiography and prosthetics.1213 The Directorship of Sir Robert Bradlaw (1959<U+2013>1970) saw the Eastman<U+2019>s teaching concept altered, with MSc courses of the University of London being introduced.14 In 1988 the Eastman moved to the old Royal Free buildings on Gray's Inn Road and in 1992 the Eastman's name was changed to the Eastman Dental Institute (EDI).15

In October 1995 the Institute established a joint project with the Rutherford Appleton Laboratory to develop ultraviolet lasers to replace conventional dental drills.16 The Institute merged with University College London in 1999, becoming the UCL Eastman Dental Institute,17 and in 2002 it won the Queens Award for Higher Education.1819

Research

The Institute achieved top scores of 5 in both the 1996 and 2001 Research Assessment Exercises and the Institute's research programme is one of only two oral themes to achieve national funding within an NHS Biomedical Research Centre, as part of the UCLH/UCL Comprehensive Biomedical Research Centre.20 Both the Institute and the Eastman Dental Hospital are part of UCL Partners, the largest academic health science centre in Europe.21

Research at the Institute is currently represented by three interdisciplinary divisions:22

  • Biomaterials and Tissue Engineering Division
  • Clinical Research Division
  • Microbial Diseases Division

Education

At postgraduate level the Institute currently offers the following masters programmes:23

  • MSc in Conservative Dentistry
  • MSc in Endodontics
  • MSc in Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery
  • MSc in Oral Medicine
  • MSc in Restorative Dental Practice
  • MSc in Special Needs Dentistry
  • MClinDent in Endodontology
  • MClinDent in Oral Surgery
  • MClinDent in Orthodontics
  • MClinDent in Periodontology
  • MClinDent in Prosthodontics

The Institute offers a clinical doctorate programme in Paediatric Dentistry and an MSc in Dental Public Health through the UCL Department of Epidemiology and Public Health.2425

The Institute also offers a large number of short courses, Certificate Programmes and Diploma Programmes.26

Library

The Institute library contains around 3,000 books and 70 locally held journal titles, and also has access to many e-journals, e-books and online databases.272829

All staff and students of the Institute, UCL and the Eastman Dental Hospital are entitled to full membership of the library.30
