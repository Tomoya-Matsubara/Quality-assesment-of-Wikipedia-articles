The 2009 Gianh River boat accident occurred on 25 January 2009 on the Gianh River near Qu<U+1EA3>ng H<U+1EA3>i Village, in the Quang Trach district of Quang Binh Province in Vietnam.1 A wooden boat sank 20 meters from the shore in strong currents during windy conditions. There were reportedly over 80 people on board, yet the boat was capable of carrying only 20. The accident caused 42 deaths and five people were missing. The Prime Minister of Vietnam sent his condolences to the victims<U+2019> families. The government of Quang Binh Province decided to cancel the fireworks supposed to take place at midnight the same day, on the eve of T<U+1EBF>t, the New Year Day in Vietnam.2
