Alexander Wilson Crozier (9 July 1904 <U+2013> 19 June 1974) was an Australian rules footballer who played with in the Victorian Football League (VFL).1

The son of John Crozier and Agnes Catherine Alexander, Alec Crozier joined Hawthorn at the start of the 1926 VFL season from Caulfield.2 After spending almost all season in the reserves he played in the final game of the season against Melbourne.3 Despite not making the senior squad at the start of the year4 he was frequently named in the better players in the reserves in 1927 and played another three senior games.

Alec Crozier married Lorna Pearl Tepper and they lived in various areas in Melbourne until his death in 1974.5
