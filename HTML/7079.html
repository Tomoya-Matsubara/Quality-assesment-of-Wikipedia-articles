Francis Joseph Boyle (June 14, 1927 â€“ September 11, 2006) was a United States federal judge.

Boyle was born in Providence, Rhode Island. He received a J.D. from Boston College Law School in 1952. He was in the United States Navy from 1945 to 1946. He was in private practice of law in Pawtucket, Rhode Island in 1953. He was in private practice of law in Newport, Rhode Island from 1953 to 1977.

Boyle was a federal judge to the United States District Court for the District of Rhode Island. Boyle was nominated by President Jimmy Carter on May 2, 1977, to a seat vacated by Edward William Day. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on June 30, 1977, and received commission on July 1, 1977. Served as chief judge, 1982-1992. Assumed senior status on December 1, 1992. Boyle 's service was terminated on September 11, 2006, due to death.

He died in Newport, Rhode Island.
