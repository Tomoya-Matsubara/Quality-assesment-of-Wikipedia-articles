The Barr Hotel is a historic hotel on the eastern side of downtown Lima, Ohio, United States. Built in 1914, the Neoclassical hotel occupies the northeastern corner of the intersection of High and Union Streets.1

Architecture

The primary portion of the hotel is a brick building, seven stories tall;2 it rests on a concrete foundation3 and is covered by a flat roof.4 Next to this portion is a connected building, four stories high and also built of brick.5 Large sash windows and Ionic-capped pilasters are among the most distinctive elements of the primary portion of the hotel.6

Historical context

During the early twentieth century, Lima experienced rapid growth as a result of its place as a hub for Standard Oil and as the home of the Lima Locomotive Works. Many large buildings were erected in the city's downtown, including the still-standing Hotel Argonne and the Ohio Theatre.7 The Barr Hotel is a typical building of this period.8

Recognition

In 1986, the Barr Hotel was listed on the National Register of Historic Places because of its significance in overall American history. As a little-altered example of Lima's golden years,9 the hotel was significant partially because of the unusually frequent destruction of historic buildings in Lima since the early twentieth century.10
