Lamine Gu<U+00E8>ye (1891, M<U+00E9>dine (now in Mali) <U+2013> 1968) was a Senegalese politician who became leader of the Senegalese Party of Socialist Action (Parti S<U+00E9>n<U+00E9>galais de l'Action Socialiste). In 1945 he and his associate Leopold Senghor were elected to represent Senegal in the French National Assembly, Gueye was also elected to the French Senate in 1958. He gave his name to the 1946 Lamine Gu<U+00E8>ye law (loi Lamine Gu<U+00E8>ye) which granted French citizenship to the inhabitants of France's overseas colonies.

He was the grandfather of Senegalese alpine skier Lamine Gu<U+00E8>ye.1
