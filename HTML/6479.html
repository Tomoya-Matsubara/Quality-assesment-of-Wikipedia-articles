Mladen <U+0160>ubari<U+0107> (born 1920) is a Croatian chess master.

In 1939 he tied for 3rd-4th in Zagreb (Svetozar Gligori<U+0107> won).1 In December 1941, he played at fourth board against dr Pazman (2 : 0) in a match Croatia <U+2013> Slovakia in Zagreb.2 <U+0160>ubari<U+0107> took 9th at Munich 1942 (European Individual Chess Championship, Wertungsturnier <U+2013> Qualification Tournament, G<U+00F6>sta Danielsson won).34

After World War II, he played several times in Yugoslav Chess Championship. In 1945 he tied for 12-16th in Novi Sad (Petar Trifunovi<U+0107> won).5 In 1946 he tied for 14-16th in Zagreb (Trifunovi<U+0107> won).6 In 1948, he tied for 17-18th in Belgrade (Gligori<U+0107> and Vasja Pirc won).7

In other tournaments, he won ahead of Mijo Udov<U+010D>i<U+0107> at Zagreb 1948,8 and took 2nd, behind Braslav Rabar, at Zagreb 1950 (CRO-ch).
