Hot Fuss is the debut studio album by American post-punk band The Killers, released in the United States and United Kingdom in June 2004.1 The album reached #1 in both the UK and Australia, and was certified 3x Platinum in Canada (300,000 units) by the CRIA on October 17, 2005.2 It stayed in the Top 50 of the Billboard 200 chart for over fifty weeks.3 Singles from the album include, in order released, "Somebody Told Me", "Mr. Brightside", "All These Things That I've Done", and "Smile Like You Mean It". Towards the end of 2005, The Killers also released the album on 7" vinyl in a boxset of 11 discs.4 On side A of each of the discs is the album track, and on the B-side are non-album tracks.5 It is also one of the five most recent albums listed as one of the 1001 Albums You Must Hear Before You Die. Hot Fuss sold more than 2,500,000 copies in the first year of its release.6 After being nominated for five Grammys, it has since sold more than 5,000,000 copies worldwide.78
