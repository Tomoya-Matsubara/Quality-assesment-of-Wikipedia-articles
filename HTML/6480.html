Mammoth Mammoth (often stylized as MAMMOTH MAMMOTH) are an Australian Hard rock/Stoner rock band, formed in 2007 in Melbourne, consisting of vocalist Mikey Tucker, guitarist Ben Couzens (Cuz), bassist Pete Bell, and drummer Frank Trobbiani (Bones).

They independently released their debut self-titled EP in June 2008 through their label, Golden Triangle, followed by their full-length released album Volume II: Mammoth in August 2009.

In November 2012, their third record Volume III: Hell<U+2019>s Likely was released, and after good sales in Europe were approached by and signed an international recording deal with Austrian label Napalm Records.1

Mammoth Mammoth<U+2019>s sound has been likened to <U+201C>Motorhead doing mushrooms with Black Sabbath at a Butthole Surfers concert.<U+201D>2

The band are well known for their raucous live shows, with Heavy Magazine Australia noting that <U+201C>their live reputation precedes them<U+201D> and lead singer <U+201C>Mikey Tucker knows no boundaries and goes in hard every time; throwing himself off the stage and throughout the crowd.<U+201D>3

Mammoth Mammoth were banned from performing at two music venues in Melbourne. At the Tote, Tucker headbutted a glass window to get the attention of some punters smoking outside, and fell through it, but continued the show covered in blood.4

 

History

Background and name

Couzens had been in Melbourne rock band Furious Dragon Love with Trobbiani, but was jamming with his friend and bassist Gareth Sweet (Gash) on the side in the original two piece line up of Mammoth Mammoth. They enlisted Tucker, whose band Black Fang was folding, as lead singer and Trobbiani on drums to form the original line up.

Couzens and Sweet originally started Mammoth Mammoth because they were tired of the Melbourne rock scene, which they described as being full of <U+201C>shoe-gazing indie-rock bands doing very little to entertain or commit.<U+201D>5

The band take their name from the wooly mammoth, because it was the <U+201C>biggest<U+201D> name they could think of.

<U+201C>We wanted a name that was big, hairy, sweaty and a bit stupid. So what<U+2019>s bigger than a mammoth? A mammoth mammoth!<U+201D>

- Ben Couzens 6

 

Volume I: Mammoth Mammoth EP (2008)

In 2008, the band recorded their debut EP before they even played live together.7

<U+201C>Then when we finally played live we took no prisoners. For all of us it was about giving people a real show and apologising for nothing.<U+201D>

- Frank Trobbiani 8  

At their earlier live shows, the audience and the press weren<U+2019>t sure if they were serious or joking when performing.9

The band are known for their hands-on approach when creating their videos and concert posters as well as their album artwork10

The song Weapon of Mass Self-Destruction from their EP features a video that was banned from YouTube due to its explicit content of 70s vintage porn images, so they posted it on porn site tube8, where it received millions of views in its first few days.

 

Volume II: Mammoth and line up changes (2009-2011)

In 2010 on the back of the release of their self-titled debut album, the band toured Australia with fellow Melbourne hard rock band Airbourne. On returning from touring, Gash left the band,11 and was replaced by Simon Jauney (Spiff), before the band settled on Pete Bell as the band<U+2019>s permanent bass player.

 

Volume III: Hell<U+2019>s Likely (2012 <U+2013> Present)

Mammoth Mammoth created the basic outline for Volume III over a continuous three day jamming session.12 They later recorded the album at Toyland and Goatsounds Studios in Melbourne, Australia, over a two-week period. They enlisted producer Jason Fuller, bassist for Australian metal band Blood Duster, to work with them on the album.13

<U+201C>We just played around with a bunch of jams. Generally Mikey will say to me, <U+201C>Hey, play a dark riff kinda like this...<U+201D> Then I ignore him and play something different and he<U+2019>ll be too drunk to realise and start singing his lyrics anyway. Then Pete joins in on bass and Bones comes back from taking a shit and gives it some heavy weight - before you know it we have new song.<U+201D>

- Ben Couzens 14

 

In 2012, after the Australian release of Volume III: Hell<U+2019>s Likely, Mammoth Mammoth were approached by Napalm Records and signed an international recording contract.15 The album was re-released Internationally to positive reviews and was supported by their European tour in 2013.

Metal Hammer magazine<U+2019>s German, French and UK editions voted Hell<U+2019>s Likely in at number two for <U+2018>album of the month<U+2019> in December 2012.16

 

Style

The band describe their sound as <U+201C>patented, good-time murder-fuzz<U+201D> and <U+201C>more awesome than God<U+2019>s tits<U+201D>. They state the effect of hearing their music is <U+201C>capable of sometimes killing unicorns<U+201D>.

Mammoth Mammoth describe their songwriting style as <U+201C>four guys in a boxing ring with no referee<U+201D>.17
