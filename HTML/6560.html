Bookham is a small village in the Southern Tablelands of New South Wales, Australia and in Yass Valley Shire. It is about 29 km west of Yass on the Hume Highway. It has a population of about 100.

thumb|200px|left|Bookham Memorial Hall Bookham was originally called Bogolong and inspired Banjo Paterson's poem Old Pardon the Son of Reprieve, based on his childhood memories of races on the Bogalong Racetrack. There are two churches: the Uniting Church (c1926) and the Catholic Church, St Columbas (c1910). 1
