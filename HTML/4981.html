Barda<U+00EF> is a small town and oasis in the extreme north of Chad, the main town of the Tibesti area, one of the four departments of the Bourkou-Ennedi-Tibesti region.

Barda<U+00EF> came to international attention in 1974, when a rebel group, led by Hiss<U+00E8>ne Habr<U+00E9>, attacked the town and captured a French archeologist, Fran<U+00E7>oise Claustre and two other European citizens.
