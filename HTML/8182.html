Thanasis Veggos, alternatively spelt Thanassis and/or Vengos (Greek: <U+0398><U+03B1><U+03BD><U+03AC><U+03C3><U+03B7><U+03C2> <U+0392><U+03AD><U+03B3><U+03B3><U+03BF><U+03C2>; pronounced: Than<U+00E1>ssis V<U+00E9>ngos; May 29, 1927 May 3, 2011) was a Greek actor and director born in Neo Faliro, Piraeus.123 He performed in more than 120 films, predominantly comedies in the 1950s, 60s, and 70s.4

Background

Veggos was the only son of a power station employee who had fought as a member of the Greek resistance during the Second World War.5 He was exiled to Makronisos from 1948 to 1950 for political reasons.678 On Makronisos he met film director Nikos Koundouros who was also exiled there.9

Life and career

Nikos Koundouros gave him his debut movie role in Magiki polis in 1955.1011 His first major role was in Psila ta heria Hitler ("Hands Up, Hitler"), 1962.12 He often played everyman characters struggling to get by, but he has took om anti-heroes, pure dramas, and on stage acted in the comedies of Aristophanes'.13 His characters were often self-named "Thanasis". He often worked with director Giorgos Lazaridis. In 1995, Theo Angelopoulos cast Veggos and American actor Harvey Keitel in "Ulysses Gaze".14

In 2000, he survived a car accident involving a collision with a train.15 He later participated in advertisements promoting road safety.16

A documentary of his life, whose title translates as A Man for All Seasons, was made in 2004. He always did his scenes without using a stuntman even for the most dangerous ones, like hanging with a single rope from a balcony, or walking through a glass door. During the "Golden Sixties" of the Greek film industry he made the most famous of his films like the sequel of Secret Agent 000, Papatrehas, Enas trellos Vengos and many others, most of them by his own company <U+0398>-<U+0392> comedy films (Tainies Geliou).17

In 2008, Veggos was appointed Commander of the Order of the Phoenix by the President of Greece, Karolos Papoulias.18

On the 3rd of May 2011, he passed away at 7:10 a.m. He had been hospitalized at the Red Cross hospital since the 18th of December 2010.19

Legacy

He is survived by his wife Asimina and two sons.20 He will always be remembered in the more than 120 films and more recent documentaries that he starred in.21 The catchphrase "<U+03C4><U+03C1><U+03AD><U+03C7><U+03B5><U+03B9> <U+03C3><U+03AC><U+03BD> <U+03C4><U+03CC><U+03BD> <U+0392><U+03AD><U+03B3><U+03B3><U+03BF>" (English translation: "runs like Veggo") has been adopted in to common usage in the Greek language since nobody has run more or faster than Veggo in his many slapstick comedies.22
