On the night of 16 December 2012, a paramedical student was gang raped in Delhi.1 The victim and her friend boarded a bus in South Delhi after watching a movie, when they were beaten by a group of six males, after which the female victim was raped.2 The victim is being treated at Safdarjang Hospital where she was on ventilatory support for five days.34 The incident has generated widespread public anger and protests on Indian streets and international coverage.567 The incident was condemned by UN Women, who called on the Government of India and the Government of Delhi "to do everything in their power to take up radical reforms, ensure justice and reach out with robust public services to make women<U+2019>s lives more safe and secure".8

On Delhi Chief Minister Sheila Dixit's suggestion, five fast track courts were approved to try the rape cases.9 In an interview she also said that she "hates Delhi being called 'rape capital' but it has indeed become one".10 The Parliament's Standing Committee on Home Affairs would meet on 27 December 2012 to discuss the issue with summons being issued to Union Home Secretary R. K. Singh and Delhi Police Commissioner Neeraj Kumar.11 On 21 December 2012, the government promised to file the charge sheet in the case <U+201C>quickly<U+201D> and seek maximum punishment of life imprisonment for the accused.12

Incident

The couple was on their way home after watching Life of Pi at a South Delhi multiplex. The couple boarded a bus at about 9 pm when a group of men, who were on the bus, began harassing her. When her friend tried to intervene, he was gagged and then hit mercilessly with an iron rod. Five men then hit the woman with the same rod and gang-raped her while the driver kept the bus moving. At about 11 pm, the couple was thrown semi-naked onto the road. A passerby phoned the police, who then collected the couple and moved the pair to a hospital. The girl had been hit with the iron rod for nearly forty-five minutes.13

On 21 December 2012, a committee of doctors was constituted by the government of Delhi to monitor the treatment of the victim and to ensure that she receives the best possible medical attention.14

Victims

The student is from Ballia district in Uttar Pradesh. The victim's male friend, aged 28, is from Gorakhpur in Uttar Pradesh and lives in Ber Sarai in Delhi.15

Alleged perpetrators

Six men have been arrested in connection with the incident.16

Ram Singh

Ram Singh was the driver of the bus. He had taken the bus out for a joy ride on Sunday evening.17 He was the first accused to be arrested and was produced before the Metropolitan Magistrate on 18 December 2012. He had cleaned the bus which was later impounded by the police from Sector 3 of R K Puram on 18 December 2012.18

Mukesh Singh

Mukesh Singh is the brother of the main accused Ram Singh.19 He was arrested by the Delhi police in Rajasthan.20

When in the Tihar Jail, he was also assaulted by other inmates which prompted the jail officials to separate him from the rest of the inmates to protect him from further harassment. 21 22

Vinay Sharma

Vinay Sharma is an assistant gym instructor in Delhi23 and was arrested in Delhi.24

Pawan Gupta

Pawan Gupta is a fruit seller by profession.25 He was arrested in Delhi.26

Raju

Raju was detained by the police in Badaun, Uttar Pradesh.27

Akshay Thakur

Akshay Thakur studied at the Tandwa school in Jharkhand. The accused had gone to Delhi to look for a job. 28 He was arrested from Aurangabad in Bihar.29

Prosecution

Three of the four accused <U+2014> Mukesh, Pawan and Vinay <U+2014> were produced in the Saket court on 19 December 2012, where they confessed to the crime. The court has remanded Pawan and Vinay to four-day police custody, while Mukesh was sent to judicial custody for fourteen days. Vinay Sharma confessed to beating up the victim's male friend and demanded that he himself be hanged. The third accused, Mukesh, brother of main accused Ram Singh, did not confess to the crime but agreed to undergo identification test by the victim's male friend. All the accused are facing charges under sections 365 (kidnapping or abducting), 376 (2)(g) (gang rape), 377 (unnatural offences), 394 (hurting in committing robbery) and 34 (common intention) of the Indian Penal Code. The victim's male friend testified in the court on the same day.30

On 21 December 2012, the Delhi High Court rapped the Delhi police for being "evasive" in its probe status report in providing details of its officers on patrol duty in the area covered by the bus,31 with further hearing on the matter to be taken by the court on 9 January 2013.32

Reactions

The incident has generated public anger with widespread protest organised throughout the country.33 Many protestors have also been demanding death penalty for the accused, which, however, is not currently permitted by the Indian constitution. Indian prime minister Manmohan Singh defined the incident as "very upsetting" and a "heinous crime".34

The following day, there was an uproar in the Indian parliament over the incident. MPs in both houses had set aside their regular business to discuss the gruesome rape case and demanded strict punishment for those who carried out the attack. Leader of the opposition in the Lok Sabha, Sushma Swaraj, demanded that "the rapists should be hanged".35 Congress president Sonia Gandhi had visited the Safdarjang Hospital and met doctors on duty in the anaesthesia and surgery departments to get an update on the victim<U+2019>s condition.36 Bahujan Samaj Party chief, Mayawati, said that proper investigation and strict punishment should be ensured and that the "action should be so strict that no one should dare to act in such a manner again".37 Jaya Bachchan said that she was <U+201C>terribly disturbed<U+201D> over the incident, and felt <U+201C>ashamed<U+201D> sitting in the House as she was <U+201C>helpless<U+201D> for <U+201C>not being able to do anything<U+201D>.38 Meira Kumar, speaker of the Lok Sabha, told reporters outside the parliament that a "new law should be brought in and must get passed to ensure the safety of women. The laws at present are not enough, we need stricter laws."39

Bollywood actors Shahrukh Khan, Amitabh Bachchan, Salman Khan, Kareena Kapoor, Juhi Chawla and Gul Panag came out in support of the victims. 4041 Filmmaker Karan Johar tweeted, "Disgusted and apalled at the tragic rape incident in Delhi...severe and immediate punishment has to be served!!! Its shameful....".42

Indian cricketers Pragyan Ojha, Harbhajan Singh, Aakash Chopra, Manoj Tiwary and Sanjay Manjrekar expressed disappointment over the incident. Yuvraj Singh wrote "shocked with the delhi rape incident. Something is terribly wrong somewhere. If this is the way women are treated God saves our society" on his twitter account.43 Later he dedicated his Man-of-the-Match award against England to the rape victim.44 Businessman Anand Mahindra asked for the social media "to sustain relentless pressure for action".45

Sitting Supreme Court judge, Justice Gyan Sudha Misra, labelled the culprits as "cobras and beasts" and that the "trial in cases relating to crimes against women needs lot of reforms".46 Author Chetan Bhagat and civil society activists Arvind Kejriwal and Kiran Bedi also voiced their anger.47

Sheila Dikshit Chief Minister of Delhi admitted that she did not have courage to meet the victim.48

Protests

Public protests took place in New Delhi on 22 December 2012 at the India Gate and Raisina Hill, the later being a high-profile political area. The protesters at Raisina Hill were targeted with baton charge, water cannons and tear gas shells by New Delhi police, in an attempt to disperse the protest. These protesters were boys and girls from different parts of Delhi with the common intention of demanding justice for the victim. The protesters from JNU, Jamia, DU and other institutes also voiced their demand for giving justice in the case of Soni Sori.{aisa pamphlet from JNU on 22/12/12}. Thirteen protesters were injured in the police action.49

In Kolkata, a mass rally was organised on the same day, and thousands of participants also lit candles to show their solidarity with the victim.
