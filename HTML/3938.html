Amateur boxing has been practised in Mongolia since 1948 and Mongolia national amateur boxing team consists of 27 boxers.1 Mongolian national amateur boxing athletes represents Mongolia in regional, continental and world tournaments and matches sanctioned by the Amateur International Boxing Association (AIBA). Mongolian olympic boxing national team was founded in 1960.Communist government of Mongolia banned boxing, however, the government ended ban on boxing soon. Professional boxing began in Mongolia in the 1990s.

Achievements

Summer Olympics

Mongolian boxers first participated at the Olympic Games in 1972.

2004 Athens Olympics

Uranchimegiin M<U+00F6>nkh-Erdene represented Mongolia in this edition of the Summer Olympics. He won his first bout but was defeated in the second.
