Raymond "Ray" Pennick (born 30 November 1946) is an English former footballer who played as a forward. He played for Willington and York City.

Career

Born in Ferryhill, County Durham, Pennick was a student teacher when playing for Willington in the Northern League.1 He impressed in a friendly against Fourth Division York City in March 1963 and was signed by the club on amateur terms later that month.23 His debut came as a 60th minute substitute for Archie Taylor in a 0<U+2013>0 draw at home to Halifax Town on 5 May 1969.4 This was Pennick's only appearance for York and he left the club in June 1969.5
