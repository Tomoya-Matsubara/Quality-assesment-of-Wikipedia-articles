152 mm gun M1935 (Br-2) () was a 152.4 mm Soviet heavy gun.

Development

Work on a long range 152 mm gun for Reserve of the Main Command units started in 1929, when the Bolshevik Plant in Saint Petersburg recieved from the Artillery Directorate requirements specifications for such a piece. The project recieved a factory index B-10. The first barrel was ready in April 1932. Testing of the B-10 continued until 1935 and revealed a number of problems, including slow elevation, low rate of fire and short service life. As a result, the gun was not adopted. The two produced pieces were used for a series of experiments: their barrels were modified for firing pre-rifled projectiles and poligonal projectiles respectively; an attempt was made to improve elevation speed by use of an electric motor. The experiments did't produce practical results.
