San Diego County, California has been called "the Craft Beer Capital of America."123 As of November 2014 the county is home to 97 licensed craft breweries and brewpubs, with 41 more in the planning stages.4 The county's brewers have pioneered several specialty beer styles, most notably Double India Pale Ale (Double IPA), sometimes called San Diego Pale Ale.56 Its beer culture is a draw for tourism, particularly during major festivals such as San Diego Beer Week and the San Diego International Beer Competition. San Diego County breweries like AleSmith Brewing Company and Stone Brewing Co. are consistently rated among the top breweries in the world.7

History

The San Diego Brewing Company opened in 1896 as the first commercial brewery in San Diego County. At the time it was the largest manufacturing enterprise in the county. It was located on 32nd street in San Diego. It closed in 1920 because of Prohibition. It reopened in 1935 and continued in operation until 1942, when it was displaced by the U.S. Navy's 32nd Street Naval Station. A brewpub by the same name now operates in Mission Valley.8

Mission Brewery, located in the Middletown neighborhood of San Diego, opened in 1913 and closed due to Prohibition. It was never reopened as a brewery, but the landmark five-story brick building was repurposed several times, for kelp extraction until the 1980s and ultimately as an office building, still in operation. The building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1989.9 The name Mission Brewery was revived in 2007 by a brewery at 14th and L Streets in Downtown San Diego.10

The Aztec Brewing Company, founded in Mexicali in 1921, moved to San Diego in 1933 following the repeal of Prohibition. It closed in 1953 after being purchased by National Brewing Company. For the next 36 years there was no local commercially brewed beer in San Diego County. The name Aztec Brewing Company has been revived in a Vista brewery which opened in 2011.11

During Prohibition (1920â€“1933), San Diegans could still drink beer by crossing the border into Mexico. In the formerly small town of Tijuana more than 180 cantinas were built to serve American visitors, and the city's population grew tenfold between 1920 and 1930. Several modern breweries were built in Mexicali to supply the demand.12

Before Prohibition there had been seven breweries in San Diego. After repeal there were three: Aztec Brewing Company, San Diego Brewing Company, and Balboa Brewing Company. These were soon either purchased by the Big Three national brewing companies (Anheuser-Busch, Coors, and Miller) or driven out of business by the aggressive marketing practices of the Big Three.13 From 1953 until the 1980s the local beer market was dominated by the large national brewers, with the alternative being imported beer from Mexico. But changes in national and state law in the late 1970s made home brewing legal, and in 1982 California allowed breweries to operate restaurants on their premises, which made brewpubs possible.14

In 1989 the Karl Strauss Brewing Company opened a brewery and brewpub on Columbia Street in Downtown San Diego and expanded into distribution brewing in 1991. This was the first commercial brewery in San Diego since 1953, and for many San Diego consumers it was their first exposure to freshly brewed beer and to styles such as amber lager and pale ale.15 In the mid-1990s a second, more adventurous wave of craft breweries appeared, led by Pizza Port, Stone and Alesmith.16 The local craft beer movement was inspired in part by homebrewing hobbyists, supported by the storefront Home Brew Mart, which opened in 1992 and later spun off the Ballast Point Brewing Company.17

Beer festivals like the Real Ale Festival, launched in 1997 by Tomme Arthur and Tom Nickel, promoted the new styles and educated a new generation of beer drinkers.18 By 2004 San Diego was so well established in the brewing world that the biennial World Beer Cup competition was held there; 11 medals went to locals.19 San Diego hosted the event again in 2008, taking 14 medals, and in 2012, taking 16 medals.20 The "Best Small Brewer" Award has gone to San Diego County brewers three times: 2004 (Nickel for Oggi's Pizza), 2008 (Arthur for Lost Abbey/Port Brewing Company), and 2010 (Ballast Point brewers).

Economic impact

Brewing has been one of the fastest-growing business sectors in San Diego, with seven new licenses issued in 2010, fifteen in 2011 and eighteen in 2012.21 In 2011 the county's brewers and brew pubs generated $300 million in direct economic impact to the county, making $600 million in sales and generating or sustaining 2,800 jobs.22 In addition, beer-related tourism is an important part of San Diego County's visitor industry throughout the year;23 the county is described as a "beer city destination".24 Many breweries offer tours of their facilities and tasting rooms, and dozens of commercial tour operators market "beer tours" in San Diego County.

Annual events

San Diego Beer Week, launched in 2009, is now a ten-day county-wide festival, held in early November and aimed at both local residents and tourists. It showcases the local beer scene and fosters knowledge about the area's brewing heritage.25 The festival kicks off with the San Diego Brewers Guild Festival26 and continues with hundreds of large and small events hosted by breweries, nano-breweries, pubs, and alehouses. In 2012 there were more than 500 events scheduled during Beer Week.27

The San Diego International Beer Festival takes place during the San Diego County Fair every June. It includes seminars and classes, beer tasting, and beer judging. Participating breweries in 2013 came from 22 states and 16 countries.28 Other special beer events throughout the year include the San Diego Beer Festival in July,29 the San Diego Festival of Beer in September,30 and the Strong Ale Festival in December.31

Notable breweries

One-third of all the breweries in the county are located in the North County along the stretch of California State Route 78 from Oceanside to Julian, so that the route is sometimes called the "Hops Highway."32

Stone Brewing Co. is the largest brewery in Southern California and the tenth largest craft brewery in the United States.33 Located in Escondido in San Diego's North County, it is known for hoppy, high-alcohol beers and an in-your-face attitude. (The company's best-known brand, Arrogant Bastard Ale, uses the slogan "Hated by many. Loved by few. You're not worthy.")34 Arrogant Bastard Ale is regarded as a "watershed beer" that "put San Diego on the craft brew map."35 Stone Pale Ale is their flagship brew. The company produces nine beers year-round as well as special releases. It also has two brewery restaurants, a store, and a tasting room. In 2014 Stone announced plans to open brewery restaurants in Richmond, Virginia and in Berlin, Germany.3637

Karl Strauss Brewing Company was the first brewery in San Diego since 1953 and launched the current wave of breweries. It is now the second largest brewery in San Diego County.38 It operates a production brewery in San Diego's Pacific Beach neighborhood as well as eight brewery restaurants in San Diego, Orange, Los Angeles and Riverside counties. The company's flagship brew is Karl Strauss Amber Lager. Its best selling beer, Red Trolley Ale, won gold medals in its category at both the Great American Beer Festival (2011 and 2013) and the World Beer Cup (2010 and 2012).39

Ballast Point Brewing Company was opened in 1996 and is now the third-largest craft brewery in the county.40 In 2010 the company was named the World Champion Small Brewery at the World Beer Cup.41

The five brewpubs of Pizza Port, together with its spinoffs Port Brewing Company (San Marcos) and Lost Abbey (San Marcos), have won dozens of medals in international competition. Pizza Port started brewing commercially in 1992; in 1994 it became the first-ever San Diego brewery to win a medal at the Great American Beer Festival.42 In 2012 Pizza Port's Ocean Beach location won three medals at the World Beer Cup including two golds, and the Carlsbad location won two; Lost Abbey also won a gold and a bronze. The Great American Beer Festival named Pizza Port's Carlsbad location the "best large brewpub" in 2009, 2010 and 2011.43 Port Brewing/Lost Abbey was named the "Small Brewing Company and Small Brewing Company Brewer of the Year" at the Great American Beer Festival in 2007, and the Best Small Brewing Company at the World Beer Cup event in 2008.44 The franchise took five medals at the 2013 Great American Beer Festival.45

AleSmith Brewing Company, located in San Diego, was founded in 1995. Many of their beers are strongly hopped and high in alcoholic content. Between 1998 and 2012 the company won eight medals in World Beer Cup competition, including four gold.46 It was the most popular brewery on RateBeer's surveys in 2004 and 2005. In 2008, AleSmith was awarded "Small Brewing Company and Small Brewing Company Brewer of the Year" at the Great American Beer Festival.47
