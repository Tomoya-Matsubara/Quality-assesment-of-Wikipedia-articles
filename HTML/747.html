"Love the Way You Lie" is a song by the American hip hop recording artist Eminem from his seventh studio album Recovery (2010). Skylar Grey, a singer-songwriter, wrote and recorded the demo alongside the producer Alex da Kid when she felt she was in an abusive romantic relationship with the music industry. Eminem wrote the verses and chose the Barbadian singer Rihanna to sing the chorus; their past experiences in difficult relationships influenced the collaboration. Recording sessions were held in Ferndale, Michigan, and Dublin, Ireland. Backed by guitar, piano and violin, the track is a midtempo hip hop ballad with a pop refrain and describes two lovers who refuse to separate despite being in a dangerous love<U+2013>hate relationship.

Interscope Records released the song in August 2010 as the second single from Recovery. Critics praised its melody but were divided on thematic aspects such as poignancy and accuracy. Eminem promoted the single with performances at the 2010 Electronic Entertainment Expo, the 2010 MTV Video Music Awards and festivals. The music video, directed by Joseph Kahn, stars Dominic Monaghan and Megan Fox in a violent relationship and shows Eminem and Rihanna in front of a burning house. The clip had a mixed reception due to scenes of domestic violence. Reporters suggested that the song and its accompanying video were influenced by Eminem's and Rihanna's relationships with their respective ex-lovers Kimberly Scott and Chris Brown.

Critics listed "Love the Way You Lie" among the best tracks of 2010 and of Eminem's career. The song won many awards and received five Grammy nominations. It is Eminem's best-selling single and was number one on twenty-six record charts, including the United States' Billboard Hot 100 for seven weeks. The single sold more than five million copies in the US and was the best-selling song of 2010 in the United Kingdom. Musical acts such as Cher Lloyd and The Band Perry performed cover versions. Rihanna has said that the theme of domestic violence, a topic on which she claims many people do not have insight, is what makes the song effective. She later recorded "Love the Way You Lie (Part II)", narrated mostly from her perspective.

Writing and production

Development of "Love the Way You Lie" began with the backing track and hook melody, which were created by the British hip hop producer Alex da Kid.1 He spent hours making loops and produced an electronic drum rhythm before adding live instrumentation. The drums were sampled from live recordings he had saved.2 Alex da Kid recorded an acoustic guitar for the verses with a Georg Neumann U87 microphone and an Avid Mbox audio interface. He told Sound on Sound magazine that this song had given him an opportunity to reintroduce live guitar in hip hop music.3 During the session, he used Waves RVerb and REQ equalization on his main kick track and a MaxxBass plug-in on two others.4

Writing began in late 2009, when the American singer Holly Brook Hafermann resided at the Artula Retreat in Bandon, Oregon,5 and composed songs.6 Later in New York, under her new stage name Skylar Grey, she visited her Universal Music publisher Jennifer Blakeman to seek help presenting her work;78 Blakeman suggested she collaborate with Alex da Kid.9 The producer emailed Grey his track, which suggested a theme of abusive romantic relationships to her as she spontaneously sang lyrics to the melody.10 She told the Los Angeles Times that she was in an abusive relationship with the music industry: "I love it so much, and I give it all that I have, yet it beats me down."11 This idea inspired her writing,12 a process she found came naturally, as if she were creating an alternative pop song for herself.13 She wrote the chorus to "Love the Way You Lie" in fifteen minutes and recorded vocals for Alex da Kid's demo.1415

In early 2010, Alex da Kid offered the Shady Records Senior Director Rigo "Riggs" Morales some backing tracks.16 Morales enjoyed the tracks and sent them to Eminem, who was seeking a different musical approach for his next album.17 Impressed with Alex da Kid's work, Eminem asked for more tracks and subsequently heard "Love the Way You Lie". He chose it and told his manager Paul Rosenberg he wanted to collaborate with the Barbadian singer Rihanna.18 Eminem told Skyrock FM, "It's one of those tracks that I felt like only she could pull it off."19 Rosenberg sent the track to Rihanna, who accepted Eminem's request "at the last moment."20 Eminem then wrote the rapped verses.21

Rihanna said she joined the collaboration because she could relate to the theme of the song, as she and Eminem had been in difficult relationships on "different ends of the table".22 Eminem had released the songs "'97 Bonnie & Clyde" (1998) and "Kim" (2000), in which he fantasizes respectively about murdering and verbally abusing his then-wife Kimberly Scott.23 They had divorced in 2001 and again in 2006 after a remarriage.24 In February 2009, Rihanna's relationship with Chris Brown, an American singer, had ended following his felony assault on her.25 Rihanna described "Love the Way You Lie" as unique, realistic and deep, saying it "broke down the cycle of domestic violence" because few people had insight on the topic.262728

Recording

Mike Strange recorded and mixed Eminem's vocals at Effigy Studios in Ferndale, Michigan.29 The sessions took two days.30 Strange edited the vocals with D-Verb<U+2014>a reverberation software<U+2014>and an Extra Long Delay plug-in.31 He preferred to make few changes; for "Love the Way You Lie" he almost exclusively used board compression and console equalization. Strange used the Bricasti and Eventide Reverb 2016 mixing tools for "brighter" reverberation.32 Detroit musician Luis Resto has contributed to the arrangement on many of Eminem's songs, but did not do so for "Love the Way You Lie".33 According to Strange, "Everything we needed was already in the track, apart from the vocals."34 Two to three weeks after the sessions, Alex da Kid arrived to help mix and master the track.35 He wanted to replace the acoustic guitar on the demo, but Eminem chose to keep it.36 Strange said, "It was simply a matter of trying to match and then to improve on the demo [Alex da Kid]'d sent us."37 Strange's brother Joe engineered the track.38

Rihanna's recording sessions took place at Sun Studios in Dublin, Ireland. Marcos Tovar mixed her vocals, which were engineered by Spike Lindsey and mastered from Detroit over the Internet.3940 The American songwriter Makeba Riddick provided additional vocal production.41 Strange used equalization, compression and reverberation but left the vocal balance.42 He assumed that Rihanna was satisfied with her recorded vocals and did not make major changes on her seven stereo vocal tracks.43

Composition

"Love the Way You Lie" is a midtempo hip hop ballad.444546 The lyrics describe a couple's refusal to separate despite having an abusive relationship.47 According to the sheet music from Universal Music Publishing Group, the song is in the key of G minor with a common time signature and a tempo of 84 beats per minute.48 The vocal range spans from B3 to D5.49 "Love the Way You Lie" opens as Rihanna sings the pop refrain over a piano:5051

Just gonna stand there and watch me burn
But that's alright, because I like the way it hurts.
Just gonna stand there and hear me cry
But that's alright, because I love the way you lie.52

The refrain is based on a Gm<U+2013>E(add2)<U+2013>B<U+2013>F/A chord progression.53 Fraser McAlphine of the BBC wrote that Rihanna feels confined "in a cell of [her] own creation".54 She sings without vibrato,55 a pulsating musical effect used to add expression.56 One commentator, The New York Times{{'}} David Brownie, wrote that despite this, her voice subtly shows grief and regret.57 Eminem then "zap[s] out into a ballady rap":58 "I can't tell you what it really is/ I can only tell you what it feels like."59 The verses follow a Gm<U+2013>E(add2)B<U+2013>Fsus/A chord progression.60 Eminem regrets an end to an abusive, failed relationship, describing mutual violence and expressing both fondness and anger.6162 The two artists' characters are portrayed in a romantic relationship when he responds to Rihanna by concluding the verse: "I laid hands on her. I'll never stoop so low again/ I guess I don't know my own strength."63 Rihanna sings the chorus again, backed by an electric guitar and a piano.6465

Acoustic guitar, violin and drums accompany Eminem's verses.6667 The lyrics transition from discussing positive aspects of love to describing violent events.68 Eminem raps, "It's the rage that took over, it controls you both, so they say it's best to go your separate ways. Guess that they don't know ya, cause today that was yesterday."69 Sady Doyle of The Atlantic interpreted these lyrics as the rapper's confession to having abused Scott, as he "turns the anger and accusations toward himself".70 In the second verse, Eminem accepts the outcome after feeling sorry, as he admits, "Yesterday is over, it's a different day."71 His frustration increases and he raps that two personalities can clash and devastate: "maybe that's what happens when a tornado meets a volcano".7273 The described love<U+2013>hate relationship worsens and leads to domestic violence.74 Eminem admits to lying about promises he makes and says late in the song,75 "If she ever tries to fuckin' leave again, I'ma tie her to the bed and set this house on fire",76 a reference to Rihanna's lyrics.77

Release and response

On May 27, 2010, Eminem revealed the title of "Love the Way You Lie" as part of the tracklist of his seventh studio album Recovery,78 which came out on June 21, 2010.79 Initially a radio single,80 the song was released later by Polydor Records as a CD in the United Kingdom on August 9, 2010.81 Interscope Records distributed it in Germany on August 20.82

"Love the Way You Lie" received generally favorable reviews. An editor for Rap-Up listed it among the four best tracks from the album.83 The New York Times writer Jon Caramanica considered it "one of the album's most engaging songs" and praised Eminem's ability to assess issues from the perspectives of both sexes.84 MTV's Rodriguez wrote that the song echoes content from Eminem's earlier albums, but finds him "sober" and "more mature".85 He added that the lyrics allude to Eminem's relationship with Scott and that "Love the Way You Lie" is his closest approach to a love song.86 Editors of The A.V. Club compared the single with "Kim" and {{" '}}97 Bonnie & Clyde"; they found he is more tempered in "Love the Way You Lie" when discussing a "mutually destructive relationship" and that Rihanna's involvement creates an eerie mood.87

Critics especially praised Rihanna's contribution. Michael Menachem of Billboard complimented her "exquisitely melodic and surprisingly hopeful" vocal performance and "Eminem's dark, introspective" rapping.88 He noted that the percussion compliments both artists and that Alex da Kid gave the classical arrangement a mainstream touch.89 Kyle Anderson, writing for MTV, wrote that the song showcases Rihanna's emotional vocals and Eminem's "most intense rhymes about his tumultuous relationships".90 He regarded "Love the Way You Lie" as an honest, "well-constructed pop song with a killer hook" and likened the "slow-burning" music to that of "Someone Like You", a 2011 single by British singer Adele.9192 The Houston Chronicle pop critic Joey Guerra commented that Rihanna brought a "sandpaper and silk sheen" to the track.93

Themes

Critics and experts have commented on the message in the lyrics. Anderson noted a dark theme,94 while Nick Levine of Digital Spy, Allie Townsend of Time magazine, and Jocelyn Noveck of The Associated Press suggested the previous relationships of both artists influenced the song.959697 Levine gave it four stars out of five.98 According to Eric Hayden of The Atlantic Wire, the song refers to an alcoholic couple, possibly inspired by Rihanna's relationship with Brown.99 McAlphine awarded "Love the Way You Lie" four stars out of five.100 According to him, the song would not have been effective if it had featured only Eminem's point of view and feelings of regret; he wrote that Rihanna's representing the opposite side of the relationship shows "proper storytelling" and "sends a message" because of her breakup with Brown.101 McAlphine wrote that as a result the song depicts a more realistic abusive relationship and could be featured in a campaign for women's shelters. He praised Eminem's accuracy and understanding of the topic.102 Winston Robbins from the music website Consequence of Sound attributed the song's poignancy to Rihanna's past abusive relationship, and a theme of infidelity and abuse.103

On the critical side, The Daily Telegraph's Jenny McCartney dismissed the metaphors in the chorus and thought that the song's topic was over-hyped.104 She disagreed with the critical praise, arguing that women accept abusive relationships for deeper reasons than sexual and emotional pleasure, such as family and financial issues and helplessness.105 In an NPR article, the writer Maura Johnston commented that the lyrics portray Rihanna as the subject of Eminem's violence.106 Jay Smooth, a New York radio personality, responded that "while Eminem explores the psyche of the abuser with an almost disturbing amount of depth and detail," Rihanna's perspective is downplayed and not explained fully.107 Johnston and Smooth wrote that such imbalances are a prevalent issue in popular music duets.108 To Sady Doyle, although "Love the Way You Lie" is one of Eminem's most affecting tracks and finds him remorseful, it does not make up for his past misogynistic actions and hateful songs.109

Noveck questioned whether the lyrics are "a treatise against (or apology for) domestic violence, or an irresponsible glorification of it? Or, is it something uncomfortable in between?"110 Marjorie Gilberg, executive director of the anti-teenage violence group Break the Cycle, commented that "Love the Way You Lie" can teach listeners about the dangers of abusive relationships if interpreted correctly. She believed that because popular culture often depicts what is socially acceptable, people may accept such violence more easily. She added, "One problem, though, ... is that the song reflects myths about domestic violence<U+2014>myths that lead to blaming the victim."111 Gilberg said the victim is often accused of being as guilty as the partner but wants to be loved, not abused.112

Recognition

"Love the Way You Lie" ranked in various best-of-2010 lists. Claire Suddath of Time placed it at number five on her top-ten list and wrote, "That this song didn't come across sounding clich<U+00E9>d or tasteless is a testament to both artists' skill."113 AOL Radio considered the song the best of 2010 hip hop music, regarding Eminem as imaginative and passionate.114 It ranked ninth on MTV News' top-twenty-five list.115 Its editor James Montgomery attributed the song's success to its theme and relation to the artists' past abusive relationships.116 He concluded, "You cannot write a song any better than that, because that's how this kind of thing happens in real life."117 The New York Post placed the song at second place on their "top 210 songs from 2010" list and regarded it as a "comeback club anthem."118 In April 2011, Gabriel Alvarez of Complex magazine ranked it at number 100 on their "100 Best Eminem Songs" list, calling it a love song and praising Rihanna's vocals as beautiful.119 "Love the Way You Lie" and its accompanying music video were nominated for five Grammy Awards at the 2011 ceremony, including "Record of the Year", "Song of the Year" and "Best Short Form Music Video".120 It won the People's Choice Awards for "Favorite Music Video" and "Favorite Song".121

Commercial performance

"Love the Way You Lie" reached number one on several record charts worldwide.122 It entered the US Billboard Hot 100 at number two in the issue dated July 10, 2010.123 The single debuted at number one on the US Digital Songs after selling 338,000 digital copies in its first week.124 From July 31 to September 11, 2010, it had a seven-week run at the top of the Hot 100,125 giving Eminem his fourth, and Rihanna her seventh, number-one US hit.126 The single sold more than 300,000 digital copies in the week of August 14, 2010, rising to number two on the Pop Songs and Radio Songs charts.127 In the Billboard issue for August 21, 2010, Nielsen Broadcast Data Systems recognized the single with the BDS Certified Award for 50,000 radio spins.128 It topped the Pop Songs chart in the same issue,129 giving Eminem his third number-one track on the chart and Rihanna her sixth.130 "Love the Way You Lie" was the first number-one hit on the Rap Songs chart with Eminem as the lead artist since his 2000 single "The Real Slim Shady".131

The song entered the Australian Singles Chart on July 4, 2010, at number fourteen.132 It rose to the top three weeks later, occupying the position for six weeks and staying on the chart for thirty-eight weeks in total.133 In Austria, "Love the Way You Lie" made its debut at number thirty-one, on July 2, 2010, and ascended to number one on September 4.134 After making a final consecutive appearance on the chart on March 4, 2011, it reappeared at number 75 on August 26. The song spent forty-seven weeks on the chart.135 "Love the Way You Lie" stayed on France's singles chart for twenty-three weeks.136 Although it never reached the top there, the single's debut and peak position was number three, at which it appeared on July 2, 2010.137

By the end of the year, 854,000 copies were sold in the UK, making "Love the Way You Lie" the country's biggest-selling song of 2010.138 In October 2011, it became the 109th song to reach one million sales there.139 By November 2012, it had sold 1.05 million copies in the UK, placing at number 100 on the Official Charts Company's "The Million Sellers" list.140 With sales of 4,245,000 copies, "Love the Way You Lie" was the third-best-selling single of 2010 in the US.141 It was South Korea's third-best-selling song in 2010 by a foreign artist, with 1,200,653 downloads.142 According to the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry, it sold 9.3 million copies worldwide in 2010.143 In December 2011, "Love the Way You Lie" was certified as Eminem's best-selling single.144 By July 2013, it had sold 5,939,000 copies in the US.145

Music video

Production

thumb|upright=0.6|The actress Megan Fox, according to the director Joseph Kahn, helped the video have a strong impact.|alt=A shot of Megan Fox from the chest up, with dark brown hair. The music video for "Love the Way You Lie" was Eminem's third to be directed by the American filmmaker Joseph Kahn.146 Despite having planned to shoot a feature film and stop directing pop videos, Kahn chose to work on "Love the Way You Lie" because, according to him, the song discusses an important topic.147 This was an opportunity for him to direct a video for Eminem without the comedic themes of their previous collaborations.148 For the video, Kahn considered the English actor Dominic Monaghan as a co-star for his versatility, which he thought would help him play an antagonist.149 Kahn cast the American actress Megan Fox, which he had presumed would be almost impossible because of her popularity with directors.150 Fox, a fan of Eminem, accepted the role unhesitatingly.151

Rosenberg gave Kahn one day to write a treatment, which Kahn finished in forty-five minutes.152 He filmed Eminem's and Rihanna's parts on July 20, 2010;153 scenes with Fox and Monaghan were shot three days later.154 By July 24, 2010, they had finished the shoot.155 Kahn confirmed the completion of the director's cut the next day.156 He said Fox's involvement made the video powerful,157 commenting for Vibe:

We wanted to make a specific story about two people<U+2014>Meg and Dom<U+2014>not a video that was representative of all couples or all domestic violence situations. I'm not saying that all couples fight this way. I just want people just to be able to identify with the characters and recognize that they've seen relationships like this where two people are together that are completely wrong for each other and things spiral out of control. ... Megan was the key to this video. ... I'll tell you as a director the reason why [Fox and Monaghan's] scenes in the video feel so real is because in the moment they were real.158

Monaghan told MTV he thought the couple represents Eminem and Scott.159 The clip was produced by Kathy Angstadt and Maryann Tanedo of HSI Productions.160 Shortly before its release, Eminem reported in a press statement: "Joseph and I worked pretty closely together to make sure we got this right".161 He said the difficult topic resulted in a powerful video because of the contributions of Rihanna, Fox and Monaghan.162 Fox donated her appearance fee to the Soujourn House, a battered women's shelter.163 On August 5, 2010, the video premiered on MTV and the music video website Vevo.164165

Synopsis

Rihanna sings the chorus in front of a burning house, while an interspersing scene shows fire on a woman's (Fox) palms. In another scene, the woman is asleep with her lover (Monaghan) and wakes up. Eminem begins to rap in an open field. The woman attacks her lover after seeing the name "Cindy" and a phone number written on his hand.167 He unsuccessfully tries to kiss the woman and brings her back after she attempts to leave him. The man then pushes her onto the wall and aims his fist at her, puncturing the wall instead. After Rihanna sings again, the video flashes back to when the couple first meet "at a seedy dive bar next door" to a liquor store.168169 The man steals a bottle of vodka and they kiss on the rooftop.170

During the present, the man apologizes to his lover and they are reconciled. In another flashback, he attacks someone who plays pool with his lover.171 Eminem joins Rihanna in front of the burning house for the final verse. Meanwhile, after the flashback, the woman comes home and locks herself in the bathroom to stop her abusive lover from entering. In another scene, the fire on her palms vanish as she clasps her hands. Flames later engulf Eminem and the couple,172 who are then shown fighting in front of the burning house. In the end, the couple stay together and the video returns to their first scene, in which they sleep.173174 {{-}}

Reception

The clip broke what was a YouTube record at the time of its release for the most hits in one day, with 6.6 million,175 and is the site's fourth-most viewed video.176 It had a mixed reception from critics, most of whom commented on scenes of domestic violence.177 NPR's Zoe Chace deemed the video sickening,178 while AOL Music listed it at number five on their list of the "Top 10 Most Controversial Music Videos in Pop".179 Stephanie Nilva<U+2014>the executive director of trauma resource center Day One<U+2014>told MTV News that it mainly raises "the topic of dating violence among young people".180 Nilva praised the clip's accurate depiction of patterns in an abusive relationship and thought the video's potency came from Eminem's history of violence-themed songs and Brown's assault on Rihanna.181

A writer for Rap-Up considered the video realistic, as "art imitates life".182 Conor Friedersdorf of The Atlantic called it an inaccurate portrayal of domestic violence due to insufficient violence, as evidenced by the lack of injuries after punching through a wall.183 Mariel Concepcion of Billboard suggested the video was inspired by the lyric,184 "Just gonna stand there and watch me burn, but that's alright because I like the way it hurts."185 Rihanna's appearance was criticized. Writing for Rolling Stone magazine, Daniel Kreps called it "especially striking" and noted her relationship with Brown.186 An editor from The Boston Globe commented that although the video is realistic, it hinders Rihanna's ability to set an example for women in abusive relationships.187 President Terry O'Neill of the National Organization for Women said the singer unintentionally glorifies domestic violence.188

Brittany Talarico of OK! magazine saw the video's message as significant to both artists.189 Billy Johnson Jr. from Yahoo! Music wrote that Fox's character alternated between vulnerable and confrontational personalities, while Monaghan's character is similar to Eminem.190 Kahn, who understood why viewers thought Eminem's and Rihanna's relationships influenced the video, asked that they realize this is untrue. He said the team had been conscious of such personal matters and Fox's and Monaghan's characters are only significant to each other.191

Reviewers discussed the acting. Peter Gicas of E! News noted that Fox and Monaghan portray "combustible behavior the tune is hellbent on describing."192 The New York Post{{'}}s Jarett Wieselman wrote that they play their roles perfectly and help make the video powerful.193 Entertainment Weekly{{'}}s Whitney Pastorek found the violent acting sexually appealing,194 while Willa Paskin of New York magazine wrote that the cast's appeal "hypnotize[s]" viewers.195 Paskin noted the use of sepia toning in the burning-house scenes.196 Matthew Wilkening of AOL Radio commented that the video leaves viewers to decide "if it's a good thing or a bad thing that the pair always end up reconciling."197 Mikey Fresh of Vibe commented that Fox's natural reaction when Monaghan pierces the wall and almost hits her is an "incredible act of vulnerability".198 L Magazine{{'}}s Benjamin Sutton likened the scene in which the characters burn to Chris Bradley, whom Monaghan portrayed in the 2009 action film X-Men Origins: Wolverine.199 The Daily Mail wrote that the fire is symbolically important in the video, as it consumes Fox's character.200

Live performances

On June 15, 2010, Eminem and Rihanna performed "Love the Way You Lie" at Los Angeles' Electronic Entertainment Expo 2010, accompanied by the drummer Travis Barker and D12's Mr. Porter.201 Eminem sang "Love the Way You Lie" at the Scottish festival T in the Park on July 10, 2010, dedicating it to "everybody who'[d] been in a fucked-up relationship."202 He then performed the song at Ireland's annual Oxegen festival on July 11, 2010.203 James Hendicott from State wrote that the rapper's personality "fill[ed] the stage" and that his vocals were sharp and packed "plenty of punch".204 He criticized the use of a pre-recorded backing track and noted the lack of live music and female vocals.205 Eamon Sweeney from the Irish Independent called Eminem's performance only "mildly impressive".206

On July 21, 2010, the rapper joined Rihanna on her Last Girl on Earth Tour to perform at the Staples Center in Los Angeles.207 He then performed at the 2010 MTV Video Music Awards and was voted the best performer of the ceremony in an MTV poll, earning 34 percent of votes.208209 Rihanna made a surprise appearance despite having said she could not perform because of her schedule.210 Suddath of Time called their duet dull.211 Eminem sang "Love the Way You Lie" without Rihanna at the 2011 Bonnaroo Music Festival for almost 80,000 people.212213 According to MTV News' Montgomery, the "sheer tenacity with which he attacked" was his strength.214 Patrick Doyle of Rolling Stone felt Eminem delivered a triumphal act by "constantly bouncing across the stage" and singing energetically.215

Eminem performed "Love the Way You Lie" on the second day of Chicago's annual Lollapalooza festival on August 6, 2011.216 Piet Levy of USA Today named the show a "tragic stunner", as members of the audience sang the chorus to compensate for Rihanna's absence.217218 Katie Hasty of HitFix added that it displayed competition between the sexes.219 On August 20, 2011, Eminem and Rihanna performed in Staffordshire on the first day of V2011 (V Festival) .220 James Lachno of The Daily Telegraph considered it an "affecting" rendition.221 The Guardian called their performance thrilling and an editor for the British newspaper Metro felt that it was the best part of the evening.222223 Eminem closed the festival on the night of August 21 in Chelmsford, Essex, for an audience of 120,000.224 A reporter for the International Business Times thought that Eminem's duet with Rihanna was the highlight of the show.225

Cover versions

Multiple musical acts have performed cover versions of "Love the Way You Lie". The country music group The Band Perry sang the song at the June 2010 CMT Music Awards.226 Two months later, Taylor Momsen<U+2014>the lead singer of the American rock band The Pretty Reckless<U+2014>performed a cover version for BBC Radio 1's Live Lounge as part of a mashup with the song "Islands" by the English pop band The xx.227 Assisted by a guitarist from her band, she began with a section of "Islands" and transitioned into the chorus of "Love the Way You Lie".228

Cher Lloyd, a British singer, performed the song in the final five of The X Factor UK{{'}}s seventh season.229230 A writer from The Sun called the performance "stripped-back" and "without any of her usual stage gimmicks."231 Mernie Gilmore of the Daily Express commented that the song is "a duet for a reason" as Lloyd performed both Eminem's and Rihanna's parts.232 In 2010, the Russian guitarist Alex Feather Akimov, released "Love The Way You Lie (Heavy Remix)", a recording that was recognized by Billboard.biz (Web Trends).233 The American post-hardcore band A Skylit Drive recorded a cover of the single for Punk Goes Pop 4, the 2011 release of the Punk Goes... series.234

Sequel

On November 3, 2010, an alternative version titled "Love the Way You Lie (Part II)" leaked onto the Internet.235 Rihanna is the lead vocalist and Eminem is a featured guest. The song mainly views matters from Rihanna's perspective and is based on Grey's demo.236237 Rihanna agreed to record a sequel after initially thinking "the original couldn't be beaten".238 She said that the sequel involved less production, with only piano and drums.239 It is the eleventh and final track on her 2010 studio album Loud.240 Grey's version appears on her 2012 extended play The Buried Sessions of Skylar Grey and her 2013 studio album Don't Look Down.241242

Rihanna performed a short medley that comprised "Love the Way You Lie (Part II)", "What's My Name?" and "Only Girl (In the World)" at the American Music Awards of 2010.243 Another medley, consisting of "Love the Way You Lie (Part II)" and "I Need a Doctor", was performed at the 2011 Grammy Awards.244 While the Chicago Sun-Times considered the track an unnecessary sequel,245 the BBC reviewer James Skinner wrote, {{" '}}Love the Way You Lie (Part II)' even bests the original, Eminem's verse exuding the kind of volatile, simmering menace that got everyone so excited about him in the first place. But it is Rihanna's vocal<U+2014>at once commanding, soulful and vulnerable<U+2014>that anchors the song".246
