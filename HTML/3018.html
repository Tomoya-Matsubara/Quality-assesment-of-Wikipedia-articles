A Bacon Explosion is a pork dish that consists of bacon wrapped around a filling of spiced sausage and crumbled bacon. The American-football-sized dish is smoked or baked. It caused an Internet sensation after being posted on the BBQ Addicts blog,12 and quickly spread to the mainstream press with numerous stories discussing the dish.3 In time, the articles began to discuss the Internet "buzz" itself.4 The phenomenon is international, with popularity in the United States and the United Kingdom.

The Bacon Explosion is made of bacon, sausage, barbeque sauce and barbeque seasoning or rub. The bacon is assembled in a weave to hold the sausage, sauce and crumbled bacon. Once rolled, the Bacon Explosion is cooked, basted, cut and served. The Bacon Explosion's creators produced a cookbook featuring the recipes which ultimately won the 2010 Gourmand World Cookbook Awards for "Best Barbecue Book in the World". The Bacon Explosion also won at the 2013 Blue Ribbon Bacon Festival.

History and origin

Jason Day and Aaron Chronister posted the dish in December 2008 on their "BBQ Addicts" blog.5 It quickly became an Internet phenomenon, generating more than 500,000 hits and 16,000 links to the blog, and was even included on political blogs because "Republicans like meat."67 There are fan clubs and follow-up videos of various attempts to create the dish.8

The inventors are experienced barbecue competition participants from Kansas City, and compete in cook-offs as the Burnt Finger BBQ team.9 According to the Telegraph, "They came up with the delicacy after being challenged on Twitter to create the ultimate bacon recipe."10 They christened their innovation the "Bacon Explosion: The BBQ Sausage Recipe of all Recipes."11 The Bacon Explosion is similar to a number of previously published recipes. Day and Chronister do not claim to have invented the concept,12 but assert the term "Bacon Explosion" as a trademark.13

Preparation

Preparing a Bacon Explosion "requires the minimum of culinary talent" and the ingredient list is short.14 It is made from of thick cut bacon, 2 pounds of Italian sausage, one jar of barbecue sauce, and one jar of barbecue rub/seasoning.15 The Bacon Explosion is constructed by weaving the bacon together to serve as a base. The base is seasoned and followed by the layering of sausage meat and crumbled bacon. Barbecue sauce is added and more seasoning is added, before rolling it into a giant "sausage-shaped monster."16 It takes about an hour per inch of thickness to cook and is then basted with more barbecue sauce, sliced into rounds, and served.17 A prepared Bacon Explosion contains at least and 500 grams of fat,18 though a smaller 8-ounce portion contains 878 calories and 60 grams of fat.19

Recognition

The popularity of the recipe has led to international coverage;20 including the US and the UK, German and Dutch media.2122 The Daily Telegraph assessed that the "recipe is most popular on the web" and that the "5,000 calorie barbeque dish has become one of the most popular meal ideas in the world."23 Commentary in major publications about the health/obesity of Americans quickly suggested dishes like Bacon Explosion as the reason for "Why Americans are fat", while another asserted that it isn't something a doctor would recommend.2425 It has also been cited as an example of the use of Web 2.0 technology (Chronister is an Internet marketer).26

Day and Chronister were reported to have "landed a six-figure book deal" for their book Barbecue Makes Everything Better.27 The book, containing the recipe, became the USA winner in the 2010 Gourmand World Cookbook Awards in the "Best Barbecue Book" category.28 The 2010 USA winner The Essential New York Times Cookbook: Classic Recipes for a New Century by Amanda Hesser also contained the recipe for the Bacon Explosion.29 BBQ Makes Everything Better went on to win the "Best Barbecue Book in the World" category by the judges of the 2010 Gourmand World Cookbook Awards, and remained as the sole entry from an American.30 The Bacon Explosion won "Savory Dish" at the 2013 Blue Ribbon Bacon Festival which secured an entry in the Bacon World Championships.3132
