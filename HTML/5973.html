Veturilo is a public bicycle sharing system in Warsaw, Poland, launched on 1 August 2012 with 55 stations and 1000 bicycles in 3 districts - <U+015A>r<U+00F3>dmie<U+015B>cie (city centre), Bielany and Ursyn<U+00F3>w,1 2 other stations started operating in the middle of August in Wilan<U+00F3>w.2 It is the biggest public bicycle sharing system in Poland.3

Name

The name Veturilo was chosen in an internet contest. It is an Esperanto word meaning vehicle. Internet users submitted nearly a thousand proposals, and a jury chose a short-list of six. In the next step, during an internet vote, users chose the name Veturilo (with 32% of votes); other proposals were: Wawabike (26%), Ziuuu (12%), Rowerynka (12%), Bajker (11%) and Wabik (6%).4

System

Bicycles are available for 9 months of the year, from 1 March to 30 November. In order to use the system, users need to register and pay a 10 z<U+0142> initial fee. The first 20 minutes of the ride are free of charge, longer rides cost at least 1 z<U+0142>. A bicycle can be used for up to 12 hours, there is a 200 z<U+0142> fee for exceeding this limit, and after 13 hours the operator can report the bicycle as stolen to the police. The fee for the theft, loss or destruction of a bicycle is 2000 z<U+0142>.5
