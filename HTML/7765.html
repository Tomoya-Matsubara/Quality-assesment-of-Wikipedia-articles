Pomeranian Voivodeship (Polish: wojew<U+00F3>dztwo pomorskie) should preferably be translated as the Pomerania province, as it is a voivodeship (administrative region) in northern Poland within the historic region of Eastern Pomerania. Capital city: Gda<U+0144>sk.

It was established January 1, 1999, out of the former voivodeships of Gda<U+0144>sk, Elbl<U+0105>g and S<U+0142>upsk, pursuant to the Local Government Reorganization Act of 1998. The province's name recalls the region's traditional name, Pomerania.

The area borders, to the west, on the West Pomeranian Voivodeship, and extends eastward along the Baltic Sea.

Gda<U+0144>sk, in the center of the province, forms part of the Tricity of Sopot, Gda<U+0144>sk and Gdynia. Also part of the province is the narrow Hel Peninsula. Well-known tourist destinations include Sopot, Jurata, <U+0141>eba, W<U+0142>adys<U+0142>awowo, Puck, Krynica Morska, Ustka, Jastarnia, Rozewie and Ku<U+017A>nica, with many fishing ports and lighthouses.

Previous Pomeranian Voivodeships

Pomeranian Voivodeship (1294-1308)

Pomeranian Voivodeship was a province of the Kingdom of Poland. After the extinction of the local dukes in 1294, the province fell to Przemys<U+0142> II of Poland.

Pomeranian Voivodeship (1466-1772)

This was a unit of administrative division and local government in the Kingdom of Poland from 1454/1466 until the partitions in 1772/1795. Together with the Che<U+0142>mno Voivodeship and Malbork Voivodeship it formed the historical province of Royal Prussia. The capital was at Gda<U+0144>sk. It was renamed to the Prussian province of West Prussia (1772-1919).

Pomeranian Voivodeship (1919-1939)

This was a unit of administration and local government in the Republic of Poland (II Rzeczpospolita) established in 1919 after World War I from the majority of the Prussian province of West Prussia which fell to Poland. Toru<U+0144> was the capital. In 1938-39 the voivodeship extended to the south at the expense of Pozna<U+0144> Voivodeship and Warsaw Voivodeship, and was called Great Pomerania afterwards (see: Territorial changes of Polish Voivodeships on April 1, 1938).

During WWII it was occupied by Nazi Germany and annexed as Reichsgau Danzig-Westpreussen "(Reich province of Gda<U+0144>sk-West Prussia)." In 1945 it was returned to Poland and superseded by Gda<U+0144>sk and Bydgoszcz voivodeships. In the years 1975-98 it was reorganized into the voivodeships of Gda<U+0144>sk, Elbl<U+0105>g, Bydgoszcz, Toru<U+0144> and W<U+0142>oc<U+0142>awek.

Pomeranian Voivodeship (1945-1950)

This was a unit of administration and local government in Poland established in 1945 from most of the pre-war Pomeranian Voivodeship, later renamed Bydgoszcz Voivodeship.

Capital city: Bydgoszcz
