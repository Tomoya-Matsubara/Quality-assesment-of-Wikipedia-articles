August Ferdinand M<U+00F6>bius (November 17, 1790 - September 26, 1868, (IPA: ) was a German mathematician and theoretical astronomer. He was born in Schulpforta, Saxony, Germany and died in Leipzig.

He is best known for his discovery of the M<U+00F6>bius strip, a non-orientable two-dimensional surface with only one side when embedded in three-dimensional Euclidean space. It was independently discovered by Johann Benedict Listing around the same time. M<U+00F6>bius was the first to introduce homogeneous coordinates into projective geometry. M<U+00F6>bius transformations, important in projective geometry, are not to be confused with the M<U+00F6>bius transform of number theory, which also bears his name. His interest was also in number theory, and the important M<U+00F6>bius function <U+03BC>(n) and the M<U+00F6>bius inversion formula are named after him.

M<U+00F6>bius studied under Carl Friedrich Gauss and was a professor at the University of Leipzig.

Trivia

  • M<U+00F6>bius was a descendant of Martin Luther by way of his mother.
  • The character of Johann Wilhelm M<U+00F6>bius - a particular scientist who tries to evade his own inventions by pretending to be insane - in D<U+00FC>rrenmatt's satiric drama The Physicists is named after him.
  • The asteroid 28516 M<U+00F6>bius is also named after him.
  • There is a tribute to him in Serious Sam: The Second Encounter, in the form on the deathmatch map dm_mobius (which has since spawned various spin-offs such as in the Unreal Tournament series).
  • He also plays a pivotal part in the ''Necroscope ''book series.
