The Irish Republican Liberation Army (IRLA) is the most new incarnation of the various Irish paramilitary organisations with aims to bring about a thirty-two county United Ireland. Although much of its origins are as of yet unknown, the IRLA was brought attention to itself with its threats of action.

Origins

The IRLA is centred around West Belfast in the Ardoyne area.

Threats

In November 2007, the IRLA threatened Sinn Fein members who were involved in District Policing Partnerships. Further to these threats the IRLA were also involved in criminal activity according to the Independent Monitoring Commission. The IMC also said the IRLA has a small arms collection, though it's threat is not "terrorist in nature."1

According to the Hansards, on March 5, 2008, Sammy Wilson asked the Secretary of State for Northern Ireland McNulty if would consider prohibiting the Irish Republican Liberation Army under the Terrorism Act 2000. McNulty gave a vague response that "As a matter of normal policy and practice we do not comment on organisations not on the proscribed list."2
