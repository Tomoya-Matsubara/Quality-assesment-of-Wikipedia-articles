Soddo (autonym k<U+0259>stane "Christian"; formerly called Aym<U+00E4>ll<U+00E4>l in Western sources, after a particular dialect of it) is a Gurage language spoken by about 300,000 people in southeastern Ethiopia. It is a South Ethiopian Semitic language of the Northern Gurage subfamily.

Grammar

Noun

As in most Ethiopian languages, noun qualifiers generally precede the noun.

The definite article is expressed by the suffix -i, eg: go<U+0161> "boy" > go<U+0161>-i "the boy"; <U+00E4>tit "sister" > <U+00E4>titi "the sister"; bayyo<U+010D><U+010D> "children" > bayyo<U+010D><U+010D>-i. If the noun ends in -a or -<U+00E4>, it normally loses this vowel when -i is suffixed: anga<U+010D><U+010D>a "cat" > anga<U+010D><U+010D>-i "the cat". A noun ending in -i usually stays the same: abi "(the) father, proprietor". A noun ending in -e, -o, -u adds a y before the suffix: ge "house" > ge<U+02B8>i "the house"; w<U+00E4>ll<U+00E4>ho "neighbor" > w<U+00E4>ll<U+00E4>ho<U+02B8>i "the neighbor". If the noun has a qualifier, the article is used with the first element: mal<U+00E4>k' ge "big house" > mal<U+00E4>k'-i ge "the big house"; y<U+00E4>-<U+0161>um-i ge "the house of the official" (lit. "of-official-the house"); y<U+00E4>-m<U+00E4>t't'-i m<U+0259>ss "the man who came" (lit. "who-came-the man".)

There is no real indefinite article, though indefiniteness can be expressed by preposing the word att<U+0259> or k'una, meaning "one".

Nouns have two genders, masculine and feminine, which affect verb concord.

Nouns which are definite objects (direct or indirect) are both marked with the prefix y<U+00E4>- or n<U+00E4>-: eg y<U+00E4>-ge<U+02B8>i a<U+017E><U+017E>o "he saw the house"; y<U+00E4>-z<U+00E4>mmih<U+02B7>an ab<U+00E4>nn<U+0259>t "he gave it to his brother" (lit. "to-his-brother he-gave-him"). Direct objects may additionally be marked by adding the object suffix pronouns to the verb: eg yabiddi t<U+00E4><U+0161>akkunn<U+0259>t "I asked my father" (lit. "my-father-obj. I-asked-him".)

A possessed noun is marked by the prefix y<U+00E4>-, and the possessor precedes the possessed: y<U+00E4>-<U+0161>um-i ge "the house of the official" (lit. "of-official-the house"). If the possessed noun has a preposition prefixed to it, this y<U+00E4>- is omitted: babiddi f<U+00E4>r<U+00E4>z rather than *b<U+00E4>-y<U+00E4>-abiddi f<U+00E4>r<U+00E4>z for "on my father's horse".

Possessives can also be formed by simply adding y<U+00E4>- to the standalone pronouns, eg: y<U+00E4>d<U+00E4>h<U+0259>m t'<U+0259>b "your clan".

Reflexive pronouns are formed by <U+00E4>ras-, gubba-, k'um- plus the possessive suffixes, eg <U+00E4>di <U+00E4>ras-<U+0259>ddi m<U+00E4>t'afi t'afkunn<U+0259>t "I myself wrote the book".

Demonstrative pronoun

Proximal: zi "this, these"; zini "this one". Eg: zi m<U+0259>ss "this man", zi m<U+0259><U+0161>t "this woman", zi s<U+00E4>bo<U+010D><U+010D> "these men".

Distal: za "that, those, that one, those ones"; zani "that one there". Eg t<U+00E4>-za m<U+0259>ss goy m<U+00E4>t't'ahi "I came with that man".

Interrogative pronoun

  • ma "who?" (man before the copula): man m<U+00E4>t't'a? "who came?"
  • y<U+00E4>ma "whose?"
  • m<U+0259>n "what?"; y<U+00E4>m<U+0259>n "why?"
  • yitta, yittat "which?" Eg yitta bayy m<U+00E4>t't'am "which child came?"
  • yittani "which one?"

Indefinite pronoun

  • (y<U+00E4>hon<U+00E4>) s<U+00E4>b "someone, somebody"
  • mann<U+0259>m (s<U+00E4>b) "any(one)" ("no one" with negative verb)
  • att<U+0259>m "any" (="no one, nothing" with negative verb); att<U+0259>mu "no one" (as pronoun)
  • lela (s<U+00E4>b) "other"
  • y<U+00E4>k'irr<U+00E4> k'<U+00E4>y "other" (lit. "remaining thing")
  • att<U+0259> "a certain"
  • l<U+0259>yyu "different"
  • k'una, zam, z<U+0259><U+010D>'<U+0259> "same"
  • <U+00E4>b<U+00E4>lo (f. <U+00E4>b<U+00E4>lit) "so-and-so"
  • zihom "such"

kull<U+0259>m = "all" (placed before or after the noun); kull<U+0259>m-u, b<U+00E4>-mollaw = "whole". y<U+00E4>t'oma = "only, alone". "Each, every" is expressed by noun reduplication.

In the past and future, it is expressed just like the copula, with n<U+00E4>bb<U+00E4>r<U+00E4> and hon<U+00E4>. In subordinate clauses the present is expressed with -all<U+00E4> conjugated in the perfect (negative -lell<U+00E4>), eg: b<U+00E4>meda yall<U+0259>mi s<U+00E4>bo<U+010D><U+010D> ara<U+0161> n<U+00E4>m "the people who are in the field are farmers".

The possessive verb "he has" etc. is expressed with the existential verb yino "it is" (agreeing with the object possessed) plus object suffix pronouns (ie "it is to him" etc.)

Verbs

A Soddo verb may have anywhere from one to four consonants, or may be a compound with balo "say" (eg b<U+0259>k'k' balo "appear".) In the former case, they fall into three "conjugations" differing in their vowels and in gemination of the imperfect, illustrated for a three-consonant verb:

  • s<U+00E4>bb<U+00E4>ro, imperfect y<U+0259>s<U+00E4>bru ("break")
  • tikk<U+00E4>lo, imperfect y<U+0259>tikk<U+0259>lu
  • <U+010D>'aff<U+00E4>ro, imperfect y<U+0259><U+010D>'aff<U+0259>ru

Derived stems can be formed in several ways:

  • reduplicative: eg g<U+00E4>dd<U+00E4>lo "kill" > g<U+0259>dadd<U+00E4>lo. This form has a wide variety of meanings, mostly intensifying the verb in some way.
  • passive/reflexive/intransitive t<U+00E4>- prefix: eg k<U+00E4>ff<U+00E4>lo "pay" > t<U+00E4>-k<U+00E4>ff<U+00E4>lo "be paid". A reciprocal action can be expressed by this prefix attached to a transitive verb with the vowel a after the first radical, or a reduplicative form, eg t<U+00E4>-g<U+00E4>dd<U+00E4>l-mun or t<U+00E4>-gdadd<U+00E4>l-mun "they killed each other".
  • causative or transitive of intransitive verbs a-: eg s<U+00E4>kk<U+00E4>ro "be drunk" > a-s<U+00E4>kk<U+00E4>ro "get someone drunk"; n<U+00E4>dd<U+00E4>do "burn (intr.)" > a-n<U+00E4>dd<U+00E4>do "burn (tr.)".
  • causative of transitive or passive verbs at- (+ -i-): eg k<U+00E4>dd<U+00E4>no "cover" > at-kidd<U+00E4>no "cause to cover" or "cause to be covered". Added to the -a- form, it expresses reciprocity and adjutative (helping): atgadd<U+00E4>lo "cause to kill one other" or help to kill".
  • Some verbs are formed with initial <U+0259>n- or t<U+00E4>n-; the only derived stem from these is the a- stem, with a- replacing <U+0259>- or t<U+00E4>-. Eg <U+0259>nkr<U+00E4>tt<U+00E4>to "be bent" > ankr<U+00E4>tt<U+00E4>to "bend".

There are two tenses, perfect (past) and imperfect (non-past); each has distinct forms for main versus subordinate clauses, and positive versus negative. There are also distinct jussive, imperative, and impersonal forms.

These are negated by the prefix ay-: ay<U+0259>sf<U+00E4>r, ay<U+0161><U+00E4>kk<U+0259>t, aygalb. The 2nd person forms then change to conform to the others: att<U+0259>sf<U+00E4>r, att<U+0259>sfer, att<U+0259>sf<U+00E4>r<U+0259>m, att<U+0259>sf<U+00E4>rma.

Eg: y<U+00E4>-w<U+00E4>z<U+00E4>lawan-hom yews<U+0259>d "let him take according to his work"; y<U+00E4>s<U+00E4>b waga att<U+0259>lg<U+00E4>d "don't touch someone's property"; <U+00E4>r<U+0259>f-<U+0259>m tona "rest and sit down" (sit down quietly).
