Corperryale "Manny" Harris (born September 21, 1989) is a sophomore guard for the Michigan Wolverines men's basketball team.1 Harris played high school basketball for Redford High School in where he was a two-time All-State first team selection and a Mr. Basketball of Michigan winner.23 He is a 2008–09 first team pre-season All-Big Ten player and both a 2007–08 second team pre-season and post season All-Big Ten Conference selection.4 He entered his sophomore season as the leading returning scorer in the Big Ten and as the conferences top rated NBA Draft prospect.5 He is the 2008–09 Big Ten Conference scoring average leader.6

High School

Born in , Harris was an Associated Press Class A All-State first team selection in both 2006 and 2007.7 Harris was selected as the 2007 Mr. Basketball of Michigan. That season he led the Redford Huskies to the Michigan High School Athletic Association Class A championship game, which it lost to Saginaw High School. During his high school basketball career, he became the first player to start for three consecutive Detroit Public School League Champions since Jalen Rose and Voshon Lenard.8 Harris once posted 52 points and 15 rebounds in a high school basketball game.9 During his 2006–07 senior season, he averaged 33.5 points, 11.5 rebounds and 5.5 assists.10 Harris was highly recruited receiving offers from the Tennessee Volunteers, UCLA Bruins, Wisconsin Badgers, George Washington Colonials, and Indiana Hoosiers men's basketball teams.11 He was ranked by Rivals.com as the #6 shooting guard in the country in the 2007 high school class.12 Scout.com ranked him as the twelfth best shooting guard.13 ESPN ranked him #7, but they listed him as a point guard.14 When Michigan announced that they were replacing Tommy Amaker with John Beilein as head coach, Harris was not sure if he would honor his signed letter of intent, but when Michigan re-signed assistant coach Mike Jackson, he decided to attend to the University of Michigan.15

College

Prior to the 2007–08 basketball season, he was selected as a second team pre-season All-Big Ten Conference selection.16 After the 2007–08 NCAA basketball season, Harris was selected as a second team All-Big Ten Conference selection and placed second to Eric Gordon in the Freshman of the Year balloting, which earned him an All-Freshman team selection. He led the Wolverines in scoring (16.4), assists (2.8), steals (1.5), minutes (32.9) and free throw percentage (82.0).17 Among his highlights during the season were being named to the Great Alaska Shootout tournament team, becoming the fourth U-M freshman to score over 500 points in first season (516), and setting the U-M freshman record for free throws made in a season while playing at least 20 minutes and starting every game.18

As a 2008–09 first team pre-season All-Big Ten player, he is the first Wolverine to earn the honor since Daniel Horton in 2004.19 Although he is the leading returning scorer and top draft prospect,20 Purdue's Robbie Hummel was named Big Ten Preseason Player of the Year.21 Preseason All-Big Ten status has not always led to regular season honors. In both 2005 and 2007, only two preseason selections made the regular season team.22 Harris opened the 2008-09 Michigan Wolverines men's basketball season with a career-high 30 points in a 77–55 victory over Michigan Tech on November 11.23 The following night in the second game of the Coaches vs. cancer classic, Harris nearly posted a triple double when he posted 26 points, and career-highs with 10 rebounds and 8 assists in a 76–56 win against Northeastern University.24 At the end of November, while his team had a 5–1 record, Harris led the team in points, rebounds, assists, free throw percentage and steals.25 He is the Big Ten Conference leader in scoring and free throws made.2627

Personal

His name, Corperryale, is a combination of the names of his cousin (Corrine), an uncle (Perry) and the "ale" of his immediate siblings -- Janelle, Jerrelle and Al. He has a total of nine brothers and sisters and is the son of Merrick (Harris-Carter) and James Carter.28
