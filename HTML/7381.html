He was born Jean-David Bourdages in Saint-Denis, the son of Louis Bourdages and Louise-Catherine Soupiran, and was educated at the S<U+00E9>minaire de Nicolet. Bourdages went on to study medicine in Quebec City and at the University of New York. He was authorized to practise medicine in Lower Canada in 1818 and settled in Sainte-Marie-de-Monnoir. He served as justice of the peace and was a member of the board of medical examiners for Montreal district. In 1832, he married Marguerite Franch<U+00E8>re, the sister of Joseph and Timoth<U+00E9>e Franch<U+00E8>re. Bourdages died in office in Saint-Denis at the age of 32.
