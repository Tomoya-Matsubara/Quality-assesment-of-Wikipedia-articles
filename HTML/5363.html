George Lee (born 27 September 1962)1 is an Irish economist, journalist and former Fine Gael politician. He worked for RT<U+00C9> from 1992 to 2009. He was appointed Economics Editor in 1996. During his time in RT<U+00C9> News and Current Affairs he was named Irish Journalist of the Year for uncovering a major tax evasion and overcharging scandal at National Irish Bank. He has also presented many documentary programmes for RT<U+00C9>.

Lee became a Teachta D<U+00E1>la (TD) for the Dublin South constituency in June 2009, winning a by-election with a 53.4% majority and was referred to as a "celebrity TD". On 8 February 2010 Lee announced his resignation both from Fine Gael and from D<U+00E1>il <U+00C9>ireann, having spent nine months in politics. His reasoning was that he had "virtually no influence or input". He is set to return to RT<U+00C9> later in 2010.

Early and personal life

Lee's father was a motor mechanic and his mother was a hairdresser.2 Lee was the seventh in a family of eight children and grew up in Templeogue, Dublin.3 He attended Col<U+00E1>iste <U+00C9>anna, a Christian Brothers' School in the Dublin suburb of Ballyroan. Lee is a graduate of University College Dublin and holds an MSc in Economics from the London School of Economics where his specialist area was labour economics and unemployment.

He is married to Mary Lee (n<U+00E9>e Kitson),4 they have two children, Alison and Harry,Cabinteely. Lee famously travelled to work in RT<U+00C9> via Segway,5 once giving it a test ride live on Tubridy Tonight.6

Career

Lee joined the civil service as an executive officer in the Central Statistics Office.7 Two years later he started at University College Dublin where he studied economics under academics as Brendan Walsh and Peter Neary.

Prior to his move into broadcasting, he lectured in NUI, Galway and then worked as a journalist with the The Sunday Business Post. Lee was also a Senior Economist at Riada Stockbrokers. He also worked as Treasury Economist with FTI8 and as a research economist with the Central Bank of Ireland.9

From 1992 to 2009, he worked in RT<U+00C9>, the public broadcasting service of Ireland. He was appointed Economics Editor with RT<U+00C9> in 1996.10 Lee was named Irish Journalist of the Year, along with Charlie Bird, in 1998 after they uncovered a major tax evasion and overcharging scandal at National Irish Bank. He has devised, researched and presented several television series, including Moneybox, More To Do, Winds of Change, and Beyond the Berlin Wall. He is thought of as an "economics guru".11 He left RT<U+00C9> "in the late 1990s" to work for BCP Stockbrokers. He left the job and returned to his RT<U+00C9> post the next day.12

Before embarking on his political career, he filmed a four-part series based on the fall of the Berlin Wall in 2008.13 It was aired on RT<U+00C9> One in November 2009.14

George Lee was parodied in the 1990s comedy Bull Island, where he was seen "menacingly staring down the lens of a camera",15 and has also been featured on RT<U+00C9> 2fm's Nob Nation.16

Political career

On 5 May 2009 on the News at One on RT<U+00C9> Radio 1, Lee announced that he was resigning as Economic Editor with RT<U+00C9> and announced his intention to seek the Fine Gael nomination for the Dublin South by-election in 2009.17 Lee took a years unpaid leave from RT<U+00C9> in May 2009.18 On 6 May 2009, Lee was chosen as the Fine Gael candidate for the by-election. He was the only candidate for the nomination.19

He was elected on the first count to represent Dublin South on 6 June 2009. He received over 53% of the 1st preference vote. In total he received 27,768 1st preference votes.2021 His RT<U+00C9> position was filled by Europe editor Sean Whelan,22 but only as correspondent.23 Instead, David Murphy was promoted to Business Editor.24 When elected, Lee was referred to as a "Celebrity TD".25

In poll, concerning support for possible candidates in the 2011 presidential election conducted by the Sunday Independent in October 2009, Lee came third, receiving 12% support, in front of former Taoiseach Bertie Ahern among other high-profile politicians.26

Lee highlighted the failure of EMPG the holding company for US publisher Houghton Mifflin Harcourt and the potential impact on the Irish taxpayers given loans by Anglo Irish Bank to the investors in EMPG on 13 January 2010. Lee saw this as another example for the urgent need to an investigation into the Irish banking crisis.27

Resignation from politics

On 8 February 2010, he announced his resignation from Fine Gael and from D<U+00E1>il <U+00C9>ireann, due to having "virtually no influence or input" into shaping Fine Gael's economic policies at a time of economic upheaval.2829 It emerged that on 2 February, Lee met with the Fine Gael leader, Enda Kenny and told him of his intention to resign. Kenny then offered Lee the frontbench position as spokesman on economic planning. Speaking to reporters outside Leinster House soon after his announcement, Lee said it would have been dishonest of him to accept the position: "I had absolutely no input for nine months. I think I had to be honest with myself and honest with the electorate about that and not pretend." Asked if his resignation was a vote of no confidence in Kenny, Lee said there were "certainly lots of large mutterings at the moment in relation to the leader's position". He said he had "minimal involvement" with Fine Gael finance spokesman Richard Bruton. "I had a maximum of two or three conversations with Richard Bruton in a total nine months period. I don<U+2019>t know how my relationship is with [him]" Lee said.30

Kenny noted Lee had been appointed chair of the party's committee on economic policy and also its forum. "I had anticipated a very important role for [George Lee] in the coming period with Fine Gael." Kenny's spokesman later dismissed the proposition that the resignation had implications for his leadership. He cited the public endorsement of Kenny by 20 D<U+00E1>il deputies over the course of the weekend.31 Former Fine Gael leader Michael Noonan said he was surprised at the decision: "I thought that George Lee was fitting in well", adding that he believed he would have been a cabinet member in a Fine Gael led government.

George Lee was criticised after his resignation by Senator Eoghan Harris, who was speaking on the Lunchtime programme of Newstalk Radio. Harris suggested financial considerations and long working hours of politicians were the reasons why Lee resigned.3233 Fine Gael TD Brian Hayes, who was Lee's campaign manager in the Dublin South by-election, said that in discussions with Lee, the latter had complained about "a major reduction in his income" since leaving RT<U+00C9> to become a D<U+00E1>il backbencher. Lee denied that financial considerations had anything to do with his decision to quit politics.34

RT<U+00C9> received a letter from Lee confirming his intentions to return after his leave of absence.35 The Sunday Tribune said on 14 February 2010 that he will have to wait for three months before returning to RT<U+00C9>.36
