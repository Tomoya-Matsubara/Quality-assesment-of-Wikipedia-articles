Papyrus 75 (ùîì75, Papyrus Bodmer XIV-XV) is an early New Testament papyrus.

Description

Originally '[it] contained about 144 pages ... of which 102 have survived, either in whole or in part.'1 It 'contains about half the text of ... two Gospels'2 - Luke (Papyrus Bodmer XIV) and John (Papyrus Bodmer XV) in Greek. It is dated in Nestle-Aland (27th edition, NA27) as being an early third century manuscript. It is one the earliest manuscripts (along with <U+0001D513>4)3 of the Gospel of Luke. 'The surviving fragment contains Luke 3:18-24:53 ...'4 5. "An unusual feature of this codex is that Luke ends and John begins on the same page."6

Text

The Greek text of this codex is a representative of the Alexandrian text-type. Kurt Aland placed it in Category I.7 The text is closer to Codex Vaticanus Graecus 1209 than to Codex Sinaiticus. Agreement between ùîì75 and codex B is 92% in John,8 and 94% in Luke9. It concurs with Papyrus 111.10

Textual variants

In Luke 11:4 phrase (but deliver us from evil) is omitted. Omission is supported by the manuscripts: Sinaiticus, Vaticanus, Codex Regius, f1, 700, vg, syrs, copsa, bo, arm, geo.11

Luke 22:43-44 omitted, as in codices <U+05D0>*, A, B, T, 1071.14

In Luke 23:34 omitted words: "And Jesus said: Father forgive them, they know not what they do." This omission is supported by the manuscripts Sinaiticusa, B, D*, W, <U+0398>, 0124, 1241, ita, d, syrs, copsa, copbo.15

The manuscript is currently housed at the Vatican Library at Rome.

The discovery of ùîì75 had a profound effect on New Testament textual criticism, because of its great agreement with Codex Vaticanus.
