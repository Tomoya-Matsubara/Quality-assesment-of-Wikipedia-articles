Nina Davuluri (born April 20, 1989) is an American beauty pageant titleholder who was named Miss Syracuse 2013, Miss New York 2013, and Miss America 2014. She is the first Indian American to be chosen as Miss America.12

Background

Davuluri was born on April 20, 1989 in Syracuse, New York, to Telugu parents of the Hindu faith.34 Her mother, Sheila Ranjani a web programmer, and her father Davuluri Koteshwara Choudhary, a gynaecologist, came to the United States in 1981 from Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh, India.56 Her maternal aunt and uncle in India are also doctors and run a nursing home, while her father<U+2019>s siblings are doctors in the United States.7

Davuluri lived in Oklahoma for a period as a child until her family moved to St. Joseph, Michigan when she was 10. She graduated from St. Joseph High School (St. Joseph, Michigan) in 2007, where she "played varsity tennis, participated in band, was a cheerleader and captain of the color guard." 8 Davuluri attended college at the University of Michigan and won several scholastic honors including the Dean's List, Michigan Merit Award, and National Honor Society Award.9She graduated in 2012 with a degree in Brain Behavior and Cognitive Science.10 Davuluri plans to become a cardiologist.11

While growing up, Davuluri studied the Indian dance forms Kuchipudi and Bharatanatyam. 1213 She has also spoken publically about her battle with weight gain and Bulimia nervosa. She lost 60 pounds gained while in college before preparing for the Miss America pagaent. 14

Miss America

In 2006 she placed Runner-up for Miss Teen America.15 For the Miss America pageant, Davuluri prepared for an entire year. According to her grandmother Koteswaramma support came from "her mother, most importantly she has strong attachment to India and the city of Vijayawada," where she spent 2 years as a baby girl.16 Davuluri was crowned Miss America on September 15, 2013, a win that was met by a backlash of anti-Indian sentiments in social media. 1718 News agencies cited tweets that misidentified her as Muslim or Arab and associated her with terrorist groups such as Al Qaeda.1920 Davuluri responded to this criticism, "I have to rise above that, I always viewed myself as first and foremost American."21

Davuluri is the second Miss Syracuse (who was also Miss New York) to win the competition after Vanessa Williams's (the first African American winner of the crown) 1983 win (for the 1984 title). Both she and Williams won their crowns when the pageant was held in Atlantic City and both had to face backlash over their respective wins.22 Congresswoman Grace Meng congratulated Davuluri and compared her win with the first Jewish-American title won by Bess Myerson in 1945. 23 Davuluri was left on stage with first Runner-Up Chinese-American Miss California Crystal Lee when she said, "We<U+2019>re making history standing here as Asian Americans."24
