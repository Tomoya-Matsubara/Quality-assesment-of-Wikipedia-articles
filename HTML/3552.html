Gisele Caroline B<U+00FC>ndchen1 (, ; born July 20, 1980)2 is a Brazilian fashion model, actress, and producer. She is the Goodwill Ambassador for the United Nations Environment Programme.3 In the late 1990s, B<U+00FC>ndchen was the first in a wave of Brazilian models to find international success.4 In 1999, Vogue noted "The Return of the Sexy Model", and she was credited with ending the "heroin chic" era of modeling. B<U+00FC>ndchen was one of the Victoria's Secret Angels from 2000 until mid-2007. B<U+00FC>ndchen pioneered the "horse walk", a stomping movement created when a model picks her knees up high and kicks her feet out in front.5 Claudia Schiffer and Naomi Campbell have stated that B<U+00FC>ndchen is the only remaining true supermodel.6

B<U+00FC>ndchen has also ventured into acting. She played a supporting role in Taxi (2004), for which she was nominated at the Teen Choice Awards for Choice Movie Breakout Performance and for Choice Movie Bad Guy. B<U+00FC>ndchen also had a supporting role in The Devil Wears Prada (2006), and from 2010 to 2011 she was the executive producer of an educational environmental cartoon, Gisele & the Green Team. B<U+00FC>ndchen supports many charities including Save the Children, Red Cross and Doctors without Borders, as well as dedicating time to environmental causes.

Since 2004, she has been the highest-paid model in the world, and as of 2007 was the 16th richest woman in the entertainment industry.7 In 2012 she placed first on the Forbes top-earning models list.8 From 2000 to 2005, B<U+00FC>ndchen was in a relationship with American actor Leonardo DiCaprio.9 She married New England Patriots quarterback Tom Brady in 2009. As of 2014, she is listed as the 89th most powerful woman in the world by Forbes.10

Family and early life

B<U+00FC>ndchen is a sixth-generation German Brazilian,11 born in Tr<U+00EA>s de Maio, Rio Grande do Sul, southern Brazil, to V<U+00E2>nia Nonnenmacher, a bank clerk pensioner, and Valdir B<U+00FC>ndchen, a university teacher and writer, who are both of German descent.12 B<U+00FC>ndchen was born and raised in Horizontina, a southern region in Brazil where there is a large population of German ancestry.1314 She grew up with her five sisters<U+2014>Raquel, Graziela, Gabriela, Rafaela, and her fraternal twin Patr<U+00ED>cia, her junior by five minutes.15 She speaks Portuguese as her native language, in addition to Spanish, English and Italian. She studied German in school.16

In 1993, B<U+00FC>ndchen joined a modeling course with her sisters Patr<U+00ED>cia and Gabriela at their mother's insistence.17 The following year, B<U+00FC>ndchen was discovered by the Elite modeling agency at a shopping mall in S<U+00E3>o Paulo while on a school excursion. She was subsequently selected for a national contest, Elite Look of the Year,18 in which she placed second. Claudia Menezes from Bahia took first place. B<U+00FC>ndchen placed fourth in the Elite Model Look world contest, held in Ibiza, Spain.19 At the age of 14, she then moved to S<U+00E3>o Paulo to launch her modeling career. In 1996, B<U+00FC>ndchen had her first New York City break at Fashion Week.20

Career

1997<U+2013>2000: Career beginnings

In 1997 B<U+00FC>ndchen traveled to London, where she auditioned for 42 shows. She got her big break when chosen for her ability to walk in towering heels<U+2014>on a slippery runway<U+2014>for Alexander McQueen's spring 1998 "rain" ready-to-wear show. Echoing similar accolades for Elle Macpherson a decade earlier, McQueen dubbed B<U+00FC>ndchen "the Body", immediately boosting her bookings.21 In 1998 she secured her first British Vogue cover, and became the most in-demand cover girl of her generation with 10 covers in fewer than four years<U+2014>even Kate Moss has never featured on more than nine in the same period according to Vogue.co.uk. In 1998, she posed for Missoni, Chlo<U+00E9>, Dolce & Gabbana, Valentino, Gianfranco Ferr<U+00E9>, Ralph Lauren, and Versace campaigns. She made the cover of the French edition of Vogue, and fashion magazine i-D featured her on its cover, profiling "A Girl Called Gisele."22 The Vogue online encyclopedia of models states, "As the year 2000 approached, Gisele B<U+00FC>ndchen was the world's hottest model, opening up a new category in the popular imagination: the Brazilian bombshell."23

She appeared on the cover of Vogue in July, November, and December 1999. She won the VH1/Vogue Model of the Year for 1999, and a January 2000 cover gave her three consecutive Vogue covers. In December 2009 she appeared alongside her husband Tom Brady on the cover of book The World in Vogue<U+2014>People, Parties, Places.24 In 2000, she became the fourth model to appear on the cover of the music magazine Rolling Stone25 when she was named "the most beautiful girl in the world".26 B<U+00FC>ndchen has been on the covers of many top fashion magazines, including W, Harper's Bazaar, Elle, Allure, international editions of Vogue, as well as style and lifestyle publications such as i-D, The Face, Arena, Citizen K, Flair, GQ, Esquire, and Marie Claire.27 She appeared in the Pirelli Calendar in 2001 and 2006,28 and in broader publications such as TIME, Vanity Fair, Forbes, Newsweek, and Veja, for a total of more than 600 magazine covers throughout the world29<U+2014>a total that is only second to Princess Diana's.30 She simultaneously featured on the covers of both the US and British editions of Vogue in January 2000, the first issue of the new millennium.

She ranked in the top 10 of the Forbes list of "Entertainment's Highest-Paid Women" in 2012, at No. 8.182

In July 2007 Forbes named her the world's top-earning model.183 By 2008, she continued to dominate the Forbes list of the world's highest-earning models, and appeared on the magazine's list of the Top 20 Richest Women in Entertainment.184 During 2009<U+2013>2010, she was the top earning supermodel.185 In 2010, she retained the top spot as Forbes's highest-earning model.186 On May 2011, Forbes ranked her as the world's top-earning model for the fifth consecutive time. Her earnings climbed over the previous year, thanks to an economic boom in Brazil, coupled with a 10 per cent surge in the value of the Brazilian real against the dollar.187 Also in 2013 she ranked No. 3 on the 16 most successful female entrepreneurs list by Forbes.188

B<U+00FC>ndchen, however, denies her wealth, telling WSJ. magazine in November 2013, "Who are they speaking to when they come up with these numbers? Not my accountant, that's for sure."189
