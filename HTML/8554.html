Deborah O'Neill, an Australian federal politician, is a member of the Australian House of Representatives representing the seat of Robertson, New South Wales for the Australian Labor Party, elected at the 2010 federal election.

Early years and background

O'Neill grew up in the western suburbs of Sydney, a child of Irish immigrants, before moving to the Central Coast when she got married.1 She was a local teacher and a lecturer in the Faculty of Education and Arts at The University of Newcastle, Central Coast Campus.2

Political career

O'Neill was endorsed as Labor candidate following a very public process that saw incumbent Labor member, Belinda Neal, lose preselection.34
