Bill Brown was a member of Donald Bradman's famous Australian cricket team tour of England in 1948. Bradman's men were undefeated in their 34 matches and this unprecedented feat by a touring side earned them the sobriquet The Invincibles.

An experienced right-handed opening batsman, Brown was on his third visit to England, having first toured in 1934 before the Second World War. However, Brown's best years were lost to the war and by 1948, Sid Barnes and Arthur Morris had risen above him in the pecking order to become Australia's first-choice opening pair. Brown was selected as a reserve opener, a decision that was controversial among critics who believed that he was past his best.

Bradman rotated his three openers in the tour matches, but in the Tests, Morris and Barnes were preferred. Bradman accommodated Brown in his first-choice team by playing him out of position in the middle order. However, Brown had appeared to be uncomfortable in the unfamiliar role, and after making 73 runs at 24.33 in the first two Tests, he was dropped.

Nevertheless, Brown had success in the tour matches when he played as an opener. He scored 1448 runs at 57.92 in all first-class matches, ranking fourth in both the aggregates and batting averages. He scored eight centuries, second only to Bradman, including 200 against Cambridge University. However, Brown gained criticism for his slow batting. Following his omission from the Test team, Brown batted in a very circumspect manner in order to increase the reliability of his batting and volume of his scoring. A very occasional off spin bowler, Brown took his first-class best of 4/16 from 25 balls in his only stint with the ball, against the South of England.

Early tour

Brown had been a regular member of the Australian Test team since his debut in 1934, and immediately after the Second World War, he captained Australia in the inaugural Test against New Zealand.1 However, in 1946–47 he was sidelined with injury and Sid Barnes and Arthur Morris opened in the Tests, averaging 73.83 and 71.85 respectively.2 Brown resumed his Test career the following season, replacing Barnes, but he was dropped after two Tests. He returned in the Fifth Test when Morris was rested.3 In the Tests against India that season, Barnes, Morris and Brown had shared the opening duties against India; their batting averages were 43.00, 52.25 and 42.66 respectively.4 In any case, Brown had done enough to be chosen to tour England with the Invincibles in 1948, his third trip there, although Barnes and Morris were the first-choice opening combination.5

Australia traditionally fielded its first-choice team in the tour opener, which was customarily against Worcestershire.6 Brown gained selection, although Bradman batted him out of position in the middle order, while Barnes and Morris opened. Brown came in 6/335 and scored 25 at a typically placid pace,78 accumulating his runs at a slower rate than the other recognised batsmen. He was out with the score at 6/402 as Australia went on to declare at 6/462 and win by an innings.9

First Test

Thus, Brown gained selection in the First Test at Trent Bridge, batting out of position in the middle order while Barnes and Morris opened, whereas Harvey was dropped despite making a century in Australia's most recent Test against India.606162

England batted first and made 165, with Brown prominent in the field. Early on the first day of play, opener Cyril Washbrook was caught on the run by Brown on the fine leg boundary after attempting to hook Lindwall,636465 leaving England at 2/15 after 41 minutes.66 He later made a series of one-handed stops in the field, helping to keep the pressure on the batsmen.67 He then took his second catch of the innings to remove Alec Bedser after a rearguard fightback, with England ending on 165.68

On the second day, Barnes and Bradman took the score to 1/121 when Barnes and Miller were dismissed further addition to the score.697071 All the while, Australia had been scoring slowly, as they would throughout the day. Brown came in at No. 5 to join Bradman, but he had played most of his career as an opening batsman and he looked unaccustomed to batting in the middle order.72 Yardley took Laker out of the attack and took the second new ball. Bradman struck his first boundary in over 80 minutes but the run rate remained low. Australia passed England's total before Yardley brought himself on to bowl, trapping Brown leg before wicket (lbw) with an off cutter in his first over.7374 This ended a 64-run stand in 58 minutes and left Australia at 4/185.7576 Australia eventually reached 509, and after England compiled 441, Brown was not needed as Australia made the required 98 runs for the loss of only two wickets.77

Between Tests, Brown was rested for the match against Northamptonshire, which Australia won by an innings.7879 He returned for the match against Yorkshire. Opening with Barnes, Brown made 19 as Australia took a 43-run first innings lead. In the second innings, Brown top-scored with 113, combining with Bradman to add 154 runs for the second wicket as the match petered into a draw.80 Brown took four hours in making his century in a patient and restrained display. Following his controversial selection, Brown was attempting to justify his selection by accumulating a large amount of runs, but earned criticism for being too dour.81 This was enough to see him retain his middle-order position for the Second Test at Lord's, where Australia fielded an unchanged team.82838485

Second Test

In the Second Test at Lord's, Australia won the toss and batted first. Centurion Morris fell at 3/166 and Miller was then trapped for four.86 Brown came in at 4/173 and helped Hassett rebuild the innings after the two quick wickets. Both scored slowly, taking more than three and half minutes on average for each run.8788 After putting on a stand of 43, Hassett was bowled by Yardley, who then trapped Brown for 24 to leave Australia stumbling at 6/225. Australia managed to avoid further collapse and ended at 350 before bowling England out for 215.89 Australia batted much more productively in the second innings in ideal weather on the third day.90 Brown joined Miller after Bradman fell at 4/329 and Australia reached stumps at 343 without further loss, with Brown on seven.9192 After the rest day, Australia resumed with a lead of 478 runs with six wickets in hand. The morning was punctuated by three rain stoppages. Just ten minutes after the start, heavy rain intervened. The weather cleared and Miller and Brown moved to lunch on 63 and 32 respectively, with Australia at 4/409.93 In 88 minutes of play, Australia added a further 117 runs.94 Brown was caught behind from Coxon without adding to his score at lunch for 32 after an 87-run partnership with Miller with the score at 5/416, before Bradman declared at 7/460, 595 runs ahead. It would take a world record chase from England to win the match.9596 England lost wickets regularly and fell for 186 to lose by 409 runs.97 Although part of a successful team, Brown was unable to replicate the Test centuries he made in the preceding tours, and it was to be the last Test of his career.98

The next match was against Surrey and started the day after the Test. Brown injured a finger while fielding in the first innings, so he was not able to bat in Australia's first innings. In the second innings, Australia's makeshift openers Harvey and Sam Loxton chased down the 122 runs for victory to complete a 10-wicket win.99100101

Dropped

For the following match against Gloucestershire before the Third Test, Brown did not play.102103 Sam Loxton scored 159 not out, featuring in two century partnerships and propelling Australia to 7/774 declared, its highest of the tour, underpinning an innings victory. This ousted Brown from his middle-order position for the Third Test at Old Trafford.104105106107108 During the drawn Third Test, Barnes was injured and Ian Johnson was used as a makeshift opener now that Morris was the only specialist opener left with Brown having been omitted.109110111 In the meantime, Brown managed only eight as Australia defeated Middlesex by ten wickets in their only county match between Tests.112113

Barnes was still unavailable for the Fourth Test, but Brown was not recalled for the Fourth Test to open; instead, Hassett was promoted to open with Morris, while the teenaged Harvey came into the middle-order. Although Hassett failed with only 13 and 17, Harvey struck 112,114 as Australia chased down 404 to set a world record for the highest successful Test run-chase and win by seven wickets.115

Brown partnered the recovered Barnes and patiently scored 140, being the fifth man out with the score at 344, as Australia amassed 456 and defeated Derbyshire by an innings immediately after the Fourth Test.116117 Looking to regain his Test position by weight of runs, Brown started slowly, with Barnes making 24 of the first 29 runs.118 He took 185 minutes to reach 50, much to the displeasure of the spectators, before accelerating and scoring the next 50 in 37 minutes.119

In the next match against Glamorgan, Brown scored 16 in a rain-affected draw that did not reach the second innings.120121 He then scored 33 and seven, falling both times to Eric Hollies as Australia defeated Warwickshire by nine wickets.122 Brown was then rested as Australia faced and drew with Lancashire for the second time on the tour. Brown returned for the non-first-class match against Durham, scoring 49 out of Australia's 282 in a rain-affected draw that did not reach the second innings.123124

Brown was overlooked for selection in the Fifth Test. Barnes returned to the team and Australia crushed England by an innings to take the series 4–0.125126

Seven matches remained on Bradman's quest to go through a tour of England without defeat.127 Australia batted first against Kent and Brown top-scored with 106. After putting on 64 with Morris, he put on 104 each for the next two wickets with Bradman and Harvey before falling at 3/272.128 Brown again batted slowly, taking 255 minutes to reach triple figures, and was greeted with ironic clapping as he slowly accumulated the runs while his partners attacked.129 Brown's dismissal triggered a collapse as Australia lost their last seven wickets for 89 to end at 361.130 Despite this, they completed an innings victory.131 In the next match against the Gentlemen of England at Lord's, Brown scored 120, featuring in a 180-run second wicket partnership with Bradman, as Australia amassed 5/610 and won by an innings.132 Jack Fingleton said "Brown has always looked the class batsman that he is when playing at Lord's, and this superb century, so different in conception and execution to the one at Canterbury [against Kent], made one reflect what a grand opening batsman this series of Tests had done without".133 He then made a duck but Australia nevertheless defeated Somerset by an innings and 374 runs.134135 He then made 13 against the South of England in his last first-class match of the season. The match was washed out, but not before Brown took 4/16 in 4.1 overs in his only first-class bowling of the tour, cleaning up the tail.136137

Australia's biggest challenge in the post-Test tour matches was against the Leveson-Gower's XI. During the last tour in 1938, this team was effectively a full-strength England outfit, but this time Bradman insisted that only six England players from the season's Test series be allowed to compete. Bradman then fielded a full-strength team,138 with the only difference from the Fifth Test team being Johnson coming for Doug Ring, while Brown missed out. The match ended in a draw after multiple rain delays.139140

The tour ended with two non-first-class matches against Scotland. In the first match, Brown was rested as the Australians took an innings victory. In the second match, Brown scored 24 not out batting at No. 8, and then took a wicket in the second innings as Australia ended the tour with another innings victory.141142
