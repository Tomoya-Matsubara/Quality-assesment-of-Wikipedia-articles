Glen Anthony Rice, Jr. (born January 1, 1991) is an American college basketball shooting guard with the Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets basketball team who is playing his junior season for the . He is the son of National Basketball Association All-Star Glen Rice. Rice, Jr. was the leading returning scorer for the Yellow Jackets as a junior.1

In high school, Rice was the Cobb County, Georgia player of the year as a junior at George Walton Comprehensive High School and an All-State selection as both a junior and senior. He joined a highly recruited class at Georgia Tech. He is currently among the statistical leaders in numerous categories within the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC).

High school

As a junior, Rice was the 2008 Cobb County high school basketball boy's player of the year, according to the Marietta Daily Journal, as well as a third team (honorable mention) All-State selection by The Atlanta Journal-Constitution.234 When he signed his National Letter of Intent in November 2008, Rice was described as a late bloomer.5 As a senior, he was a Class AAAAA All-state selection (2nd team by The Atlanta Journal-Constitution and 1st team by the Georgia Sportswriters Association).67

Rice was ranked as the 13th, 32nd and 35th best high school basketball shooting guard in the country as a senior in 2009 by Rivals.com,8 Scout.com,9 and ESPN,10 respectively. His father's alma mater, University of Michigan did not recruit him for their basketball team until after he had already committed to Georgia Tech.11 Rice, Jr. was part of a Georgia Tech recruiting class that was ranked 21st in the nation and included Derrick Favors.12

Rice's Georgia Tech biography credits him with leading his high school to a state title in basketball as a senior,13 but the Georgia High School Association records show that his high school did not win a state championship in any sport during his senior season.14 The Atlanta Journal-Constitution confirms that his basketball team was eliminated in the second round of the class AAAAA tournament.15

College

Rice started his freshman season on the bench,16 but he played in all but one game for the 2009-10 Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets men's basketball team. He scored in double figures six times, including the 2010 NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Tournament second round game against Ohio State.17 He had season highs for points (17), rebounds (8) and assists (4) in the March 2, 2010 game against Clemson.1819 Rice earned ACC All-Academic team recognition.20 Although he was one of four people to receive at least one first place vote for ACC Rookie of the year,21 he was not on the five-player ACC All-Rookie team.22

As a sophomore, Rice posted six 20-point games,23 but three times (all Georgia Tech victories) he played at least 10 minutes while scoring no points.24 He started 23 of 31 games that year.25 Rice ranked second on the team in terms of minutes, points, rebounds, assists and steals.26 He ranked 17th in the ACC in scoring and 6th in steals.27 Three times Rice had more than 10 rebounds, giving him three double-doubles.28 Early in his sophomore season, the was in the same Legends Classic Tournament as the 2010-11 Michigan Wolverines men's basketball team, but since the Wolverines did not qualify for the championship game, he did not get to play against his father's alma mater.29 In mid January, Rice had his first back-to-back twenty-point scoring nights on January 16 and January 19 against North Carolina and Wake Forest.3031 Later in the season, he posted season highs in rebounds and point in back-to-back games:32 Rice had 12 rebounds on January 30 against the Maryland Terrapins and 28 points on February 3 against the .3334

Personal

Rice, Sr. is the all-time leading scorer for Michigan and led the 1988<U+2013>89 Wolverines to the 1989 NCAA Men's Division I Basketball Championship. He was also a member of the 1999<U+2013>2000 Los Angeles Lakers championship team.42 Rice, Jr. has a younger brother G'mitri Rice.43
