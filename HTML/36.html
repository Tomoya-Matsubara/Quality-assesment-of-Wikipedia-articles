Bad Blood (2004) was a professional wrestling pay-per-view event produced by World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE), which took place on June 13, 2004 at the Nationwide Arena in Columbus, Ohio. It was the third and final annual Bad Blood event. Seven professional wrestling matches, performances with pre-determined outcomes between wrestlers with fictional personalities that are portrayed as real, were featured on the event's card. The buildup to the matches and the scenarios that took place before, during, and after the event were planned by WWE's script writers. The event starred wrestlers from the Raw brand: a storyline expansion of the promotion where employees are assigned to a wrestling brand under the WWE banner.

The main event was a Hell in a Cell match, a match where wrestlers fight in a ring surrounded by a steel structure of metal, featuring wrestlers fighting in a ring surrounded by a steel structure of metal. Triple H (Paul Levesque) defeated Shawn Michaels (Michael Hickenbottom) by pinfall after two consecutive Pedigrees. Two featured bouts were featured on the undercard. In respective standard wrestling matches, World Heavyweight Champion Chris Benoit defeated Kane (Glen Jacobs) to retain his championship and Randy Orton defeated Shelton Benjamin to retain the WWE Intercontinental Championship.

Including the scripted buildup, Bad Blood (2004) grossed over $494,000 ticket sales from an attendance of 9,000. When the event was released on DVD, it reached a peak position of third on Billboard's DVD Sales Chart.

Background

The main event at Bad Blood was fought in a Hell in a Cell between Triple H (Paul Levesque) and Shawn Michaels (Michael Hickenbottom). The buildup to the match began after Backlash, WWE's previous pay-per-view event. On the May 3 edition of Raw, authority figure Eric Bischoff, a portrayed match maker and rules enforcer, booked a World Heavyweight Championship match, for that episode, between World Heavyweight champion Chris Benoit and Shawn Michaels.12 The title match saw Benoit successfully retain the World Heavyweight championship, after interference by Triple H, one of the opponents in the Triple Threat match at Backlash. Triple H managed to perform a Pedigree on both Benoit and Michaels, which led to Triple H stagger Benoit's arm to pin Michaels.34 On the May 10 edition of Raw, Bischoff granted Triple H a World Heavyweight championship match the following week.56 That same night, a match was scheduled between Shelton Benjamin and Triple H. The match resulted in Michaels attacking Triple H, ultimately resulting in Bischoff suspending Michaels for his actions.78 The following week, a Number One contender's Battle Royal match was scheduled, in which the winner would go on to face Chris Benoit for the World Heavyweight championship at Bad Blood.910 One of the participants in the match included Triple H, who after eliminating Shelton Benjamin, was eliminated by Michaels, who was not a participant in the match, as he clotheslined Triple H out of the match.1112 On the May 24 edition of Raw, Triple H pleaded with Bischoff to lift Michaels suspension and reinstate him back in the roster.1314 On the same evening, Michaels confronted Bischoff if he was scheduled in a match with Triple H at Bad Blood, in which Bischoff assured Michaels the match that would take place. Moment afterwards, Michaels was jumped from behind by Triple H, after Michaels attacked Triple H earlier.1516 Bischoff gave orders to Raw superstars to attack Triple H and Michaels, if seen fighting.1718 The roster tried breaking the fight up, but were unsuccessful in doing so.1920 The result of the fight gave Bischoff the authority to schedule Triple H and Michaels in a Hell in a Cell match at Bad Blood.2122

One of the featured preliminary matches was contested for the World Heavyweight Championship, in which Chris Benoit defended the championship against Kane. On the May 17 edition of Raw, Kane won a twenty man battle royal to receive a World Heavyweight Championship title shot against Chris Benoit at Bad Blood.2324 The following week, Kane revealed that he envied Benoit for having the World Heavyweight championship and concluded that he would get the World Heavyweight.2526 On the May 31 edition of Raw, Kane was scheduled in a match against Eugene (Nick Dinsmore). The match ended in a disqualification, after Kane hit Eugene with a steel chair.2728 After the match ended, Kane performed a chokeslam, when a wrestler grasps their opponent's neck, lifts them up, and slams them to the mat, on Eugene, which prompted Kane to set him up for a belly-to-belly piledriver. Benoit, however, made the save.2930 The following week on Raw, a handicap match was scheduled between Benoit and Edge (Adam Copeland) facing off against La R<U+00E9>sistance (Sylvain Grenier and Rob<U+00E9>rt Conway [Rob Conway]) and Kane. The match saw Kane perform a chokeslam on Benoit, thus pinning Benoit for the win.3132

The other featured preliminary match was Randy Orton versus Shelton Benjamin in a singles match for Orton's Intercontinental Championship. On the May 17 edition of Raw, Randy Orton, who was a heel, a bad guy, bragged about how he was the company's longest reigning Intercontinental champion in the last seven years.33 Shelton Benjamin interrupted Orton's speech and challenged Orton to an Intercontinental championship match. The challenge, however, was denied by Orton, in which prompted Benjamin to attack Orton.3435 The following week, Orton was a guest in Chris Jericho's (Christopher Irvine) segment, The Highlight Reel. In the segment, Orton stayed true to his word, saying that he would not grant Benjamin an Intercontinental title match. This led to Orton turning his attention towards Jericho and stating that he would enjoy adding Jericho to the list of legends that he has killed off, prior to Orton defeating legends in a "Legend versus Legend Killer" match.3637 An all-out brawl quickly ensued between Jericho and Orton. The brawl brought out fellow Evolution member Batista (David Bautista, Jr.), as he ran down to make it two on one. Benjamin also made his way to the ring and evened the sides. Bischoff announced that the battling that took place, would become in the form of a tag team encounter. The match saw the team of Benjamin and Jericho defeat Orton and Batista.3839 On the May 31 edition of Raw, it was announced that Orton would defend the Intercontinental championship against Benjamin at Bad Blood.4041 That same episode, Orton faced Benjamin in a non-title match, in which Benjamin got the win over Orton.4243 The following week on Raw, a match was booked between Benjamin and Batista. During the match, Orton came to ringside and distracted Benjamin, the distraction led to Batista performing a sitdown powerbomb, in which the wrestler drops into a sitting position as they slam their opponent down to the mat, and gaining the pinfall victory over Benjamin. After the match, all three members of Evolution triple-teamed Benjamin, with Orton finishing the assault, as he hit Benjamin with the title to the face.4445

Event

Before the event went live on pay-per-view, Batista defeated Maven (Maven Huffman) in a match taped for Heat, one of WWE's secondary television programs.46

Preliminary matches

After Sunday Night Heat, the pay-per-view began with a tag team match for the World Tag Team Championship, where La R<U+00E9>sistance (Sylvain Grenier and Rob<U+00E9>rt Conway) defended the titles against Chris Benoit and Edge. La R<U+00E9>sistance gained the early advantage when Conway grabbed Edge and rammed him onto the security wall, back first. Conway then rolled Edge back into the ring, in which Grenier dragged Edge to the corner, where Conway was standing, and double teamed Edge. The advantage was gained back by Edge, when he knocked Grenier with a spinning heel kick. Edge tagged Benoit into the match, which led him to get the advantage over Grenier and Conway. Benoit inserted Grenier in an arm trap crossface. Kane's pyrotechnics went off and made his way to the ring, as he entered the ring he hit Benoit with a big boot knocking him down and the referee ending the match in a disqualification, thus La R<U+00E9>sistance retaining the World Tag Team titles.4748

The second wrestling match on the pay-per-view was Tyson Tomko (Travis Tomko) (with Trish Stratus [Patricia Stratigias]) versus Chris Jericho in a standard match. In the early stages both competitors wrestled inconclusively, reversed each other's attacks, before Tyson backed Jericho into a corner and hit numerous reverse elbows. Afterwards, Jericho went for a middle rope springboard moonsault, but Tomko rolled out of the way, sending Jericho to crash down to the mat. The match concluded with Jericho performing an attack that struck the back of Tyson's head and pinning Tomko for the three count.4950

The next match was a standard match for the WWE Intercontinental Championship, in which Randy Orton defended the title against Shelton Benjamin. The match began with Benjamin performing a drop kick on Orton, which knocked him out to the outside of the ring. Benjamin proceeded to knock Orton over the security wall into the crowd. Orton got the advantage, after Ric Flair made his way to the ring, distracting Benjamin in the process. Back and forth action took place between Orton and Benjamin. The match came to an end when Benjamin went off the top rope and came with a cross body, but Orton rolled through and hooked Benjamin's tights to pin Benjamin for the three count and retaining the Intercontinental title.5152

Main event matches

The first featured preliminary match was a fatal four-way match for the WWE Women's Championship, in which Victoria (Lisa Marie Varon) defended the title against Trish Stratus, Lita (Amy Dumas), and Gail Kim. The start of the match saw Victoria and Lita double team Stratus, but Stratus rolled out of the ring. Lita and Victoria went on to double team Kim, however, Stratus pulled Lita out of the ring, which left Victoria to perform a move in which Victoria stood next to Kim lying on the ground and executed a backflip and landed on her. The match was controlled by Victoria, Stratus, Lita, and Kim in several spots in the match. Lita performed a move in which Kim fell down which drove her head into the mat, as Lita went for the pin, Stratus rolled Lita up for the pinfall victory, thus Stratus' becoming Women's champion for a fifth time.5354

The fifth match was between Eugene and Jonathan Coachman in a singles match. The match started with Eugene hitting an arm drag on Coachman and hitting numerous headbutts. An unknown woman in a bikini came out to the ringside area with some cookies, Coachman offered cookies to Eugene. The woman lured Eugene out of the ring and Eugene went over and grabbed some cookies. As Eugene went to grab more cookies, Coachman slammed Eugene's head into the cookie tray. A spot in the match saw Garrison Cade (Lance McNaught) come out to ringside with a stuffed animal, which prompted Cade to tore up toy animal. Cade tried to hold Eugene so Coachman could get the advantage, however, Eugene was able dodge the attack, as Coachman ended up hitting Cade instead. Eugene grabbed Coachman and performed a variation which sees Eugene hook Coachman's leg in order to lift him easier and a Feint leg drop transitioned into an elbow drop to the chest, with theatrics. Eugene pinned Coachman for the three count for the win.5556

The featured preliminary match was for the World Heavyweight Championship, in which Chris Benoit defended the title against Kane. Benoit started the match by hitting Kane numerous times with backhand chops, a wrestler slaping the chest of his opponent with the palm of the hand using a backhand swing. Mid-way in the match, Benoit charged towards Kane, but Kane hit a sidewalk slam, where a wrestler stands side-to-side and slightly behind with the victim, facing in the same direction, and reaches around the victim's torso with his near arm across the victim's chest and under both arms and places the other arm under the victim's legs him and tossed him over the top rope to the arena floor, Kane then stepped out of the ring and grabbed Benoit, in which he wanted to launch Benoit into the ring post, but Benoit was able to reverse Kane's attack and slammed him into the ring post. After blocking an arm trap crossface attempt, Kane lifted Benoit by the throat and slammed him down to the mat for a two count. The match concluded with Kane headed to the top rope and coming off to perform a flying clothesline, a move in which a wrestler will jump from an elevated position and perform a clothesline to a standing opponent, but Benoit went for another arm trap crossface, but Kane fought out of and Benoit rolled Kane up in a pin to get a three count, and thus retaining the World Heavyweight title.5758

The main event was the Hell in a Cell match between Shawn Michaels and Triple H. The match began with Michaels hitting a Lou Thesz press, a move in which it sees the attacking wrestler jump towards a standing opponent and knock him over, resulting in the opponent lying on his back with the attacking wrestler sitting on the opponent's chest, pinning him in a body scissors. This was followed with Michaels' slamming Triple H's head onto the steel cage, resulting in Triple H bleeding from the forehead. Triple H got the advantage, as he was able to counter a Piledriver, a move in which the wrestler grabs his opponent, turns him upside-down, and drops into a sitting or kneeling position, driving the opponent's head into the mat, and performed a back body drop on Michaels. Another attack saw Triple H bring the steel steps and nail Michaels with it, which resulted in Michaels bleeding from the forehead. A spot in the match saw Michaels lay Triple H in a table, as he climbed onto a ladder he took out from under the ring, and performed a diving elbow drop by diving onto Triple H and driving the elbow into his chest, thus breaking the table in the process. Following the spot, Triple H gained the advantage and tucked Michaels' head between his knees and lifted them up to slam Michaels' head to the mat, twice, a move that Triple H calls the Pedigree. Triple H covered Michaels for the win via pinfall.5960 After the match, Michaels staggered to his feet and was given a standing ovation by the audience.6162

Aftermath

Following Bad Blood, Raw commentator Jim Ross tried making amends between Shawn Michaels and Triple H by making them shake hands. Michaels and Triple H extended their hands to shake, but Kane came out and attacked Michaels, which resulted in Kane crushing Michaels throat with a wedged chair.63 Michaels was then taken out in an ambulance for medical attention.64 That same episode, Lita revealed that she was pregnant. On the June 21 episode of Raw, it appeared that Lita's, boyfriend at that time, Matt Hardy was going to propose to Lita, but he was interrupted by Kane, who claimed to be the father of Lita's child, as Lita had slept with Kane to persuade him to stop attacking Hardy.6566 Two months later, it was revealed that Kane was, in fact, the father.6768 Hardy and Kane feuded for several months, culminating in a "Till Death Do Us Part" match at SummerSlam, with the stipulation that Lita would be obliged to marry Kane should Hardy lose.6970 Kane won the match,71 leading to he and a reluctant Lita marrying one another on the August 23 episode of Raw.7273 On the August 30 edition of Raw, Kane stated that Eric Bischoff's wedding gift to him and Lita was to name any match he wanted for Unforgiven.7475 Lita then informed Kane that Bischoff's present was for both of them, where they were able to pick Kane's opponent for Unforgiven, which resulted in Shawn Michaels being picked.7677 At Unforgiven, Michaels defeated Kane in a No Disqualification match.78

Eric Bischoff informed Triple H that if he could defeat Eugene, for the following week's episode, that he would be Number one contender to face Chris Benoit for the World Heavyweight title.7980 The following week on Raw, the match between Triple H and Eugene ended in a No Contest, after Chris Benoit ran down to save Eugene from a chair assault by Triple H.8182 On the June 28 edition of Raw, a rematch for the World Heavyweight championship between Benoit and Kane took place. A stipulation was placed that Benoit had to win the match by a submission, as Kane could win by pinfall, submission, disqualification or count out.8384 It was also announced that the winner of the match would face Triple H at Vengeance, in which the World Heavyweight title would be on the line.8586 Benoit won the match, after he made Kane submit to an arm trap crossface.8788 The following week, Bischoff scheduled a handicap match between Triple H, Ric Flair and Eugene versus Benoit and Edge. The match was won by Triple H, after Eugene hit Benoit with a steel chair and Triple H pinning Benoit for the win for his team.8990 At Vengeance, Benoit defeated Triple H to retain the World Heavyweight title.91

On the June 21 edition of Raw, a confrontation between Randy Orton and The Rock (Dwayne Johnson) took place. After the Rock assaulted Orton, Bischoff sent security guards to escort the Rock out of the arena. Orton demanded that they stop playing the Rock's theme music. Edge, however, sneaked behind Orton and speared him.9293 That same episode, a tag team match was booked between Edge and Chris Jericho versus Orton and Batista. During the match, Jericho took a shot to the head and was unconscious as they went to commercial break. Several referees went to ringside, and took Jericho to the back, leaving Edge by himself against Orton and Batista. The match saw the team of Orton and Batista defeat Edge.9495 The following week, it was announced that Orton would defend the Intercontinental championship against Edge at Vengeance.9697 Later that night, per the week before, a similar tag team match between Orton and Batista versus Edge and Jericho took place. This time, Edge and Jericho won the match, after Batista accidentally hit Orton with a lariat and Edge spearing Batista, thus allowing Jericho to pin Batista for the win.9899 At Vengeance, Orton lost the Intercontinental title to Edge.100

Reception

The Nationwide Arena has a maximum capacity of 19,500.101102 The event grossed over $494,000 in ticket sales from an attendance of 9,000.103104 Canadian Online Explorer's professional wrestling section rated the entire event a five out of ten stars.105 The rating was higher than the Bad Blood event in 2003, which rated a four out ten stars.106 The World Heavyweight championship match between Chris Benoit and Kane was rated an eight out of ten stars.107 The match between Eugene and Jonathan Coachman was rated zero out of ten stars.108

The event was released on DVD on July 13, 2004.109 The DVD was distributed by the label, Sony Music Entertainment. The DVD reached third on Billboard's DVD Sales Chart for recreational sports during the week of August 28, 2004, although falling thereafter.110 It remained in the chart for two consecutive weeks, until the week of September 25, 2004, when it ranked 19th.111 The DVD also received reviews from customers; the average customer rating from Amazon.com was four out of five stars.112
