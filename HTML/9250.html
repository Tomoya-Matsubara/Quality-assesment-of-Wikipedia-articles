Kristina Stefanova Sandulova (, born in Sofia, Bulgaria), is a concert pianist, Bulgarian by birth and Dutch by nationality. She emigrated to the Netherlands in 2001, and currently resides in Vienna, Austria. She has an international concert career. Sandulova is the daughter of the renowned Bulgarian composer prof. Julia Tsenova.

Biography

Kristina Sandulova began her piano studies with her mother, the composer Julia Tsenova, having her first public performance at the age of six, at the National Palace of Culture (<U+041D><U+0414><U+041A>). She later studied at the National Academy of Music <U+201C>Pancho Vladigerov<U+201D> under the supervision of prof. Stella Dimitrova-Maistrova.1 In 2001 she received a grant from the <U+201C>Stichting Labbert<U+00E9>-Hoedemaker Fonds<U+201D> in order to continue her studies in the Netherlands. There Sandulova studied with Paul Komen, Geoffrey Madge and Kl<U+00E1>ra W<U+00FC>rtz at the Prins Claus Conservatorium, the Royal Conservatory of The Hague and the Conservatory of Utrecht, respectively.

Currently Sandulova resides in Vienna, Austria, and has an international concert career, having performed in the Netherlands, Austria, Germany, Irelend, Bulgaria, Norway, Iceland, Japan and the United Kingdom. She is also the founder of the <U+201C>Julia Tsenova Music & Arts Tribute<U+201D>, a concert series dedicated to the memory of her mother, Julia Tsenova, which began as a joint initiative with artist-composer Joseph Johannes Visser.2

Sandulova participated in numerous international festivals for contemporary music, including <U+201C>ppIANISSIMO<U+201D> in Sofia, Bulgaria and the <U+201C>Buchkunstbiennale<U+201D> in Horn, Austria.34 She has also delivered international piano master classes and lecture-recitals about Julia Tsenova, among others at the Koyo Conservatory in Kobe, Japan,5 the Reykjav<U+00ED>k College of Music in Iceland and the Staatliche Hochschule f<U+00FC>r Musik und Darstellende Kunst Mannheim, Germany.6

Competitions Record

Sandulova has prizes at the following national and international competitions:

  • <U+201C>Liszt-Bartok competition<U+201D>, Sofia, Bulgaria: Honorable mention (1995).7
  • <U+201C>Music and Earth competition<U+201D>, Sofia, Bulgaria: Piano Duo Performance, awarded by Julia Ganeva and Konstantin Ganev (1996).8
  • <U+201C>Music and Earth competition<U+201D>, Sofia, Bulgaria: Award for Artistic Merit (1997).
