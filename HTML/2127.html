Ricardo Iv<U+00E1>n Rodr<U+00ED>guez Araya (born 25 August 1992) is a Swiss professional footballer who plays as a left back for VfL Wolfsburg in the Bundesliga.

Born to a Spanish father and a Chilean mother, Rodr<U+00ED>guez began his professional career at local club FC Z<U+00FC>rich in 2010, having spent eight years in the youth setup. After breaking through into the first team at Z<U+00FC>rich, Rodr<U+00ED>guez was sold to VfL Wolfsburg in January 2012 for <U+00A3>7.5 million.1 He has been described as an accomplished defender and is noted for his strong heading ability.2 He has also become known for his ability to deliver pinpoint crosses.345 Rodr<U+00ED>guez is also noted for his speciality in dead ball situations and for penalty taking abilities, emerging as Wolfsburg's first-choice penalty taker.67

Rodr<U+00ED>guez has earned caps at every level of the Swiss national team setup, winning the FIFA U17 World Cup in 2009. A full international for Switzerland since 2011, Rodriguez has earned over 25 caps for the country. He was part of the Swiss team at the 2014 FIFA World Cup and later that year was voted Swiss Footballer of the Year.8

Club career

FC Z<U+00FC>rich

Rodr<U+00ED>guez began playing football at the highly regarded youth club FC Schwamendingen in 2001 before joining the youth setup of FC Z<U+00FC>rich as an 11-year-old in 2002.9 He was promoted to the senior squad in 2009 at age 16, making the bench for Z<U+00FC>rich's 2<U+2013>3 defeat to NK Maribor in the first leg of their third qualifying round Champions League tie on 29 July 2009.1011

He made his Z<U+00FC>rich debut as a 17-year-old in the 25th round of the Swiss Super League on 21 March 2010, replacing the injured Hannu Tihinen in the first-half of the 2<U+2013>0 win over Bellinzona.12 His full debut came in the Z<U+00FC>rich Derby against Grasshoppers on 5 April, playing the entire 3<U+2013>2 win for the hosts.13

He made only his second start in the first match of the Swiss Super League season on 20 July 2010, playing the full 90 minutes in a 2<U+2013>3 defeat to rivals FC Basel.14 On 28 April 2011, he scored his first professional goal for the club, opening the scoring for the hosts as they ran out 3<U+2013>0 winners over Neuch<U+00E2>tel Xamax at the Letzigrund.15

Rodr<U+00ED>guez made his European debut in the first-leg of their third qualifying round tie against Standard Li<U+00E8>ge on 27 July 2011.1617 He played the entire match and provided the cross for fellow Z<U+00FC>rich youth team graduate Admir Mehmedi as the Swiss secured a 1<U+2013>1 draw at the Stade Maurice Dufrasne in Belgium.18 In the second leg, Rodr<U+00ED>guez missed from an open goal from close range but Z<U+00FC>rich won 1<U+2013>0 to earn a spot in the play-off round against Bayern Munich.19 He played the entirety of both play-off matches against Bayern,2021 but the Swiss side fell 0<U+2013>3 on aggregate and dropped into the Europa League.22

On 26 October 2011, Rodr<U+00ED>guez scored his second goal for the club, netting his side's second from the penalty spot, in a 2<U+2013>0 win over FC Thun.23 The club's final Super League match before the winter break on 10 December 2011, turned out to be Rodr<U+00ED>guez's last in a Z<U+00FC>rich shirt, playing the entire 1<U+2013>1 draw with FC Sion.24 On 11 January 2012, Z<U+00FC>rich officials announced that the club had accepted a bid for Rodr<U+00ED>guez by German side Wolfsburg and that he was on the verge of completing the transfer, subject to a medical.25 Despite being sold mid-way through the season, Rodr<U+00ED>guez was voted as the Fans' Player of the Season on 4 June 2012, ahead of Oliver Buff and Pedro Henrique.26

VfL Wolfsburg

Early career

VfL Wolfsburg purchased Rodr<U+00ED>guez as a 19-year-old on 13 January 2012 for <U+00A3>7.5 million with the player signing a four and a half year deal.2728 He quickly asserted himself into the first-team lineup, making his Bundesliga debut the next day against 1. FC K<U+00F6>ln, a 1<U+2013>0 victory for the Wolves.29 Rodr<U+00ED>guez went on to play every game of the season from that point on without being substituted, becoming a favorite on the left of the defense for manager Felix Magath.30

Through the first ten league games of the following campaign, Rodr<U+00ED>guez was once again an ever-present in the squad, featuring in all the outings, starting eight.31 But Magath was sacked after a poor run of form in the league and when Lorenz-G<U+00FC>nther K<U+00F6>stner was installed as interim manager, the more experienced Marcel Sch<U+00E4>fer was preferred at left back.32 Once Dieter Hecking was appointed manager though, Rodr<U+00ED>guez won his place back in the first team.33

2013<U+2013>14 season

On 9 November 2013, he scored his first goal for Wolfsburg, from a direct free kick in a home Bundesliga match against Borussia Dortmund.34 The match ended in a 2<U+2013>1 win for Wolfsburg, although they were losing at half-time.35 His second goal for Wolfsburg came from the penalty spot against Hamburg on 29 November, earning his side a 1<U+2013>1 draw.36 He opened the scoring in their Bundesliga match on 14 December, curling the ball past keeper Sven Ulreich, as Wolfsburg won 3<U+2013>1.37

He finished the 2013<U+2013>14 season with five league goals, in addition to nine assists.3839 He failed to make it into the Bundesliga Team of the Season, but was included in a list of the Best Defenders for the 2013<U+2013>14 campaign.40 Rodr<U+00ED>guez's nine assists also meant that he created more goals than any other full-back in Europe.41 His impressive displays during the season included 2.3 key passes per game, the same number as Andrea Pirlo, the two-time reigning Serie A Footballer of the Year, and 2.6 successful dribbles per game, more than Cristiano Ronaldo, the reigning Ballon d'Or winner.42

2014<U+2013>15 season

He scored his first ever goal in European football for Wolfsburg on 18 September 2014, netting directly from a free-kick after having previously scored an own-goal in a 4<U+2013>1 defeat to English side Everton.43 Three days later, Rodriguez scored twice more for Wolfsburg, once from the penalty spot and a volley from a Kevin De Bruyne corner, as Wolfsburg defeated Bayer Leverkusen 4<U+2013>1.44 He scored his third league goal of the season against Werder Bremen on 27 September, converting a De Bruyne cross to put the Wolfsburg up 1<U+2013>0 in an eventual 2<U+2013>1 victory.45

In October 2014, Rodr<U+00ED>guez was ruled out for a number of weeks with a troubled patella tendon and thigh.46

International career

In 2009, he was part of the Swiss U17 team who won the 2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup.47 His first goal of the tournament came in Switzerland's second group game against Japan on 27 October 2009, netting the winning goal as the match ended 4<U+2013>3.48 He opened the scoring for the Swiss in the 35th minute against Germany in the Round of 16 as Switzerland eventually won 4<U+2013>3 in added extra time.49 In the semi-finals against Colombia, he scored Switzerland's final goal of their 4<U+2013>0 win to reach their first final of a FIFA competition since a 3<U+2013>0 loss to Uruguay in the 1924 Olympics.50 He played the entire final against Nigeria on 15 November, helping Switzerland to a 1<U+2013>0 victory to become just the third European nation to lift the trophy.51

Rodr<U+00ED>guez made his debut for Switzerland in a Euro 2012 qualifying match against Wales on 7 October 2011, replacing Xherdan Shaqiri in the second half of the 0<U+2013>2 loss.525354 In his full debt four days later, Rodr<U+00ED>guez played the entire match as the Swiss defeated Montenegro 2<U+2013>0 to end their qualification campaign on a high, despite missing out on a playoff spot to the Balkan side.5556 In a friendly match against the Netherlands on 11 November, he played the entire match as the Swiss kept a clean sheet in Amsterdam.57

He played all three matches for Switzerland at the 2012 Olympics, in which they were eliminated in the group stage.58

Rodr<U+00ED>guez featured in nine of ten World Cup qualifying matches for the Swiss, totaling 810 minutes,59 as they qualified for the World Cup with a game to spare, thanks to a 2<U+2013>1 win over Albania on 11 October 2013.6061 On 13 May 2014, Rodr<U+00ED>guez was named in Switzerland's squad for the 2014 FIFA World Cup.62 In their opening group game against Ecuador on 15 June, he took the corner which was headed by Admir Mehmedi for the equaliser, as well as crossing for Haris Seferovi<U+0107> to score the winner in a 2<U+2013>1 victory.63 During the tournament finals in Brazil, he averaged 5 tackles and 2.5 interceptions per game, earning plaudits for his strong tackling as well as his ability to read the game.64

Playing style

Rodr<U+00ED>guez is known for being equally adept at defending as he is going forward.65 Experts consider him as an accomplished defender and note his strong heading ability.66 As well as considering him a continual attacking threat and noting his exceptional stamina and pace which allow for his constant, buccaneering runs up the wing.676869 Conversely, he has been criticized for giving too much room to opposition attackers, taking up poor positioning and lacking in defensive discipline.70 While other experts have criticized his concentration on defense.71

He has become known for his pinpoint crossing and accurate left foot and has been compared to Everton and England left back Leighton Baines.727374 He is also noted for his speciality in dead ball situations and for penalty taking abilities, emerging as Wolfsburg's first-choice penalty taker during the 2013<U+2013>14 season.7576

Personal life

Rodr<U+00ED>guez was born in Z<U+00FC>rich to a Spanish father and a Chilean mother.77 As a result, he is fluent in Spanish and holds Spanish and Chilean nationalities.787980 He is the younger brother of FC St. Gallen midfielder Roberto Rodr<U+00ED>guez and the older brother of FC Z<U+00FC>rich and Switzerland U19 midfielder Francisco Rodr<U+00ED>guez.81
