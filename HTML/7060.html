Johann Baptist Drechsler, a painter of flowers, was born at Vienna in 1766, and became a professor at the Academy of that city. He died at Vienna in 1811. He painted fruit and flower pieces in imitation of Van Huysum. There are examples of his work in the Hermitage at St. Petersburg, and in the Vienna Gallery.
