Alexander George "Alex" Claire (born 14 September 1985) is a British singer-songwriter.1 Clare adopted his current stage name, Alex Clare, in 2010, replacing Alexander G. Muertos, a pseudonym he first used whilst still at school. His debut album, The Lateness of the Hour, was released in the UK on 11 July 2011 on Island Records2 and was produced by Mike Spencer and Major Lazer.3

The biggest hit from the album, "Too Close", peaked at number 4 on the UK Singles Chart and number 7 on the US Billboard Hot 100. The song was nominated for the Brit Award for Best British Single at the 2013 BRIT Awards.4

Early life

Clare was born in Southwark, London, England. He grew up listening to his father<U+2019>s jazz records and was drawn to blues and soul (Donny Hathaway, Stevie Wonder), which eventually led to interests in drum and bass, dubstep and UK garage. Though Clare took up the trumpet and drums as a youngster, he eventually placed an emphasis on guitar and songwriting, playing open mic nights with original material.5 Clare attended Shavington High School in Crewe, Cheshire and thereafter Westminster Kingsway College from 2002 studying an NVQ in catering.

Music career

A demo earned him a deal with the Island label in the UK. His debut album, The Lateness of the Hour, produced by Mike Spencer and Major Lazer6 was released in July 2011. The first single from the album was "Up All Night", followed by "Too Close" and "Treading Water".

On 19 May 2011, Reggie Yates made Alex Clare's cover of Prince's "When Doves Cry" his Song of the Day.7 A day later, the Dutch radio station 3FM announced single "Too Close" to be the '3FM's Megahit of that week.8

On 21 June 2011, Clare performed at the Queens' College, Cambridge May Ball.9 On 14 August 2011, Clare performed at the Summer Well festival in Buftea, Romania.

Following the disappointing performance of the album, Clare was dropped from his record contract with Universal Island in the UK.10

In March 2012, Clare's song "Too Close" was featured in several Internet Explorer 9 advertisements.11 Following its use, the song debuted at #68 on the US Billboard Hot 100,12 and later reached #7, becoming his first Top 10 hit. Clare saw success on the German charts too, topping the charts at #1. On 16 May, Clare's song peaked at #4 on the UK Singles Chart.13 Soon after the song appearance in charts, Universal Republic signed Clare to a distribution deal that allowed The Lateness Of The Hour to be released in America in March 2012.1415 In mid-2012 it was announced that Universal Republic was going defunct moving artists from its roster to Republic Records including Alex Clare.16

In 2012 he sang the role of Voice of Humanity on the album Jeff Wayne's Musical Version of The War of the Worlds <U+2013> The New Generation along with Gary Barlow, Joss Stone and Liam Neeson.

In May 2014, Clare released the video for his single "War Rages On", which will serve as the lead single from his upcoming sophomore album "Three Hearts", which was set to be released 21 July 2014 but then pushed back to 11 August 2014 for unknown reasons.17

Personal life

Clare's upbringing was "very, very secular", and he became more religious during the mid-2000s.1819 He is now an Orthodox Jew, and as such will not perform, travel or work on the Jewish Sabbath or religious holidays. Clare has said he lost one record deal opportunity due to his Sabbath observance,20 but has found other ways to continue his career successfully. Clare also keeps kosher and travels with his own sets of pots and pans. While on tour, Clare maintains a schedule of learning Jewish topics in the Talmud and studies the Tanya.21

In 2006, before gaining critical success, Clare dated British singer Amy Winehouse, but the relationship lasted less than a year.22 They met while he was performing and working at a bar she frequented, the Hawley Arms in Camden, North London.23

He lives in Golders Green, an area in northwest London, but often travels to Jerusalem when his work permits.24
