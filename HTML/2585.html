Rodney Williams, Jr. (born July 23, 1991) is an American basketball player for the Delaware 87ers of the NBA Development League. Williams played college basketball for four years at the University of Minnesota and then went undrafted in the 2013 NBA draft. His height is 6'7 and he plays small forward.

Williams is the son of Rodney and Shanell Williams. He attended Robbinsdale Cooper High School, where he became the schools all-time leading scorer. At Minnesota, he finished his career with 1,145 points, which is 26th on the Gophers scoring list.1

College

He matriculated at Minnesota in 2009. Trevor Mbakwe missed most of the season for the 2011<U+2013>12 Golden Gophers.2 In Mbakwe's absence, Williams finished among the 2011<U+2013>12 Big Ten Conference leaders in several statistics: rebounds per game (10th, 5.6), steals per game (10th, 1.3), blocked shots per game (4th 1.4), and field goal percentage (5th, 56.4%).3 Williams led the team scoring (12.2 ppg), rebounding, steals and blocks averages that year and in the 2012 National Invitation Tournament, he averaged 19.2 points and 5.6 rebounds.4 The following year, upon Mbakwe's return, most of Williams' averages were down slightly, but he still finished among the 2012<U+2013>13 Big Ten Conference leaders in blocked shots per game (6th 1.3).5

Pro career

After going undrafted in the 2013 NBA Draft, he signed that night with the Philadelphia 76ers.6 After signing with the team he played on the 76ers team in the Orlando Summer League.7 He was cut by the 76ers at the end of training camp and plays for their NBA Development League affiliate 87ers.
