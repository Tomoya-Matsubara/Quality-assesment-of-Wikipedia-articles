Herbert Dwight Smith (February 10, 1926 - July 1, 2011) was a longtime lawyer in Alva, Oklahoma. He was born on the Smith farm East of Alva, in the area known as <U+201C>Little Potsdam<U+201D>. His parents, Raymond Robert Smith and Esther Margaret Coulter Smith welcomed their second son on February 10, 1926.

Early life

Herbert D. Smith was born on February 10, 1926 to Raymond Robert Smith and Esther Margaret Coulter Smith. He became a member of the Alva Presbyterian Church at an early age. Herb helped with the farming, which included working with a garden, orchard and dairy, plus assisting with wheat harvest in Oklahoma and Texas. Most of all after the death of his brother, Dean, in World War II and his return, he felt more responsibility for his parents and still helped with farming, etc.

Education

Smith graduated from Alva High School and was a member of the National Honor Society in 1943 and attended Northwestern briefly, before joining the U.S. Army. The Army provided some education at Baylor University, but eventually he was sent overseas to Germany. After being sent to Fitzsimmons Hospital, he returned to his home and enrolled at the University of Oklahoma. He served on the Student Senate and obtained his Chemical Engineering degree in 1950. That same year he was made homeless by the Bachelor Officer's Quarters fire. He then returned to Alva and assisted his father with farming, eventually forming his own harvesting operation traveling to various states, but home always looked the best. Herb became interested in law and enrolled at O.U. in the School of Law in the Fall of 1955.

War Experience

Herbert D. Smith was a corporal in the Infantry Co. G 232nd Inf. 42nd Div. WWII. Smith was a runner for his company commander and was sent to survey the base's surrounding land. One day, he went out to survey the land and saw that German troops were coming. He informed his company commander of the enemy troops, but the commander said that they could not leave their base. He was captured in the Black Forest by the Germans and confined to Stalag No. IV-B for 108 days. He was only served cabbage and a small ration of sugar. Prisoners had to sleep with their knees up to their chests because there was not enough space to lay out flat. They were also only allowed one uniform for their entire stay at the camp. Smith said that his uniform was so dirty that he could take his fingernail and scrape the dirt off of it. He was liberated by the Russians and returned home. His Honorable Discharge states he received the following Decorations and Citations: European-African-Middle Eastern Campaign Medal, American Theater Medal, World War II Victory Medal, and Good Conduct Medal. In November, 1988, he received his Prisoner of War Medal. His battles and campaigns were Rhineland (GO 40 WD45) Central Europe (G0 40 WD45).

Political career

Smith was elected to the Oklahoma House of Representatives in 1954 on the Democratic ticket and served one term serving on the Enrolling and & Engrossing Bills Committee as Co-Chair. Herb passed the Oklahoma Bar and set up his law practice in July 1958. He was a member of the Oklahoma Bar Association and the American Bar Association and has served as Acting County Attorney, Acting County Judge and Alva City Attorney.

Acting career

He received the Best Actor Award in 1974 for his role as Elwood P. Dowd in Harvey. There were several return performances as Charles Condamin in Blithe Spirit, plus roles in Teahouse of the August Moon, Arsenic and Old Lace, and South Pacific to name a few. He was a quick study and had a <U+201C>razor sharp<U+201D> memory.

Membership

He served as deacon and elder for the Presbyterian Church and served as Building Chairman for the new Presbyterian Church dedicated May 4, 1965. He has served as Worshipful Master of the Masonic Lodge, Rainbow Dad for the Rainbow Girls and Advisor for the DeMolays. He is Past Commander of the American Legion, a past member of the Alva Planning Commission and a past president of the Alva School Board and a member of Elks and Kiwanis.

Family Life

He was engaged to Gerree Coley and they were married in the First Methodist Church in Hollis, Oklahoma on November 24, 1955, Thanksgiving Day. Gerree Smith served as his first legal secretary.

He was <U+201C>Superman<U+201D> to his three children: Anita Lynn, Herbert Dean and Lisa Coley. The family never had a set of encyclopedia because he could answer any of their questions. He was always teaching his children and others. He was a wonderful Sunday School teacher for the Men's class and the Couple's class. When grandchildren arrived he was certain they needed to know about the 'declination of the sun'. He loved his grandchildren, Megan, Bryan and Asia and Bryan's wife, Linnea and has become very fond of his son-in-law Ed and Ed's cooking.

Interests

Playing bridge and pitch were enjoyable with club members, also the Daily Oklahoman's crossword puzzles, movies and comic strips helped make things a little more fun and interesting. He loved a challenge. Recently, his niece, Leslie, asked him to write a story about his father for her nephew, Ray, as a Christmas present in 2010. Eventually, he got everything written down and sent to her for Ray's enjoyment. He also was asked to go with certain local pilots and navigate for them. He had obtained a pilot's license himself in early years. Herb and Gerree traveled to London for Theatre Tours offered by Lawton Theatre group. Tourists asked Herb for directions in London.

Awards

In 2008, Herb received his 50 year pin from the Oklahoma Bar Association with Woods County Law Wives hosting the reception at County Court House. Judge Dean Linder made the presentation.

Illness and Death

Herbert D. Smith battled with Parkinson's disease for the last few years of his life. In May 2011, he was taken to Share Hospital in Alva, Oklahoma for cellulitis, a common skin infection caused by bacteria. Other problems arose while he was there. On June 20, 2011, he was driven to St. Mary's in Enid, Oklahoma. He had surgery the next day for adhesions in his intestines and was moved to the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) for ten days. Smith passed away on July 1, 2011.
