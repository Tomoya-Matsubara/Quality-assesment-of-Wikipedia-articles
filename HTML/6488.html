Radim Ne<U+010D>as (born 26 August 1969) is a Czech football manager and former player. He played for the four times between 1995 and 2000. Ne<U+010D>as made more than 300 top-flight appearances spanning the existence of the Czechoslovak First League and the Gambrinus liga. He became the most expensive player in the history of Czech football in 1992 when Slavia Prague signed him from Ban<U+00ED>k Ostrava for 25 million Czechoslovak koruna.

Playing career

Club

Ne<U+010D>as started his professional career at Ban<U+00ED>k Ostrava in 1987, where he played for five years.1 One of the highlights of Ne<U+010D>as' time spent at Ostrava was scoring from a free kick at Bazaly in the first round of the 1990<U+2013>91 UEFA Cup against .2 The same season, Ne<U+010D>as won the Czechoslovak Cup with Ostrava.3

Ne<U+010D>as became the most expensive player in the history of Czech football in 1992, when he left Ostrava for Slavia Prague for a fee of 25 million Czechoslovak koruna.45 Two years later, he went to Cheb and then on to Greek side Xanthi in December 1995.6 When he returned to the Czech Republic, he played for Jablonec, where he won the 1997<U+2013>98 Czech Republic Football Cup.7 He returned to Slavia in 2000, playing and scoring in the second round of the 2000<U+2013>01 UEFA Cup against OFI Crete.8 In January 2001 he signed for Slovak club Slovan Bratislava.9 He played for Bratislava for two years before returning to the Czech Republic in 2003, where he signed for Bohemian Football League team Chrudim.10

International

Ne<U+010D>as started playing for the national youth teams of Czechoslovakia in 1984, making a combined total of 47 appearances over four years at age groups from under-15 to under-18. In 1988 he made his first appearance for Czechoslovakia U21; he went on to make 28 appearances and score six goals during his four years with the team.11

He made his full international debut for the in a friendly match against on 8 May, 1995. He played in another friendly match later the same year, against , but it was 1998 before he made his third appearance, again in a friendly, this time against . His fourth and final appearance was at the 2000 Carlsberg Cup tournament in a match against .12

Management career

In the summer of 2006, Ne<U+010D>as took over at Bohemian Football League side FK Bohemians Prague (St<U+0159><U+00ED><U+017E>kov), replacing Lubo<U+0161> Urban.13 However his tenure lasted just five matches before he was dismissed, having lost only once during this period.14

Ne<U+010D>as spent some time at Liberec as assistant to manager Ladislav <U+0160>korpil until they were both sacked, along with other assistant coach Lubo<U+0161> Kozel in November 2009.15

Ne<U+010D>as went to Slovakia to manage FK Senica in February 2010 during the 2009<U+2013>10 Slovak Superliga.16 At the beginning of the 2010<U+2013>11 season he went back to the Czech Republic and joined another Bohemian Football League club, this time Arsenal <U+010C>esk<U+00E1> L<U+00ED>pa.17 Having steered the club to mid-table safety, with the club in 11th position with just two games remaining, it was announced that Ne<U+010D>as would leave <U+010C>esk<U+00E1> L<U+00ED>pa at the end of the season.18

In the summer of 2011, Ne<U+010D>as joined Sezimovo <U+00DA>st<U+00ED> in the Czech 2. Liga as assistant manager to Lubo<U+0161> Z<U+00E1>kostelsk<U+00FD>.19
