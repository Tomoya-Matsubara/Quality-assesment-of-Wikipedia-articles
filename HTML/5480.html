Warszawska Kolej Dojazdowa (Warsaw Suburb Railway, WKD) is a suburban railway in Warsaw. It is co-owned by the city of Warsaw, the Masovian Voivodship self-government and all the municipalities crossed by its line. It was set in 1927 as Elektryczna Kolej Dojazdowa (Electrical Suburb Railway), from 1951 it holds the current name. The WKD's line crosses the city of Warsaw and the following towns and municipalities: Micha<U+0142>owice, Pruszk<U+00F3>w, Brwin<U+00F3>w, Podkowa Le<U+015B>na, Milan<U+00F3>wek and Grodzisk Mazowiecki. Company's headquarters and the main technical depot are in Grodzisk Mazowiecki.
