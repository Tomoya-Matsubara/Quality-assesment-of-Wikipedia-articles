The Remix is a compilation album of remixes by American singer Lady Gaga released in Japan on March 3, 2010. A revised version is set for release in additional markets, beginning on May 3, 2010. For the remix album, Gaga collaborated with a number of producers and remixers. The album positive reviews from contemporary critics. It charted within the top five of the album charts in Belgium Falnders region, Canada, Japan and the United Kingdom, while reaching the top-twenty in others. The Remix received a gold certification from the Recording Industry Association of Japan.

Background

On April 15, 2010, The Guardian reported that Lady Gaga has collaborated with a number of artists, including Pet Shop Boys, Passion Pit and rocker Marilyn Manson, for a remix album, titled The Remix.1 The album was originally released in Japan on March 3, 2010, containing sixteen remixes of Gaga's songs.2 The revised version, consisting of seventeen remixes, was released on May 3, 2010.3 Manson features on the Chew Fu remix of "LoveGame", Passion Pit remixed "Telephone", while Pet Shop Boys remixed "Eh, Eh (Nothing Else I Can Say)". Other artists who remixed Gaga's songs included Alphabeat, Frankmusik, Stuart Price, Monarchy and Robots to Mars.4 The album was released in the United Kingdom on May 10, 2010 and featured a different artwork only for that region.5

Critical reception

Simon Cage from the Daily Express gave the album three out of five stars and said that she "has a winning way with ostentatious hats but Lady Gaga<U+2019>s true talent is actually selling the same album over and over again. [...] It<U+2019>s great but<U+2026> enough already!"6 Music critic J. D. Considine, while reviewing the album for The Globe and Mail, complimented the piano and voice version of "Poker Face" and said, "the smartest track on her newest remix album <U+2013> her second such album in nine months <U+2013> isn<U+2019>t a remix at all, but an unadorned piano-and-voice version of Poker Face that brings out her inner Elton John. And yes, this move, too, is probably just another bit of calculated image management, but that doesn<U+2019>t make it any less brilliant."7 Matthew Richardson from Prefix Magazine reviewed The Sound of Arrows remix of "Alejandro" and said that they "have been releasing material in a slow trickle, but the quality has been such that people are still paying attention. While we're all waiting for their album to drop, hopefully sometime before the world ends, this remix that the duo did of Lady Gaga's "Alejandro" has recently surfaced. The laser house drought is over, for now."8 Robert Copsey from Digital Spy commented that "The Remix feels like a fairly natural progression, as opposed to the cynical label cash-in often associated with this sort of release. What's more, it features input from a tasty mix of world-class knob-twiddlers and trendy up-and-comers, both of whom are as vital to keeping the singer's music fresh as her Haus Of GaGa designers are to her image."9

Chart performance

Following the album's release in Japan, it debuted at number nine on the Oricon Japanese Albums Chart.10 On the issue dated May 17, 2010, the album moved to a new peak of seven on Oricon Albums Chart and has been certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of Japan (RIAJ) for shipment of 100,000 copies.1112 The album also charted in Australia, Belgium (Flanders and Wallonia), the Netherlands and New Zealand.13 In Canada, the album debuted at five on the Canadian Albums Chart issue dated May 22, 2010.1415 In the United Kingdom, the album debuted at number three on the official UK Albums Chart dated May 16, 2010.16
