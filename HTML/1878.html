James William Reiher, Jr.1 (born September 1, 1971)2 is an American professional wrestler and the son of professional wrestler "Superfly" Jimmy Snuka.3 He is best known for his time in World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) under the ring names Sim Snuka and Deuce between 2007 and 2009.4

Reiher began wrestling in the late 1990s, and worked for the UWA and the Xcitement Wrestling Federation. Ater signing a contract with World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE), Reiher was assigned to WWE's developmental territory Ohio Valley Wrestling (OVW) in Louisville, Kentucky in 2005, where he wrestled under the name Deuce Shade. He was placed in tag team competition, alongside Dice Domino, and they won the OVW Southern Tag Team Championship on three occasions. It was also during this time that he and Domino, alongside their manager Cherry, were known as the group The Throw-Backs and later The Untouchables. Also at OVW, Reiher won the OVW Television Championship in one occasion in singles competition.

The group was called up to the SmackDown! roster in January 2007, and the team underwent a name change to Deuce 'n Domino. Three months later, Deuce 'n Domino went onto win the WWE Tag Team Championship. After dropping the title in August 2007, the group disbanded in June 2008, after a series of losses. The split was then further cemented when Reiher was drafted to the Raw brand as part of the 2008 WWE Supplemental Draft. During his time on Raw, Reiher expressed interest in joining Randy Orton's Legacy faction. He, however, was not permitted to join. On June 19, 2009, Reiher was released from his WWE contract.

Professional wrestling career

Early career

After deciding to becomce a professional wrestler, Reiher trained with his father, Jimmy Snuka in New Jersey.5 He then began working under the ring name Solo in the UWA in California.6 During his time in UWA, he was offered a contract by World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE), but turned it down in favor of a more lucrative contract from UWA.7 After the UWA went out of business in late 2001, Reiher began working for the Xcitement Wrestling Federation (XWF), a promotion run by Jimmy Hart, The Nasty Boys, and Greg Valentine.8 In XWF, he used the name Jimmy Snuka, Jr., and was managed by his father.910 He appeared at the first round of XWF tapings in November 2001, defeating Buddy Lee Parker and Vapor in two separate matches.11

World Wrestling Entertainment (2005<U+2013>2009)

Ohio Valley Wrestling (2005<U+2013>2006)

Reiher, as "Deuce Shade", made his debut in Ohio Valley Wrestling (OVW) on January 27, 2005, defeating Brent Albright.12 On May 24, 2005, he defeated Chad Wicks in an OVW Television Championship tournament match. He later went on to beat Ken Doane in the tournament finals for the OVW Television Championship on June 7.13 He ended up losing it to Doane later that month.14 On August 24, Shade won a number one contenders battle royal to earn the right to face OVW Champion Johnny Jeter later that night, but he lost this match.15

In January 2006, Shade started teaming with Domino Dice, calling themselves "The Throw-Backs".16 It was at this time that Cherry Pie started accompanying them to the ring, as their manager.17 They later changed their name to "The Untouchables", and removed the Shade, Dice, and Pie parts of their respective names. On March 19, Reiher defeated Mike Mizanin to win the OVW Southern Tag Team Championship for his team.18 The team lost their titles on April 5, in a three-way match to Roadkill and K.C James; the match also involved Kenny and Mikey of the Spirit Squad.19

In mid-August 2006, The Untouchables quickly became embroiled in a scripted rivalry with Cody Runnels and Shawn Spears over the OVW Southern Tag Team Championship.2021 Runnels and Spears captured the championship from The Untouchables first on October 18.22 In November 2006, Runnels and Spears defeated The Untouchables in a tag team match, and won the services of Cherry, The Untouchables' manager.23 On November 29, the OVW Southern Tag Team Championship was vacated after a match between The Untouchables and Runnels and Spears ended in a draw.24 The following week, a rematch between the two teams was held for the vacant championship.25 During this match Cherry turned on Spears and Runnels, re-joining The Untouchables and helping them regain the OVW Southern Tag Team Championship.2627 Two weeks later, on December 20, Runnels and Spears defeated The Untouchables in a Street Fight to win the championship.28

SmackDown! (2007<U+2013>2008)

On the January 19, 2007 episode of SmackDown!, the team, now using the name "Deuce 'n Domino", along with Cherry, debuted with their 1950s greaser in-ring persona.29 They quickly defeated a team of jobbers<U+2014>wrestlers who consistently lose to make their opponents look stronger<U+2014>and did the same to another jobber team the next week.3031 On the February 2 episode of SmackDown!, they defeated WWE Tag Team Champions Paul London and Brian Kendrick in a non-title match.32 They earned a chance at the championship at No Way Out, but failed to win the title.33 On the April 13 episode of SmackDown!, Deuce and Domino got a chance at the tag titles again, but lost after Deuce got them disqualified.34 The next week on SmackDown!, they defeated London and Kendrick to win the tag team titles.35 On the May 25 episode of SmackDown!, London and Kendrick competed against William Regal and Dave Taylor, but Deuce and Domino interfered, resulting in Regal and Taylor being disqualified.36 This made both teams the number one contender, which led to a triple threat tag match the next week where Deuce and Domino retained the titles.37 On June 24, Deuce and Domino issued an open challenge at the pay-per-view event, Vengeance, which was accepted by Jimmy Snuka (Deuce's father) and Sgt. Slaughter.38 Deuce and Domino retained their title.39

On the June 29 episode of SmackDown!, Domino suffered an injury during a match with Cryme Tyme, sidelining him for a month.40 After Domino's return, the team began a scripted rivalry with Batista and Ric Flair.4142 On the August 31 episode of SmackDown!, he and Domino lost their tag team title to Montel Vontavious Porter (MVP) and Matt Hardy.4344 They engaged in a rivalry with MVP and Hardy, and attempted to regain their championship, but were unsuccessful.454647

Throughout late 2007 and early 2008, Deuce 'n Domino wrestled sporadically, facing teams such as Jimmy Wang Yang and Shannon Moore, Jesse and Festus, and Finlay and Hornswoggle.484950 On the May 23 episode of SmackDown, Deuce and Domino parted ways with Cherry and replaced her with WWE Diva Maryse.51 After a series of losses, Deuce attacked Domino on the June 20, 2008 episode of SmackDown to end their partnership.52

Raw and departure (2008<U+2013>2009)

As part of the 2008 WWE Supplemental Draft, Deuce was drafted to the Raw brand.53 He made his debut for the brand on the September 22 airing of Raw, losing to Santino Marella.54 On the December 15, 2008 episode of Raw, Deuce announced that his new ring name would be "Sim Snuka", after revealing his father is Jimmy Snuka, and faced Rey Mysterio in a losing attempt; after Mysterio fell into Cody Rhodes and Manu, who were at ringside, they attacked Mysterio, and the match ended in a disqualification.55 Two weeks later, he defeated Charlie Haas (who was dressed as Snuka's father) to pass step one towards earning a spot in Randy Orton's Legacy faction.56 A week later, Snuka teamed with Rhodes against Cryme Tyme, with Rhodes getting the pinfall.57 It was revealed after the match that only the man who got the pinfall would advance into the group, leaving Snuka out.58 He, along with Manu, brought back Ted DiBiase to help them attack The Legacy, only to be betrayed and attacked by DiBiase, Orton and Rhodes.59

Following this, Snuka appeared rarely.60 His last WWE appearance was at WrestleMania XXV, where he posed as an unnamed cameraman during the match between Shawn Michaels and The Undertaker, and failed to catch The Undertaker during a dangerous suicide dive spot during the bout.61 Although he was rumored to be one of the commentators for WWE's new Superstars show, he was released from his WWE contract on June 19, 2009.6263

Personal life

Prior to getting in the wrestling business, he worked as a youth counselor and a TV cameraman in Utah.64 Reiher is a former Mr. Hawaiian Islands in bodybuilding.65

He is a second generation wrestler; his father James Reiher wrestled using the name Jimmy Snuka.66 Reiher's cousins include Dwayne Johnson and Solofa Fatu, who wrestled as The Rock and Rikishi respectively.67
