Alison Clare Quinn, OAM1 is a Paralympic athletics competitor from New South Wales, Australia. She won two gold medals in the 1992 Barcelona Games in the Women's 100 m C7<U+2013>8 and Women's 200 m C7<U+2013>8 events, for which she received a Medal of the Order of Australia.23 As a gold medalist in the Barcelona games she was recognized for service to sport. In the 1996 Atlanta Games, she won a bronze medal in the Women's 100 m T36<U+2013>37 event.4. T36 <U+2013> 37 is a classification which refers to athletes with cerebral palsy. The Atlanta games began on the 16th of August and ended on the 25th of August. It was the first Paralympics game to get mass sponsorship. Its budget was eighty one million US dollars. She won a gold medal with a world record time at the 2000 Sydney Games in the women's 100 m T38 event and a silver medal in the women's 200 m T38 event.5 The 2000 Paralympics Games that took place in Sydney started on the 18th of October with an opening ceremony and completed on the 29th of October. They were the 11th Paralympics games. Approximately 3800 athletes took part in the games and it was the second biggest sporting event in Australia<U+2019>s history. In the year 2000 Quinn received an Australian Sports Medal which was an award given in the year 2000 to recognize high achievers in Australian sports.

Personal

Quinn was born with cerebral palsy (which is categorized by its affect on a persons physical movements) and has hemiplegia on the left side of her body.67 At the age of two she began gymnastics to increase coordination and symmetry.8 She now trains in various sports including but not limited to swimming, weights, and track work at the Sydney Academy of Sport. 9

Career

Quinn is currently employed as a part time coach of gymnastics as well as endeavoring to make a career of motivational speaking and increasing awareness of disabled sport in the community.10 In December 2000 Quinn was a quest speaker at the Penford Australia Ltd Oxley High School sports awards.11 Oxley High School is in North Tamworth, Tamworth in New South Wales and has approximately 1200 students.12 Her role was to give congratulations and awards to various achievers involved in sports. Such awards include for the <U+2018>Sport star of the year<U+2019>, volleyball, athletics, water polo, BMX, gymnastics and tennis. Also in the category of sports administration are coach/manager, junior coach, outstanding junior sports person, team, and recognition of refereeing.13

Training

Quinn was trained by Jacqueline Byrnes who was a national level athlete in the 1960s. Like Alison Quinn, Jacqueline Byrnes also received an Order of Australia medal for her service to athletics.14 The Order of Australia medal was established by Queen Elizabeth the second for the recognition of Australian citizens for outstanding achievements.15

Recognition

In 2000, she received an Australian Sports Medal.16 Lists of her sporting achievements include receiving two gold medals in 1992 in athletics. One of which was in the women<U+2019>s 100 meter sprint (C7-8) and the other was the women<U+2019>s 200 meter sprint. 17 Competing in athletics in 1996 in the women<U+2019>s 200 meter sprint (T34-37) and obtaining a bronze medal in the women<U+2019>s 100 meter sprint (T36-37). She also participated in the women<U+2019>s 400 meter (T38) sprint, received a gold medal in the 100 meter (T38) and silver in the women<U+2019>s 200 meter (T38) in the year 2000.18
