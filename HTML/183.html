Flaming Pie is the tenth studio album by Paul McCartney, first released in 1997. His first studio album in over four years, it was mostly recorded following McCartney's involvement in the highly successful Beatles Anthology project.1 The album was recorded in several locations over two years, 1995 and 1997, featuring two songs dating from 1992. The album featured several of McCartney's family members and friends. In Flaming Pie's liner notes, McCartney said: "[The Beatles Anthology] reminded me of The Beatles' standards and the standards that we reached with the songs. So in a way it was a refresher course that set the framework for this album."2

Flaming Pie peaked at number 2 in both the UK and US, and was certified gold. The album, which was well received by critics, also reached the Top 20 in many other countries. From its release up to mid-2007, the album sold over 1.5 million copies.

Background

"Calico Skies",{{#tag:ref|"Calico Skies" was written when McCartney tried to write something similar to the Beatles' "Blackbird".3|group="nb"}} which Paul McCartney had written when Hurricane Bob had hit while McCartney was staying on Long Island in 1991,45 and "Great Day", which features backing vocal from his wife Linda McCartney,6 hailed from a 1992 session,{{#tag:ref|Also recorded during this session is the unreleased song "When Winter Comes".7|group="nb"}}8 recorded even before Off the Ground had come out. Starting from the mid-1990s for four years, McCartney was involved in The Beatles Anthology, a documentary on the history of the Beatles.9 During 1995, as the Anthology albums were starting to be released over a two-year period, EMI did not want McCartney to release a solo album in the meantime.10 McCartney said that he "was almost insulted at first" before then realising that "it would be silly to go out against yourself in the form of the Beatles. So I fell in with the idea and thought, 'Great, I don't even have to think about an album.'"11 McCartney was occupied with working on Standing Stone in the interim.12

Recording and structure

Beginning in February 1995, McCartney teamed up with Jeff Lynne,13 Electric Light Orchestra lead singer and guitarist, an ardent Beatles fan. Lynne had previously worked with former Beatle George Harrison on his 1987 album Cloud Nine and also co-produced "Free as a Bird" and "Real Love" for the Anthology project. Intending to produce something pure and easy <U+2014> and without elaborate productions <U+2014> McCartney sporadically recorded the entire album in a space of two years, working not only with Lynne, but with Steve Miller,{{#tag:ref|Miller and McCartney had worked together once before, on 1969's "My Dark Hour" at Abbey Road Studios.14 After finding out his son, James, was a fan of Miller, McCartney decided to renew their friendship.15|group="nb"}} George Martin, Ringo Starr and his own son, James McCartney,16 who plays lead guitar on "Heaven on a Sunday".17 McCartney wrote the song "Young Boy" while his wife Linda was making lunch for a New York Times feature on 18 August 1994.18 McCartney and Miller started recording "Young Boy" on 22 February 1995 in Sun Valley, Idaho.19 They reconvened a few months afterwards in May at McCartney's home studio, The Mill, recording <U+2013> a song described as a "road song" <U+2013> "If You Wanna" and the jam track "Used to Be Bad" in the process.2021

The duo also recorded the B-side "Broomstick" and three unreleased tracks: "(Sweet Home) Country Girl", "Soul Boy", and an untitled song.22 Also in May, McCartney, by himself, recorded the unreleased tracks "Stella May Day", for his daughter Stella McCartney, which would be used playing over loudspeakers at her fashion shows, and "Whole Life" with Dave Stewart.{{#tag:ref|McCartney would later re-recorded "Whole Life" in 2003.23|group="nb"}}24 "Somedays", which was written while McCartney was escorting Linda to Kent for a photo shoot,25 features an orchestration score by George Martin.2627 "The Song We Were Singing",{{#tag:ref|McCartney plays a stand-up bass on the track. The bass originally belonged to Elvis Presley's bassist, Bill Black.28|group="nb"}} which was about the times McCartney and his former-songwriting partner John Lennon were at 20 Forthlin Road,29 was recorded in 3/4 time.30 "Little Willow" was written for the children31 of Starr's first wife, Maureen Starkey Tigrett, who had recently died of cancer.32 "Souvenir" features the sound of a 78 rpm record towards the end of the track.33 The title track, recorded in a four-hour session,34 is in similar style to the Beatles' "Lady Madonna".35

In May 1996, Starr and McCartney were working on a track that McCartney had started a decade previously, "Beautiful Night",3637 which featured vocals from Starr.38 Lynne showed up the next day and the trio, with McCartney on bass, Starr on drums, and Lynne on guitar, jammed, with the finished results being the track "Really Love You", the first track credited to McCartney<U+2013>Starkey.3940 McCartney and Starr also recorded the B-side "Looking for You" and an untitled song.41 "Heaven on a Sunday", which was written while McCartney was in the US sailing on holiday, was recorded on 16 September 1996, and features backing vocals by both Linda and James.42 Martin added orchestration to "Beautiful Night",43 on 14 February 1997 at Abbey Road Studios.44 An unreleased song was recorded with Lynne producing, titled "Cello in the Ruins",45 had its copyright registered in 1994,46 despite work on the song only getting started a year later, in May 1995.47 The track was nearly issued as a single for War Child's The Help Album in 1995, but ultimately shelved.48 This album was the last McCartney studio album to feature vocals and participation from Linda,49 who died of breast cancer in 1998.50

Release and reception

Upon its 1997 release, on 5 May in the UK on Parlophone{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone 724385650024/CDPCSD 17151|group="nb"}} and on 20 May in the US on Capitol,{{#tag:ref|US Capitol CDP 7243 8 56500 2 452|group="nb"}}53 the critical reaction to Flaming Pie was very strong, with McCartney achieving his best reviews since 1982's Tug of War. With fresh credibility even with young fans who had been introduced to him through the Anthology project,54 and anticipation raised with the excellent reviews, Flaming Pie debuted at number 2 in the UK in May, giving McCartney his best new entry since Flowers in the Dirt eight years before. It was kept off the top spot there by the Spice Girls' album Spice.55 In the US, reaction was also very positive.56 The album debuted at number 2 with 121,000 copies sold in its first week, also behind the Spice album, which sold only 16,500 more copies that week.5758

In both the UK and the US, Flaming Pie was the most commercially successful new entry, and also reached gold in both countries. It was also certified gold in Norway.59 According to Nielsen SoundScan, the album had sold over 1.5 million copies worldwide up to June 2007.60 The singles "Young Boy",{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone CDRS 6462/72438839512061|group="nb"}}{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone RP 6462/7243 8837867362|group="nb"}} "The World Tonight"{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone CDRS 6472/7243 8 84298 2 563|group="nb"}}{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone PP 6472/7243 8 84298 7 064|group="nb"}} and "Beautiful Night",{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone 7243 8 84921 2 665|group="nb"}}{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone RP 6489/7243 8 84970 7 766|group="nb"}} all of which were released as picture discs, became UK hits, all making the top 40 in the sales charts. The only single in the US from the album was "The World Tonight",{{#tag:ref|US Capitol C2 7243 8 58650 2 267|group="nb"}} released on 17 April 1997,68 a top 30 entry on the Billboard mainstream rock listing.69 The album was also released on vinyl.{{#tag:ref|UK Parlophone 7243 8 56500 1 7/PCSD17170|group="nb"}}{{#tag:ref|US Capitol C1 7243 8 56500 1 771|group="nb"}} To promote the album, McCartney held an online chat party, and the event entered the Guinness Book of World Records for the most people in an online chatroom at once.72

In the World Tonight, a film about the making of the album, was broadcast in the UK on ITV, and on VH1 in the US, around the release of the album.73 Also broadcast was an hour-long radio show about the album on 5 May 1997 on BBC Radio 2.74 It received a Grammy nomination for Album of the Year, although Bob Dylan won the award with his back-to-form album Time Out of Mind.75 "Young Boy" and "The World Tonight" appeared in the 1997 Ivan Reitman comedy Fathers' Day.76 The title Flaming Pie (also given to one of the album's songs) is a reference to a humorous story John Lennon told in a story in Mersey Beat in 1961 on the origin of the Beatles' name:7778
