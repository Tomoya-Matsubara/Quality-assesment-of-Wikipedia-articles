Andrea Reimer is a Canadian politician, currently serving on Vancouver, British Columbia's City Council. She was first elected in 2002 to the Vancouver School Board as a Green Party candidate.1 She was defeated as a Green Party candidate in her re-election campaign in 20052 and then joined the Vision Vancouver party to support Gregor Robertson's mayoral campaign.3 She subsequently ran for and won a council seat in the 2008 municipal election.4

Background

Reimer grew up with adversity,56 a background she shares openly to inspire others,7 and prompt concern about homeless people.8

Reimer was born in Saskatoon and put into foster care.91011 When she was 8 months old she was adopted by a couple from Vancouver12 who subsequently relocated to Calgary13 prior to her starting school.1415 Reimer's adoptive parents divorced when she was a pre-teen.16

Reimer smoked her first cigarette and took her first drink at the age of 10, and started to experiment with illicit drugs at the age of 11,17 and LSD18 at the age of 12.19 She drifted into a life on the streets202122 from the ages of 14-1923 which included extensive worldwide travel to places throughout Canada, the United States, Asia, Central America, Europe and North Africa.2425 During this period she dumpster-dived,2627 got arrested several times,2829 got involved in hard drugs30 and developed addictions.31 When she was 18, Reimer worked in El Salvador at a low income housing project, which she later cited as the catalyst for her environmental awareness.32

Although an atheist, at 19 Reimer enrolled at Concordia University to major in religious studies.33 She subsequently dropped out during her first year,3435 after six weeks of classes,36 and moved to Vancouver with her boyfriend at the time, who was a cocaine addict.3738 He kicked the habit after arriving in Vancouver.39 In Vancouver Reimer joined a government sponsored job training program and started working with Gordon Neighbourhood House Youth Works. She was then sent for a practicum to the Western Canada Wilderness Committee.4041 After Reimer started working her boyfriend fell back into the cocaine addiction and they broke up.42

Reimer eventually worked her way up to become the executive director of the Western Canada Wilderness Committee,43444546 where she was paid approximately $2000 a month.47 While executive director of the Wilderness Committee, Reimer supported the creation of a carbon tax by the BC Provincial Government.48 It was subsequently passed.49 In 2007, she was chosen by Al Gore to deliver the Inconvenient Truth presentation to local audiences,50515253 which she delivered to over 10,000 people.54

Reimer has volunteered with many community organizations including serving as:

  • Past President of the CCEC Credit Union 55
  • Past President Canadian Women<U+2019>s Voters Congress 56
  • former Vice-Chair of Your Local Farmer<U+2019>s Market Society 57
  • former Co-Chair of the City of Vancouver<U+2019>s Food Policy Council 5859
  • Director of Toxic Free Canada 60

She currently serves as a Director of Environmental Education Action Project,61 and sits on the Vancouver Foundation<U+2019>s Health and Social Development Advisory Committee.62

Political career

Green Party

Reimer joined the Green Party in 1996, and volunteered on the 1996 provincial election, the 1999 civic election, the 2000 federal by-election and the 2001 provincial election.63 She has held internal elected office as Communication Chair, Green Party of BC (1999<U+2013>2002) and Chair, Green Party of Vancouver (2002<U+2013>2008). Reimer was the first Green Party candidate ever elected to a school board seat in Canada, when she was elected in 2002.646566 She ran for re-election as a Green Party candidate in 2005 yet was defeated.67

Vision Vancouver

Reimer joined the Vision Vancouver party to support Gregor Robertson's mayoral campaign,68 which she co-chaired. She subsequently ran for697071 and won a council seat in the 2008 municipal election.72

School Board

As a School Trustee, Reimer supported efforts to remove vending machines73 and corporate logos from schools.74 She pushed for getting school garden programs into schools.75

In January 2003, she supported the Green Party's position against bringing the Olympics to Vancouver in 2010 due to concerns about the money it would require.76 In 2008 she characterized her position as still ambivalent about the money spent on the Olympics but prepared to put her full energy into ensuring the Games were as good as possible.7778

City Council

As City Councillor, Reimer is a member of Gregor Robertson's Greenest City Team.79 The team is tasked with catalyzing immediate action on green issues, a ten year action plan to become the world's greenest city and creating an environment supportive of green economic development in the city of Vancouver.80 Reimer introduced a motion, which passed, to open Vancouver city data to the public and endorse the principles of open source.818283 Reimer introduced a motion, which passed unanimously, to allow Vancouver residents to keep hens in their backyards.84 The motion was opposed by the British Columbia Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals, the Vancouver Humane Society, and the BC Poultry Association.85 The proposal was brought forward as a result of a recommendation from the City of Vancouver's Food Policy Council.86

Reimer's other appointments on Vancouver City Council include:

  • Chair, Standing Committee on Planning and Environment87
  • Vancouver Economic Development Commission88
  • Joint Childcare Council89
  • Family Court-Youth Justice Committee90

Reimer also sits on the Metro Vancouver Regional District board91 and has been appointed to the Regional Planning Committee,92 Agricultural Committee,93 Electoral Area A Committee94 and GVRD/UBC Joint Committee.95

Twitter controversy

In October 2009, Reimer posted on Twitter regarding the British Columbia Minister of Housing and Social Development Rich Coleman's weight96 in response to provincial legislation proposed by Coleman to bring homeless people to shelters during extreme weather, even against their will.97 Reimer posted that instead of police bringing homeless people to shelters during extreme weather that she was thinking of introducing legislation to have the police bring Coleman to Jenny Craig, an international weight loss company, on his next visit to Vancouver.9899100 Coleman responded by calling the comment amateurish and from a Councilor that "doesn't know any better."101 Reimer later apologized for the posting.102

Family

Reimer and her husband have a daughter.103 They live near Trout Lake in East Vancouver.104
