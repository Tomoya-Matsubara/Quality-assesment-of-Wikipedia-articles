Guan Hanqing () (c. 1225-1302), sobriquet "the Oldman of the Studio" (<U+9F4B><U+53DF> Zh<U+0101>is<U+01D2>u), was a notable Chinese playwright and poet in the Yuan Dynasty.

Biography

Guan was born in the capital city of the Yuan Dynasty, Dadu and produced about 65 plays, mostly in the vernacular of the time. He has been considered as one of the Four Great Yuan Playwrights, the other three being Ma Zhiyuan, Bai Renfu, and Zheng Guangzu. Fourteen of his plays are extant, including:

  • Saving the Dusty-windy a.k.a. Saving the Prostitute a.k.a. Rescued by a Coquette (<U+8D99><U+76FC><U+5152><U+98A8><U+6708><U+6551><U+98A8><U+5875> Zh<U+00E0>o P<U+00E0>n <U+00C9>r F<U+0113>ng Yu<U+00E8> Ji<U+00F9> F<U+0113>ng Ch<U+00E9>n)
  • The Conference of a Single Dao a.k.a. Meeting the Enemies Alone a.k.a. Lord Guan Goes to the Feast (<U+95DC><U+5927><U+738B><U+7368><U+8D74><U+55AE><U+5200><U+6703> Gu<U+0101>n D<U+00E0> W<U+00E1>ng D<U+00FA> F<U+00F9> D<U+0101>n D<U+0101>o Hu<U+00EC>)
