Cristina Berm<U+00FA>dez1 (pronunciation: [kristina be<U+027E>mude<U+03B8>]) was a Princess of Le<U+00F3>n and Galicia. She is also known as Cristina de Le<U+00F3>n.2

Biography

Cristina was a daughter of King Bermudo II the Gouty and his first wife, Queen Velasquita Ram<U+00ED>rez.345

Her grandparents were King Ordo<U+00F1>o III of Le<U+00F3>n

  • Rodr<U+00ED>guez Fern<U+00E1>ndez, Justiniano: Ordo<U+00F1>o III Ediciones Leonesas, S.A.(1982) ISBN 84-86013-04-6 and Queen Urraca Fern<U+00E1>ndez.6

Cristina<U+2019>s stepmother was Elvira Garc<U+00ED>a and her half-siblings were Alfonso the Noble, Teresa and Sancha.7 She was an aunt of Princess Sancha of Le<U+00F3>n.

In 1010, Cristina married Prince Ordo<U+00F1>o Ram<U+00ED>rez, son of Ramiro III of Le<U+00F3>n8 and Sancha G<U+00F3>mez. They had children:

  • Don Alfonso Ord<U+00F3><U+00F1>ez, who had a daughter called Cristina
  • Do<U+00F1>a Aldonza Ord<U+00F3><U+00F1>ez, wife of Don Pelayo Froilaz
  • Don Ordo<U+00F1>o Ord<U+00F3><U+00F1>ez, named after his father
  • Do<U+00F1>a Pelaya Ord<U+00F3><U+00F1>ez910

After her husband died, Cristina become a nun and lived in Monasterio de San Salvador.
