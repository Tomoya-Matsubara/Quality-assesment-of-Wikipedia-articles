Maryland Route 159 (MD 159) is a state highway in the U.S. state of Maryland. The route runs from a dead end at Bush River near Perryman north to U.S. Route 40 in Aberdeen.

Route description

Maryland Route 159 begins at a dead end at Bush River, an estuary that flows into Chesapeake Bay, as Perryman Road, a two-lane road through a forested area with a speed limit of . The road parallels the Amtrak Northeast Corridor railroad tracks as it passes Clubhouse Road, where the speed limit increases to , and Forest Greens Road, which provides access to Forest Greens Country Club. As MD 159 passes Canning House Road, the road heads away from the railroad tracks. The speed limit drops to as the road enters the village center of Perryman, where the route encounters Michaelsville Road. A left turn at a stop sign at Chelsea Road, which branches to the south, is required to remain on MD 159.

After the intersection with Chelsea Road, MD 159 enters an area of scattered residences with several entrances to distribution centers. After passing Spesutia Road and the Aberdeen Fire Department, the speed limit increases to . After passing Cranberry Road, another left turn is required to continue on MD 159 when it meets Old Philadelphia Road at a stop sign. Old Philadelphia Road is an old alignment of Maryland Route 7 and, before that, U.S. Route 40. Shortly after the turn onto Old Philadelphia Road, MD 159 ends at U.S. 40. Old Philadelphia Road continues on the other side of the intersection as MD 7.

History

The Perryman Road section of Maryland Route 159 was paved to Perryman by 1930 and completed to Bush River by 1933.12 The Old Philadelphia Road section was paved by 1927 as it was then U.S. Route 40.3 The 1939 Maryland state highway map shows MD 159 following the Perryman Road stretch.4 The Old Philadelphia Road stretch was MD 7, a number assigned when U.S. 40 was moved to the new Pulaski Highway. MD 159 replaced MD 7 on that length of Old Philadelphia Road in 1950.5
