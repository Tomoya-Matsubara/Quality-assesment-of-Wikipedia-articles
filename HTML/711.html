Reginald C. Lindsay (born 1945) is a United States federal judge.

Lindsay was born in Birmingham, Alabama. He received a B.A. from Morehouse College in 1967. He received a J.D. from Harvard Law School in 1970. He was in private practice of law in Boston, Massachusetts from 1970 to 1975. He was a State commissioner of public utilities, Massachusetts from 1975 to 1977. He was in private practice of law in Boston, Massachusetts from 1977 to 1993.

Lindsay was a federal judge to the United States District Court for the District of Massachusetts. Lindsay was nominated by President William J. Clinton on October 27, 1993, to a seat vacated by David Sutherland Nelson. He was confirmed by the United States Senate on November 20, 1993, and received commission on November 24, 1993.
