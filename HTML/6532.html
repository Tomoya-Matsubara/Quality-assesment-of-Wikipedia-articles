Chorsu ( and ) in Samarkand, Uzbekistan, is an old domed bazaar building, constructed in the 15th century, rebuilt in the 18th century. It is located near the Registan, on its northeast side. 1

The main bazaar has already moved to Siyob Bazaar near Bibi-Khanym Mosque. Chorsu has recently been converted into an art gallery. "Chorsu" is a word from the Persian language, meaning a "crossroad". 2
