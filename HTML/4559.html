Weeraketiya is a small town in Hambantota District, Southern Province in southern Sri Lanka. It is known for being the birthplace of Mahinda Rajapaksa.1 The town belongs to Weeraketiya Divisional Secretariat.

History

The St. Joseph's Estate operated in Weeraketiya in colonial times and produced tomatoes and coconuts.2 In 1969 the Germans funded the installation of medium-wave 50 kilowatt transmitters at Maho and Weeraketiya.3 It was installed by the first quarter of 1970.4 In April 2009, a pastor in Weeraketiya was harassed and threatened by four people, leading to the arrest of one individual.5

Landmarks

The town lies around the Udakiriwila Tank (lake). The Seegala Temple of Pillars lies near its western bank. The town contains the Rajapaksha Central College and a National Youth Corps Training Center. To the south of the tank is Weeraketiya Hospital, Singhapura Mithrathwa National School and the Teacher Training Center and Weerakatiya Rajapaksha Central College.6
