Jenaro Flores Santos (b. September 19, 1941, Antipampa1), a Bolivian trade union leader and politician. Flores Santos was the founder of the CSUTCB. He also played a prominent role in formulating katarismo as a distinct trend in the social and political struggles in Bolivia.2

Flores Santos did his military service at the Waldo Ballivi<U+00E1>n Regiment in 1965. During his military service he witnessed to the suppression of the popular militias (created after the 1952 revolution), an event that impacted his political orientation. Later he begun studies at the Faculty of Law at the Universidad Mayor de San Andr<U+00E9>s. There he founded, along with other students from his home province, the Juli<U+00E1>n Apaza University Movement (MUJA).3 From 1968, Flores Santos emerged as the leader of the La Paz-based Aymara nucleus of peasant activists that sought to challenge the Military-Peasant Pact.4

In January 1969, Flores Santos became the local head of the trade union in Antipampa, and two months later he became the union head for the Aroma province. In June 1969 he became the executive secretary of the La Paz Department Farmer-Labourers Federation (FDTCLP).5 In connection with his arrival to the FDTCLP leadership, the name "Tupaj Katari" was added the the name of the organization.6

In 1970 Flores Santos took part in agrarian struggles in the La Paz Department, with seizures of land areas. These moves were later legalized through an agrarian reform the same year.7 In August 1971, at its sixth congress (organized by the government), Flores Santos was elected executive secretary of the National Farmer-Labourer Confederation of Bolivia (Confederaci<U+00F3>n Nacional de Trabajadores Campesinos de Bolivia, CNTCB).8

In 1971 Flores Santos was forced into exile to Chile. He returned the next year, to organize underground resistance against the military government.9

In 1978 he was one of the founders of the Tupaj Katari Revolutionary Movement (MRTK).10

On June 26, 1979 the CSTUCB was founded with Flores Santos as its founding executive secretary. In November 1979 he led struggles against a new military coup, organizing blockades of highways across the country. During the military government of Garc<U+00ED>a Meza, he served as the clandestine executive secretary of the Central Obrera Boliviana (COB).11 The coup of Garc<U+00ED>a Meza had caught the COB leadership by surpsrise, and only Flores Santos who had left a COB leadership meeting to make a phone-call had escaped arrest.12 This was the first time a peasant leader was in charge of COB.13 His tenure as COB leader ended after being shot by a military patrol on June 18, 1981, after which he was paralyzed.1415

In the 1980s, the katarista movement was divided into two political parties. Flores Santos became the leader of one of them, FULKA.16
