Emeel Badie Salem, Jr. (born February 11, 1985 in Birmingham, Alabama) is a professional baseball player in the Tampa Bay Rays organization. He bats and throws left-handed, and primarily plays center field. Salem attended the University of Alabama from 2004 to 2007 and received many awards while playing for the Alabama Crimson Tide baseball team. He was also awarded for his achievements as a student-athlete, including becoming the first college baseball player to win the Lowe's Senior CLASS Award in 2007 after achieving a 3.85 grade point average from his freshman to senior years in college. He was drafted by the Rays in the 2007 Major League Baseball Draft. Although Salem has never played in Major League Baseball, he has played for the Class-A Short-Season Hudson Valley Renegades (2007), the Class-A Columbus Catfish (2008), the Class-A Advanced Charlotte Stone Crabs (2009), and the Double-A Montgomery Biscuits (2010) in the minors. Over his career, he has primarily played center field, however, Salem has played left field, right field and designated hitter.

Amateur career

Salem attended Mountain Brook High School in Mountain Brook, Alabama and later the University of Alabama. During his freshman season at Alabama, Salem batted .304 with 16 runs, 21 hits, two doubles, one home run, 11 runs batted in (RBIs) and five stolen bases in 41 games. In 2005, Salem's sophomore season, he batted .277 with 31 runs, 56 hits, four doubles, three triples, 14 RBIs, 13 stolen bases and seven caught stealing in 59 games. On May 1, 2006, during his junior season, Salem won the Louisville Slugger National Player of the Week awarded by Collegiate Baseball Newspaper, and was named Southeastern Conference (SEC) Player of the Week.1 At the end of the season, Salem was awarded the Southeastern Conference Scholar-Athlete of the Year award as voted upon by the 12 SEC head coaches.2 As of May 23, 2006. he was double majoring in marketing and Spanish and sustained a 3.9 grade point average.3 From 2004 to 2006, Salem made the SEC Academic Honor Roll and in 2006 was selected to the coach's First-Team All-SEC team.4 On the 2006 season, he Salem batted .356 with 67 runs, 101 hits, 14 doubles, seven triples, two home runs, 32 RBIs, and 36 stolen bases. In the 2006 Major League Baseball Draft, Salem was drafted by the Baltimore Orioles in the tenth round, but did not sign.5 On June 13, 2006, Salem and teammate Wade LeBlanc were named to the American Baseball Coaches Association (ABCA) First-Team All-South Region team.6

Before his senior season in 2007, Salem was named to the Brooks Wallace Award and Dick Howser Trophy watch list.7 He was also named a pre-season All-American by the College Baseball Foundation, Rivals.com and the National Collegiate Baseball Writers Association.8 On his academic success, Salem commented, "I really do take pride in my grades...When a lot of guys go out and everything, I'm in the library studying. So, I definitely make the sacrifices."9 On his 22 birth day, Salem hit two home runs in his first two at-bats in a game against Georgia State University.10 In 2007, Salem was nominated by the University of Alabama for the H. Boyd McWhorter Scholar-Athlete Post-Graduate Scholarship and received the Algernon Sydney Sullivan Award after sustaining a 3.85 grade point average after his senior year.11 He later won the H. Boyd McWhorter Scholar-Athlete Post-Graduate Scholarship, which was awarded to him at the annual SEC spring meeting in Destin, Florida, on May 31, 2007.12 On April 17, 2007, it was announced that Salem, along with 30 other college baseball players, were finalists for the Lowe's Senior CLASS Award.13 Salem was named to the ESPN the Magazine's First-Team Academic All-American Team, making it his second consecutive season to receive that honor.14 Salem won the inaugural Lowe's Senior CLASS Award after beating out 29 other finalists.15 Salem was named to the National Collegiate Baseball Writers Association All-American Team after the 2007 season.16 On the 2007 season, his final at Alabama, Salem batted .351 with 61 runs, 81 hits, seven doubles, three triples, seven home runs, 32 RBIs, and 23 stolen bases in 57 games.

Professional career

The Tampa Bay Devil Rays (now known as the Tampa Bay Rays) drafted Salem in the sixth round of the 2007 Major League Baseball Draft.17 Salem began his professional career with the Class-A Short-Season Hudson Valley Renegades. With the Renegades, he batted .311 with 41 runs, 70 hits, 11 doubles, seven triples, one home run, 23 runs batted in (RBIs) and 28 stolen bases in 58 games. Salem was tied for second in the New York <U+2013> Penn League in triples and was third in stolen bases.18 He was selected to the New York <U+2013> Penn League Post-Season All-Star team after the season.19 In 2008, Salem spent the entire season with the Class-A Columbus Catfish. During the month of April, the Tampa Bay Rays named Salem the Player of the Month in their minor league organization.20 On May 16, 2008, Salem was placed on the disabled list after he broke his elbow from sliding head-first into second base.21 His injury was season ending and he spent the rest of the season rehabbing at Naimoli Complex in St. Petersburg, Florida.22 In 38 games, Salem batted .301 with 24 runs, 26 hits, four doubles, three triples, seven RBIs, and 25 stolen bases. Salem began the season with the Class-A Advanced Charlotte Stone Crabs. On the season, Salem batted .255 with 54 runs, 91 hits, 12 doubles, seven triples, 22 RBIs, and 24 stolen bases in 99 games. He led the team in triples.23 Salem was assigned to the Double-A Montgomery Biscuits where he currently plays.

Other activities

Salem led the Pinnacle Baseball Camp, a camp for baseball players in grades kindergarten to eighth grade.24 Along with Salem, other professional players instructed the camp including second baseman Matt Downs and shortstop Greg Paiml.25

Personal

Salem's father, Emeel Salem, Sr., was drafted by the Chicago Cubs out of high school but chose to attended the University of South Alabama where he played baseball under coach Eddie Stanky.26 Salem is a concert pianist and speaks several different languages.27 Salem is also related to football players Ed Salem, George Salem, Sr., George Salem, Jr. and Jimbo Salem.2829
