Jordan David Kovacs (born June 12, 1990) is an American football safety who played his redshirt sophomore season in 2010 for the Michigan Wolverines football team. He was a walk-on who earned 2009 CollegeFootballNews.com Freshman All-America second team recognition and 2009 Sporting News Freshman All-Big Ten honors for the 2009 Wolverines while being the second-leading tackler on the team. Granted a scholarship for his second year, he was the second-leading tackler in the Big Ten Conference and was selected as a 2010 All-Conference honorable mention.

Background

Kovacs attended Clay High School, which was the rival high school of current Wolverine teammate Kevin Koger.1 In high school, he earned varsity letters in basketball as a sophomore and junior. He also earned All-City recognition in track and field three times (first team as a senior, second team as a junior and honorable mention as a sophomore) and was a four-year letterman. He qualified for the Ohio State championships in the 4x400 meter relay. He was a three-year letterman in football, where he earned All-Ohio academic honors and All-district honors as both a wide receiver and defensive back.2

He was not recruited by any Division I schools as a high school football player, but he was recruited by some Division II schools and was considered as a preferred walk-on at the University of Toledo.3 Electing to attend Michigan even though he would not receive an athletic scholarship, he was initially waitlisted at the school and was finally admitted in June.4

College career

He did not make the 2008 Wolverines due to a knee problem, but was advised to try out again after his knee healed.5 He had surgery to repair cartilage damage, but that did not alleviate his problems.6 After discovering a torn meniscus, he had a second knee surgery, which was successful.7

In 2009, he was one of the most highly regarded walk-ons in the nation.8 His debut on September 5 against the Western Michigan was on special teams.9 The following week he registered 31 plays against Notre Dame after starting safety Michael Williams suffered leg cramps.10 He made his first start on September 26 against Indiana.11 On October 3 of that season, he had 17 tackles against Michigan State in the annual Paul Bunyan Trophy.12 He posted ten tackles and made his first career interception on November 14 against Wisconsin.13 That season he played free safety before being switched to strong safety.14 He started one game at free safety and seven at strong safety over the course of the season.15 He finished the season as the second leading tackler for the 2009 Wolverines.16 He earned 2009 CollegeFootballNews.com Freshman All-America second team recognition and 2009 Sporting News Freshman All-Big Ten honors.17 He was also a Rudy Award candidate.18

Kovacs earned a scholarship at the beginning of the 2010 NCAA Division I FBS football season.19 In the second game of the season on September 11 against Notre Dame in the Michigan <U+2013> Notre Dame football rivalry game, Kovacs recorded his first interception of the season and second of his career as part of a 10-tackle effort.2021 On October 16 he recorded 2.5 tackles for a loss against Iowa.22 After eight games in the 2010 season he ranked second in the Big Ten Conference in tackles.23 On November 18, he was nominated for the Burlsworth Trophy, which was a newly created award for the most outstanding player to have begun his career as a walk on.24 On November 20, he posted his first career quarterback sack during a season-high twelve tackle performance against Wisconsin.25 With one week remaining on the conference schedule, the performance tied Kovacs for third-overall in the Big Ten, gave Kovacs a wide lead among Big Ten underclassman (8.64 vs. 7.55 per game over the next highest performer) and extended his lead among Big Ten defensive backs (8.64 vs 8.27).26 In the final game of the regular season on November 27 against Ohio State in the annual Michigan <U+2013> Ohio State football rivalry game, Kovacs recorded his second interception of the season and third of his career as part of a 17-tackle effort.27 He collected a career-best 41 return yards following the interception.28 In the game, Kovacs tied a career-high with 17 total tackles including a new career-high 9 solo tackles.29 Kovacs ended the 2010 Big Ten Conference football season ranked second in the conference to teammate Jonas Mouton in tackles.3031 Following the Big Ten Conference season, he was selected as an honorable mention All-Conference selection by the media.323334

Personal

His father Louis, who was a walk-on for Michigan coach Bo Schembechler,35 lettered for the 1982 Michigan Wolverines football team.36 His family regularly brought him to watch Michigan home games when he was growing up. The family regularly sat in Section 27 of Michigan Stadium, on the north side of the press box. Today the family sits underneath the scoreboard, since they have a son on the team.37
