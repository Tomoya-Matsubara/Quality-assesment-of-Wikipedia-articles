Suffering from Success is the seventh studio album by DJ Khaled. The album was released on October 22, 2013, by We the Best Music Group, Cash Money Records and Republic Records. The album was supported by the hit single "No New Friends" which has peaked at number 37 on the US Billboard Hot 100. The album features guest appearances from Future, Ace Hood, Diddy, Meek Mill, Rick Ross, T.I., Swizz Beatz, 2 Chainz, French Montana, Big Sean, Timbaland, Plies, Lil Wayne, Chris Brown, Wiz Khalifa, Wale, Nicki Minaj, Drake, Mavado, J. Cole, Akon, Anthony Hamilton, Jadakiss, Bas, John Legend, Scarface, Birdman, Jeremih, Vado and Kat Dahlia.

Background

On January 17, 2013, DJ Khaled announced that he had already started working on his seventh album and that it would be titled Suffering from Success.1 In January 2013, during an interview with MTV News DJ Khaled explained where the album title came from, saying:

"We just got done turning in Ross<U+2019> album, and you know people don<U+2019>t realize, when you turn in an album, it<U+2019>s a lot of work behind the scenes; from clearing records, dealing with lawyers, sample clearances, mix and mastering, marketing plan. At that time, I<U+2019>m running around and I notice a big bald spot on my beard that kept getting bigger and bigger. I<U+2019>m acting like I don<U+2019>t want to see it, I<U+2019>m brushing it away and it kept getting it bigger and bigger. So When I finally got home off the road, I was like you know what, <U+2018>I<U+2019>m gonna go to the doctors.<U+2019> I don<U+2019>t like going to the doctors, so I go, and she<U+2019>s like it<U+2019>s a disorder that comes from your nerves and your stress, but she said <U+2018>I<U+2019>m going to give you medication, and I<U+2019>ll see you back in 4 months.<U+2019> Through that whole process while I<U+2019>m at the doctor<U+2019>s office, my phone<U+2019>s ringing, I<U+2019>m dealing with marketing people, I<U+2019>m dealing with lawyers, I<U+2019>m dealing with Ross, I<U+2019>m dealing with personal shit, a whole bunch of shit at the same time. It didn<U+2019>t stop, she<U+2019>s thinking I<U+2019>m crazy. So she gave me my medicine and all that, and she found out who I was, and after watching that 45 minute doctor<U+2019>s session, she grabbed my shoulder and she just goes, <U+2018>Son, you<U+2019>re suffering from success.<U+2019> I looked at her and was like, <U+2018>That might be the realest thing you can tell me, and it opened my mind up like, success comes with stress, and happiness. So Im driving home from the doctor<U+2019>s office and I<U+2019>m like, <U+201C>that<U+2019>s gonna be the name of my new album, Suffering From Success.<U+2019>"2

On June 10, 2013, it was announced that the album would be released on September 24, 2013.3 On July 31, 2013, the album cover was released.4 On August 26, 2013, DJ Khaled announced the album would be pushed back from September 24, 2013, until October 22, 2013.5

Guest appearances

Early reports from MTV said, the album may feature guest appearances from Lil Wayne, Rick Ross and Kanye West.6 Drake, Future and Nicki Minaj would be featured on the first two singles.7 In May 2013, Khaled also confirmed working with Big Sean on the album.8 On a August 26, 2013, vlog, it was revealed Khaled had been in the studio with Meek Mill, Vado, Birdman, Juelz Santana, Ace Hood, Mavado, and Swizz Beatz.9 On September 12, 2013, Scarface announced that Akon and himself, would be featured on the album.10 On September 27, 2013, DJ Khaled revealed that President of the United States, Barack Obama would be featured on the album in a unspecified way.11 On October 6, 2013, the track listing was released revealing guest appearances on the album from Future, Ace Hood, Diddy, Meek Mill, Rick Ross, T.I., Swizz Beatz, 2 Chainz, French Montana, Big Sean, Timbaland, Plies, Lil Wayne, Chris Brown, Wiz Khalifa, Wale, Nicki Minaj, Drake, Mavado, Bas, Jeremih, J. Cole, Akon, Anthony Hamilton, Jadakiss, John Legend, Scarface, Birdman, Vado and Kat Dahlia.12

Singles

On April 14, 2013 DJ Khaled released a promotional video for Suffering for Success and the first single "No New Friends" featuring Drake, Rick Ross and Lil Wayne was released on April 19, 2013.1314 The music video was shot on May 15 and 16, 2013 in Miami, Florida15 and featured cameos by Ace Hood and Birdman among others.16 The music video for "No New Friends" premiered on MTV Jams on June 9, 2013, and was co-directed by Colin Tilley and Drake.17 "No New Friends" has peaked at number 37 on the Billboard Hot 100.18

On July 25, 2013, Khaled publicly "proposed" to fellow Cash Money artist Nicki Minaj via MTV. He supported his offer with a 10 karat diamond ring from Rafaello & Co.,valued at about $500,000.19 Later, he reaffirmed his proposal in an interview with Power 106's DJ Felli Fel, stating: "I ain't a young boy no more. I'm on my thinking the future. I just had to be honest. I always liked her. She's my friend, of course. And I like her. It's more than a crush."20 On July 29, 2013, in an interview with Funkmaster Flex, Minaj denied Khaled's proposal, saying: "Khaled is my brother and Khaled was not serious with that damn proposal, ya'll. Please let it go. He was kidding."21 The same day, Flex premiered Khaled's new single, titled "I Wanna Be with You" which featured Minaj, as well as frequent collaborators Future and Rick Ross. This lead some to believe that his proposal was a "publicity stunt" to promote his new single.2223 Shortly after the song's release, Khaled confirmed it was all a joke and a way to introduce the record.24 The music video was shot in Miami, Florida on August 9, 2013.25 On September 3, 2013, the music video for "I Wanna Be with You" premiered on MTV Jams.26

On October 17, 2013, DJ Khaled released a making of video for the song "Never Surrender", featuring Scarface, Akon, Jadakiss, Meek Mill, Anthony Hamilton, and John Legend.27 The following day the audio to the song was premiered.28 Then on October 20, 2013, the song's music video was released. The video shows a "crew of drug dealers that gets unraveled when one of their own gets caught and snitches on the rest of them."29
