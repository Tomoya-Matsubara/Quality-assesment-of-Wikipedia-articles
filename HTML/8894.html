Ivan Hryhorovych-Barskyi or Ivan Grigorovich-Barsky () (born 1713 - died 1785 in Kiev) is a Ukrainian architect who worked in the Late Cossack Baroque style. He was a graduate of the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy, and designed many buildings and churches in Kiev and elsewhere.

Buildings designed in Kiev by him are: the church and belfry of Saint Cyril's Monastery and Church (1750<U+2013>1760), the Church of the Holy Protectress (1766), the Church of Saint Nicholas on the Bank (1772<U+2013>1775), the belfry of Saints Peter and Paul Monastery (1761<U+2013>1773), the Old Bursa of the Kyivan Mohyla Academy (1778), the Gostynyi Dvor warehouse (1760s), the Fountain of Samson (1748-1749), and the Magistrat grain warehouse (1760).

Other buildings designed by him are: the Church of the Three Saints (1761) in Lemeshi near Chernihiv, the regimental chancellery (1757), the Church of the Nativity of the Mother of God (1752<U+2013>1764, in collaboration with Andrey Kvasov) in Kozelets, the buildings of the Mezhyhirskyi Monastery in Vyshhorod.
