Charles Freeman Lee, also known as Freeman Lee, (August 13, 1927- June 15, 1997) was an American Jazz Trumpeter, Pianist, Composer, Engineer and Vocalist, recording with the Elmo Hope Quintet.

Early Life and Education

Charles Freeman was born in New York, NY, to Louella Simpson Lee and Charles Henry Lee. He studied at Wilberforce Academy (OH), and played with Wilberforce Collegians. Has a B.S. in Biology from Central State College

Early Career

1950 played piano with Snooky Young. 1951 played trumpet with Candy Johnson. In 1952 played trumpet with Sonny Stitt and Eddie "lockjaw" Vinson. In 1953 performed with Joe Holiday. In 1954 and 1955 he freelanced in New York City. 1956 played James Moody and then he visited Ohio for a year, later returning to NYC and gigging around, including Birdland, both Piano and trumpet. He also played witn Babs Gonzalez and Tom Lord.

Professional Career

He was the trumpeter for the Elmo Hope quintet, other members of the group being Elmo Hope - piano, Frank Foster - tenor sax, Percy Heath - bass and Art Blakey - drums. He played on the albums "Elmo Hope quintet, Volume 2'" 1952 and "Hope Meets Foster" 1955.

Personal Life

After his career as a jazz trumpeter, he was a Science Educator at Krueger Junior High School in Michigan City, Indiana. His sisters Mary W. White and Jane Wilfrid Ball were also educators. He never married or had children. He was buried in Xenia Ohio.
