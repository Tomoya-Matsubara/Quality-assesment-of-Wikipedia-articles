Mitchell Hanson (born February 9 1988 in Derby, England) is an English footballer, playing for Derby County in the Championship. His preferred position is at either right back or in central defence.

Career

He had extensive reserve team experience and made his debut for the reserve team whilst a schoolboy. Hanson's first taste of first team at Derby was appearing as an unused substitute in the 5-0 defeat away to Reading in April 2006.1 Hanson, who graduated through the academy, signed his first professional contract with Derby in June 2007.2 After struggling to break into Paul Jewell's first team, Hanson joined League One strugglers Port Vale on a season long loan, despite struggling with a calf injury.3 He returned to Derby in April 2008, without making an appearance for The Valiants.4 He made his first team debut for Derby as an 89th minute substitute in a 4-1 victory over Brighton & Hove Albion on 4 November 2008.5

International Career

Hanson has played for England at Under-18 level, showing his versitility by adapting to a holding midfielder role rather than his preferred position in defence.
