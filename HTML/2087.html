UiTM Malaysia Law School (UiTM Law School) is the law faculty at Universiti Teknologi MARA Malaysia. Its formal name is Faculty of Law, UiTM. UiTM Law School (or ITM Law School) is an informal name called by its graduates in the 60s, 70s, 80s and 90s. It is one of the best and oldest law school in Malaysia.

History

The Faculty of Law (formerly known as the School of Administration and Law and the Faculty of Administration and Law) was founded in 1968. It began as a centre offering British external programmes, namely the LL.B (London-External) and the Chartered Institute of Secretaries (now Institute of Chartered Secretaries and Administrators). The only internal programme offered then was the Diploma in Public Administration and Local Government1.

In 1978 the LL.B (London-External) programme was discontinued and replaced by the Diploma in Law (DIL) programme. The DIL programme is equivalent to the LL.B programme offered in British Universities. It is a three-year academic degree course based on the structure of British Universities undergraduate law programmes. Unlike most of the British programmes however, the DIL programme at the faculty is conducted on a semester system. In 1982 the Faculty introduced a one-year Advance Diploma in Law (ADIL) programme whereby graduates of the DIL programme could advance their studies. The ADIL programme is a simulatory programmes designed to provide professional training for students in preparation for their career in the legal practice as Advocates and Solicitors.

Soon after, the faculty had renamed its programmes and DIL was renamed as the LL.B while ADIL became the LL.B (Hons.). The LL.B programme, however, has been replaced with the Bachelor of Legal Studies (Hons), which was introduced in 2002. Nevertheless, LL.B (Hons.) programme was retained and it still serve as the best programme for students who wish to practice law as Advocates and Solicitors.

The delivery of the curriculum for this course adopts the method and strategy of simulated or experiential learning. The unique experience it provides to students in their legal training has led this programme to be widely recognized and accepted among the Malaysian legal fraternity. In 1995, the Faculty introduced the degree of Bachelor in Corporate Administration (Hons) (to train young and bright Bumiputras to hold office as Company Secretaries) and Bachelor of Administrative Science (Hons) in December 2002. In the pipeline are some new courses, namely, Bachelor of Law and Management (Hons) and Bachelor of Shariah and Law.

Separation of the Faculty

As of 14 January 2004, the Faculty of Administration and Law have been restructured to constitute two faculties namely the Faculty of Law and the Faculty of Administrative Science and Policy Studies.

The Faculty of Law enjoys strong affiliations with the legal fraternity, namely, the Malaysian Bar, the Attorney General's Chambers and the Judicial and Legal Service. It takes pride in continuously developing pioneer options in its degree programmes, both at the academic and professional levels. The Faculty currently comprises some 69 academic staff. It has about 1144 students reading for the Pre-Law, BLS (Hons), LL.B and LL.B (Hons) and Postgraduate programmes (Phd. and LL.M Programmes).

Location

UiTM Law Faculty is divided into two academic blocks. The main block is situated between the Business School near the Tun Abdul Razak II Library which includes the Law Library at Level Three. Another block is situated at Kolej Cempaka / Kenanga where two blocks of the residential college had been converted into the faculty's main office and lecture rooms.

Faculty's Objectives

  • To achieve an incremental percentage of graduating students with CGPA of more than 3.00 to 20% within three years.
  • To improve the percentage of academic man power with experienced Ph.D/Professional qualification of up to 30% by 2008
  • The introduction of new academic programs within 3 years and to review existing programs.
  • To ensure that 80% research/consultation projects registered with UiTM at the national and international level are implemented in accordance as scheduled.
  • To ensure the involvement of true academic staff in writing and/or academic publications are achieved at a rate of one writing and/or academic publication per academic staff every year.

Faculty's Mission and Vission

Mission

  • To run programs for the Bumiputera professional graduates who are exemplary, competitive, ethical and global through process of an effective teaching and study methods of high quality.
  • To ensure that the academic staff are qualified, experienced and high caliber.
  • The creation of multidisciplined centres of specialization and professionalism in Law.
  • The enhancement of research, writing and publication activities to enrich professionalism knowledge.
  • To provide referential services and consultation based on professional ethics and virtuos values.

Vision

  • The establishmentry a faculty that excels, exemplary and of world status in educational, training and research leadership in law.
