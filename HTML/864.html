Robin Harvey was a sportsman from St Columb Major in Cornwall,was educated at Blundell's School and formally represented his County in both Cricket (1959 - 1971) and Rugby.1

County Cricket

He was Captain of Cornwall County Cricket Club and emmigrated to South Africa in 1968. In 1969 he became the first Cornishman to be awarded the Wilfred Rhodes Trophy for having topped the batting average in the competition that season.2

County Rugby

Harvey played for Cornwall County team on eight occasions. His father Geoffrey Harvey was also an accomplished rugby player and was captain of The Hornets RFC. 3
