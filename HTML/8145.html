Velvet Revolver is an American hard rock supergroup consisting of former Guns N' Roses members Slash, Duff McKagan and Matt Sorum, alongside Dave Kushner, formerly of punk band Wasted Youth. Stone Temple Pilots vocalist Scott Weiland was Velvet Revolver's lead singer from their formation until 2008. In 2004, the band achieved commercial success with their debut album, Contraband. Despite positive reviews, some critics initially described Velvet Revolver as a mere combination of Stone Temple Pilots and Guns N' Roses, criticizing them for a "disconnection" between Scott Weiland and the rest of the band. With their single "Slither", they won the 2005 Grammy Award for Best Hard Rock Performance.

The band released Libertad in 2007, driven by the release of the single "She Builds Quick Machines", and embarked on a tour with Alice in Chains. In April 2008, Weiland left Velvet Revolver and reunited with Stone Temple Pilots. Velvet Revolver has been on indefinite hiatus since April 2008, and in November of that year, the band was released by their record label RCA Records at their request to allow them "complete freedom to go through whatever process it would take to accomplish" replacing Weiland.

The release of Slash's self-titled debut solo album and Duff McKagan's addition to the Jane's Addiction lineup seemed to put the future of the band in doubt. However, McKagan left Jane's Addiction a few months after joining. Despite being on hiatus, Velvet Revolver has written new songs and plans to audition singers and reconvene in 2011.

History

Formation (1996<U+2013>2003)

Slash, Duff McKagan, and Matt Sorum were members of hard rock band Guns N' Roses. Slash and McKagan had been members since 1985, while Sorum joined the band in 1990.1 Guns N' Roses toured and achieved international success following the release of the albums Appetite for Destruction, Use Your Illusion I, and Use Your Illusion II. However, a distancing relationship with singer Axl Rose resulted in Slash, in 1996, and McKagan, in 1997, leaving the band while Sorum was fired the same year.2 Following their departures, the trio focused on separate projects with Slash reforming Slash's Snakepit3 and McKagan reforming 10 Minute Warning,45 as well as recording his second solo album6 while Sorum rejoined The Cult.7

Despite being involved with other projects, the trio occasionally collaborated. Slash guested on McKagan's unreleased second solo album Beautiful Disease,8 while the trio recorded original music for the independent film Soundman910 in 1998. They also performed together with Lanny Cordola, Chuck Wright, and Teddy Andreadis for a one-hour plus concert at the Slamdance Film Festival in Park City, Utah to promote the film in 1999.11 Rumors quickly arose of a Guns N' Roses reunion, however, McKagan stated that they "[were] just [t]here to play. It's not that big of a deal, but [they] play good together."12

By 2001, Slash's Snakepit had disbanded for the second time.13 Slash began working with The Black Crowes drummer Steve Gorman and an unnamed bassist on a new project:14 writing the music for what would become "Fall to Pieces".15 McKagan reformed Loaded,16 previously his band for the tour in support of Beautiful Disease,17 with Geoff Reading. McKagan also added both Mike Squires and Jeff Rouse to the lineup.18 Following a tour of Japan in 2002,19 former Zilch, Wasted Youth, Electric Love Hogs, and Dave Navarro guitarist Dave Kushner joined Loaded in place of Mike Squires.20

When musician Randy Castillo died from cancer in 2002, Slash, McKagan, and Sorum performed at a benefit concert to raise money and commemorate Castillo21 with Josh Todd and Keith Nelson of Buckcherry as well as B-Real and Sen Dog of Cypress Hill.22 Recognizing that their musical relationship was still intact,23 the trio began rehearsing with Todd24 and Nelson, working on material that would become "Dirty Little Thing",25 but eventually decided against forming a group with them.2627 During a Loaded show at Hollywood's Viper Room,28 McKagan introduced Kushner to Slash, who were previously friends in junior high and high school.2930 Kushner was invited to jam with the group and was soon invited to join with Slash, stating that "Dave brought a cool vibe to what [they] were doing. There was no deliberation; that was it, it was a perfect fit."31 Their former Guns N' Roses band mate Izzy Stradlin also joined them for two weeks,32 eventually suggesting that "Duff and [Stradlin] will sing and [they] will just do a club tour in a van." Slash states in his autobiography that it was hard to tell if Stradlin was serious or kidding.33 After auditioning Kelly Shaefer of Atheist and Neurotica,34 Stradlin left the group.35

While Shaefer's audition was unsuccessful, the quartet continued auditioning for a lead singer, with VH1 filming the recruitment process36 while being referred to as the temporary name "The Project".37 The resulting documentary was aired as VH1 Inside Out: The Rise of Velvet Revolver. A number of lead singers auditioned for the band, including Todd Kerns, formerly of Age of Electric,383940 Sebastian Bach, formerly of Skid Row,41 Shawn Albro of U.P.O.,42 and Travis Meeks of Days of the New.43 Myles Kennedy, formerly of The Mayfield Four, declined an invitation from Sorum to audition.44 Ian Astbury of The Cult and Mike Patton of Faith No More45 also declined audition offers.46 The band were also interested in auditioning Stone Temple Pilots singer Scott Weiland, who had become friends with McKagan after attending the same gym.4748 Weiland once played on the same bill as Kushner,49 and was in rehab at the same time as Sorum.5051 Weiland was sent two discs of material,52 and felt that the first disc "sounded like Bad Company gone wrong."53 When he was sent the second disc, Weiland was more positive, comparing it to Core-era Stone Temple Pilots,54 though he turned them down because Stone Temple Pilots were still together.55

When Stone Temple Pilots disbanded in 2003,56 the band sent Weiland new music, which he took into his studio and added vocals. This music eventually becoming the song "Set Me Free".5758 Despite delivering the music to the band himself, Weiland was still unsure whether or not he wanted to join them,59 despite performing at an industry showcase at Mates.60 They recorded two songs with producer Nick Raskulinecz,61 a recorded version of "Set Me Free" and a cover of Pink Floyd's "Money", for the soundtracks to the movies The Hulk and The Italian Job, respectively.62 Weiland joined the band soon after.63 "Set Me Free" managed to peak at number 17 on the Mainstream Rock Chart64 without any radio promotion or a record label.65 It was prior to a screening of The Hulk at Universal Studios that the band chose a name.66 After seeing a movie by Revolution Studios,67 Slash liked the beginning of the word, eventually thinking of Revolver because of its multiple meanings; the name of a gun, subtext of a revolving door which suited the band as well as the name of a Beatles album.68 When he suggested Revolver to the band, Weiland suggested back Black Velvet Revolver, liking the idea of "something intimate like velvet juxtaposed with something deadly like a gun."69 They eventually arrived at Velvet Revolver,70 announcing it at a press conference and performance showcase at the El Rey Theatre71 while also performing the songs "Set Me Free" and "Slither" as well as covers of Nirvana's "Negative Creep", Sex Pistols' "Pretty Vacant", and Guns N' Roses' "It's So Easy".72

Contraband and mainstream success (2003<U+2013>2005)

Prior to the recording of their debut album, Weiland took material that the band had previously written to his studio, Lavish, in Toluca Lake.73 With engineer Doug Grean, Weiland rearranged the music to fit his vocals, eventually coming out with the songs "Big Machine" and "Dirty Little Thing".74 The band worked on new material for songs such as "You Got No Right", "Slither", "Sucker Train Blues", and "Do It for the Kids", among others.75 It was during this time that Weiland was arrested at the parking lot of his studio for drug possession.76 Upon release from jail, he wrote lyrics to material he was given previously, writing the song "Fall to Pieces".77 Velvet Revolver soon began recording their debut album. Initially, they recorded "Slither" with producer Bob Ezrin at Henson Studios, but were dissatisfied with the result.78 After recording "Headspace" with Josh Abraham, the band liked the track enough to do the rest of the album with him.7980

Velvet Revolver soon gained major label attention with Warner Bros. and Chrysalis. RCA and Elektra were also interested in signing the band.81 They eventually signed with RCA Records.8283 They recorded their album at NRG Recording Studios, while Slash recorded his guitar parts at a smaller studio on the southern corner between Highland Avenue and Sunset Boulevard.84 During recording, Weiland could only work for three hours a day due to a court order mentioning that he was to stay in a halfway house.85 The marketing campaign for Velvet Revolver in the run-up to the release of the first album was profiled as part of the Frontline program The Way the Music Died,86 which included interviews with the band members and producers.

The resulting album, titled Contraband, was released on June 8, 2004,87 and, helped by the success of the single "Slither",88 debuted at number one on the Billboard 200,89 selling over 250,000 copies in the first week.90 Contraband went on to sell four million copies worldwide, 2.9 million of which were sold in the United States, and was certified 2x platinum by the RIAA.9192 Both "Slither"93 and "Fall to Pieces"94 managed to peak at number one on the Mainstream Rock Chart as well as number 56 and 67 on the Billboard Hot 100, respectively.95 "Slither" also peaked at number one on the Modern Rock Chart and number 35 on the UK Singles Chart.96 The album's third single, "Dirty Little Thing", peaked at number eight on the Mainstream Rock chart.97

Critically, the album was generally well-received.98 Despite being praised for its hedonism and maturity,99 critics noted a disconnection between "singer and band".100101 Velvet Revolver won the Kerrang! Award for Best International Newcomer in 2004, and the following year they won the Best Hard Rock Performance Grammy Award for "Slither". They also received a nomination for Rock Artist of the Year at the Billboard Music Awards while "Fall to Pieces" was nominated for a Song of the Year/Rock Radio Radio Music Award. They recorded a new song entitled "Come On, Come In" for the movie Fantastic Four in 2005, which peaked at number 14 on the Mainstream Rock Chart.102 "Fall to Pieces" then re-entered the charts, peaking at number twenty-five on the Adult Top 40 the same year.103

Velvet Revolver toured extensively for nineteen months104 in support of Contraband. They toured both the United States and Europe twice, while also performing in Australia, New Zealand, and Japan. They performed at Live 8105 and various festivals including Download Festival106 and Ozzfest. It was during the tour that the band members, with the exception of Kushner,107 began to relapse on alcohol and drugs.108109 Though they managed to get clean in time for the recording of their new album,110 Slash felt that "[the band] lost [Weiland]" and "thought the overall spirit of everything was declining at that point."111

Libertad and hiatus (2005<U+2013>2008)

Weiland announced in 2005 that Velvet Revolver's next album would be titled Libertad and would be a concept album.112 When they started writing material, they decided against the concept idea.113 Initially, the band started working with producer Rick Rubin on the album. However, due to his methods, such as having a crew to do the work and engineering while only popping in occasionally,114 and due to the fact that he was also working with other bands at the same time,115 they decided against continuing with Rubin.116 At the suggestion of Weiland,117 Velvet Revolver began working with Brendan O'Brien.118 Slash stated that O' Brien "brought more than just discipline to the equation, he brought a musicality that stems from the fact that he plays guitar. bass and drums. At any given moment he could play along [the band] and it really helped the process."119 He also said that the "sessions were consistent, everyone was there, everyone contributed, and everyone appreciated what each player was doing" and that the "mutual participation surpassed the first Guns sessions."120 While writing for the album, Weiland believed that his band mates were going to reunite with Guns N' Roses121 when the band's manager was talking to Axl Rose about switching management companies,122 and were not going to record their second album.123 He was later convinced by the band that this was not the case.124

Following the completion of the album, Velvet Revolver performed for and inducted Van Halen into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, with Weiland and Slash speaking on the band's behalf, on March 12, 2007.125126 The band played a medley of "Ain't Talkin' 'bout Love" and "Runaround".127 Shows in South American with Aerosmith followed in April.128 They released the EP Melody and the Tyranny on June 1129 to serve as a precursor to the release of their new album, which featured two songs from Libertad, a cover of Talking Heads song "Psycho Killer" and a video documentary about the making of Libertad as well as a live video of the band performing "Do It for the Kids".130

Libertad was released on July 3, 2007,131132 peaking at number five on the Billboard 200.133 The album's first single "She Builds Quick Machines" peaked at 74 on the Hot Canadian Digital Singles.134 The second and third singles, "The Last Fight" and "Get Out the Door", both peaked at number 16 and 34 on the Mainstream Rock Chart, respectively.135 Critical reception to the album was mixed. Though some critics praised the album136137 and felt that Libertad gave the band an identity of their own,138 outside of the Guns N' Roses and Stone Temple Pilots comparisons, others described the album as "bland"139140 and noted that the band have still to gel with them "play[ing] to their strengths instead of finding a collective sound."141

In support of Libertad, Velvet Revolver toured North America and Canada with Alice in Chains from August 2007 to October.142 They also performed at the Virgin Festival,143 Gods of Metal, and Download in 2007.144 A November tour of Japan was canceled after they were denied visas,145 and in 2008, a tour of Australia was postponed, due to health issues,146 and later canceled147 following Weiland's decision to voluntarily enter a rehab facility.148 On November 21, 2007, Weiland was arrested after crashing his car while driving on an L.A. highway. He was charged with driving under the influence of drugs with a prior conviction and later released on $40,000 bail.149 Velvet Revolver then toured both the US and the UK, as well as some European shows, on the Rock n' Roll as It Should Be tour from January 24 to April 1, 2008. They also played at the Dubai Desert Rock Festival on March 8 the same year.150 It was during the tour that Weiland "got back into his old ways",151 which started to take their toll on the rest of the band152 with the cancellation of the Australian tour seen as the "final blow".153154

On the UK tour, the band members never spoke with Weiland, with the exception of a few arguments around the stage.155 Tensions came to a head during Velvet Revolver's Glasgow show on March 20, 2008, where Weiland announced to the crowd that it was the band's last tour,156 unaware that the band were already planning on firing him.157158 After Sorum posted a message about the show on his website,159 Weiland issued a statement through Blabbermouth.net in response, saying he "made many attempts to remain cordial with the members of [Velvet Revolver], but mainly, the likes of [Sorum]" and that "[the band] were a gang. But ego and jealousy can get the better of anyone."160 Slash later stated that it would not be Velvet Revolver's last tour.161 Weiland's departure was announced on April 1.162 Weiland also departed the cover band Camp Freddy,163 which also featured Sorum, and has since reunited with Stone Temple Pilots.164165

Velvet Revolver soon began recording new material166 before going on hiatus, while a number of singers were rumoured to be working with the band. Amongst them were Lenny Kravitz,167168 Chester Bennington of Linkin Park,169 Steve Isaacs formerly of Skycycle and The Panic Channel,170 Royston Langdon of Spacehog,171 Donovan Leitch of Camp Freddy,172 and Scars on Broadway guitarist Franky Perez,173 as well as previous auditonee Sebastion Bach.174 Slipknot and Stone Sour singer Corey Taylor unsuccessfully auditioned for the band,175 while Velvet Revolver invited both Myles Kennedy176 and Shooter Jennings177 to join. However, with Kennedy still in Alter Bridge178 and Jennings recording a new album,179 both turned the offer down. The band also approached their record label in June 2008, asking to be released from their recording contract180 to allow them "complete freedom to go through whatever process it would take to accomplish" replacing Weiland.181 Sony BMG/RCA dropped the band from the label in November of the same year.182

Hiatus activities (2008<U+2013>2010)

McKagan reunited with previous band Loaded in 2008,183 releasing an EP entitled Wasted Heart the same year McKagan also released the band's second album, entitled Sick, in 2009.184185 They completed the recording of their third album with producer Terry Date in 2010,186 and began collaborating with filmmaker and documentarian Jamie Burton Chamberlin on a film based on the album the same year.187 McKagan joined Jane's Addiction in March 2010, replacing founding bassist Eric Avery.188 However, his tenure with the band lasted only five months, with his departure from the band announced on September 6 of the same year.189

Kushner began composing music for film and television. He co-wrote the theme song entitled "This Life" for FX series Sons of Anarchy,190 receiving an Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Main Title Theme Music in 2009191 and an ASCAP Award in 2010.192 In 2010, he and John O'Brien composed the music for the ABC drama series Detroit 1-8-7.193194 He also contributed music to the films Four Christmases and Couples Retreat.195 Kushner also collaborated with Scars on Broadway guitarist Franky Perez,196 who had previously worked with Velvet Revolver,197 releasing songs under the pseudonym of DKFXP. One of the songs, "Beyond the Wire", was later used in the film Hisss, with the new title of "Beyond the Snake". The song featured vocals and lyrics by Shruti Haasan.198 In 2010, Kushner and Dave Navarro collaborated on a cover of The Kinks' "You Really Got Me" to be used in a Hyundai commercial.199

Sorum continued work on cover band Camp Freddy's debut album200 before guesting on Sammy Hagar's solo album Cosmic Universal Fashion.201 He also produced two songs, entitled "Are You Blind" and "Coming For You", for Los Angeles-based band, Drive A, in 2008.202 In 2010, Sorum formed the cover band Carnival of Dogs with Franky Perez, L.A. Guns guitarist Tracii Guns, and former Ozzy Osbourne and Billy Idol bassist Phil Soussan203 before going on to tour with Mot<U+00F6>rhead, temporarily filling in for drummer Mikkey Dee.204 He also began working with Cherie Currie, formerly of The Runaways, on her new solo album.205

Slash worked on his solo album, writing and demoing songs before sending them to numerous singers and musicians.206 Recorded with producer Eric Valentine,207 Slash, released on March 31, 2010, featured a number of guests such as Andrew Stockdale of Wolfmother, M. Shadows of Avenged Sevenfold, Kid Rock, Ozzy Osbourne, Myles Kennedy, and Fergie, among others.208 His band for the tour in support of the album consisted of Myles Kennedy, guitarist Bobby Schneck, and drummer Brent Fitz. It also included early Velvet Revolver audtionee Todd Kerns on bass.209 He also guested on Rihanna's album Rated R on the song "Rockstar 101",210 as well as Kennedy's debut solo album while also confirming that his next solo album would feature Kennedy as the sole vocalist.211212

Possible return (2010<U+2013>present)

After running into one another on a flight to Las Vegas in February 2010, Slash stated he had "no issues" with Weiland and that he was excited to hear the new Stone Temple Pilots album,213 and also stated that, in May, unlike the "Guns N' Roses situation", he and Weiland are still friends.214 Weiland also later stated that his time with the band was a "great experience until near the end" and that he "[does not] hold any resentment towards any of the guys in the band."215 Velvet Revolver soon began writing new songs and auditioning new singers,216217218 with Slash stating that "the new material is a lot heavier than what Velvet Revolver has put out, so [he is] dying to put out the quintessential Velvet Revolver record."219 Rumours quickly arose that Myles Kennedy was to be the new Velvet Revolver vocalist220 due to his collaborations with Slash. However, Slash confirmed that no effort was made to have Kennedy join the band.221 Kennedy also stated that while joining "Velvet Revolver [would] be enticing, [he made plans] and [his] plate is pretty full."222

Slash, McKagan, and Sorum all contributed to the song "Kissed It" for the Macy Gray album The Sellout, which was released on June 22.223 Despite not featuring Kushner, the trio were credited as Velvet Revolver on the album.224 The band released their first concert DVD on November 16, entitled Live In Houston, which was filmed in 2005 while the band was touring in support of Contraband.225 The same month, Slash revealed that a "big name" singer is due to audition soon for Velvet Revolver,226 with more auditions also being held in December.227 Sorum revealed in December that the band were close to finding a new singer. They agreed on one person, describing him as "younger and stronger." Despite being positive, they plan to continue rehearsing with the singer in January before making a decision.228229 In January 2011, it was revealed that the band had nine demos recorded, and was due to make a decision on their singer;230 Matt Sorum also revealed that the band was likely to release its next album before the end of 2011, so long as they find time to record together.231 In a video interview with Vintage Rock at NAMM 2011, Sorum stated that the rest of the band do not necessarily share his opinion of the "mystery singer."232 Previous auditionee Corey Taylor has been rumored to be the singer Velvet Revolver were working with.233 In an interview with Billboard, Taylor neither confirmed nor denied the rumor,234 while McKagan, in an online chat for ESPN, also stated that he "can neither confirm nor deny" that Taylor was the new singer.235236 Slash later confirmed that Velvet Revolver were looking at Taylor as a possible replacement for Weiland. However, due to his solo tour, no decision was made.237 It has been confirmed that Corey Taylor, vocalist to Slipknot and Stonesour, is now the new vocalist for Velvet Revolver.

Musical style

Velvet Revolver's first album Contraband was described by Johnny Loftus of Allmusic as an "updated version of Guns N' Roses swagger behind Scott Weiland's glammy, elastic vocals."238 David Browne of Entertainment Weekly stated that "[a]nyone expecting Use Your Illusion III, though, will be in for a slight buzzkill" and that "[t]he songs suggest the pop grunge of Weiland's old band more than the careening overdrive of GN'R."239 A number of reviewers made some comparisons to the members previous bands240241 with PopMatters reviewer David Powell stating that "Contraband is a pretty good record of unpretentious rock and roll that suffers from inevitable comparison with the best efforts of its parent bands." He went on to state that while Velvet Revolver's "heritage is evident on most of the songs", Contraband "improves with repeat listening, which is encouraging."242 With Velvet Revolver's second album Libertad seen the band's style change with the presence of producer Brendan O'Brien, noted by Allmusic reviewer Stephen Thomas Erlewine.243 Erlewine also stated that "too often, there are concessions between Weiland and the others during the course of a song."244 Tom Sinclair of Entertainment Weekly stated that Libertad is "feels both comfortingly familiar and vaguely exotic."245 Songs such as "Let it Roll" and "She Mine" seen some comparisons to The Doors, The Rolling Stones and The Stooges, noted by San Francisco Chronicle reviewer Jaan Uhelszki.246 The New York Post commented that "Slash's guitar riffs throughout this new record are as aggressive as a caged cat" and " singer Scott Weiland's vocals are crisp and controlled yet passionate."247

Awards and nominations

Velvet Revolver have received one Grammy Award. The band won the Grammy when "Slither" was nominated for Best Hard Rock Performance in 2005. The song "Fall to Pieces" received a nomination for Song of the Year/Rock Radio Radio Music Award in 2005. The band won the Best International Newcomer Kerrang! Award in 2004 while they were nominated for a Rock Artist of the Year Billboard Music Award in 2005.
