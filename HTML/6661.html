The following is a timeline of the Presidency of Barack Obama, from January 1, 2010 to December 31, 2010, his second calendar year as president of the United States. For his time as President-elect, see the Presidential transition of Barack Obama; for a detailed account of his first months in office, see First 100 days of Barack Obama's presidency; for a complete itinerary of his travels, see List of presidential trips made by Barack Obama.

January

  • January 5 - Obama holds a major security meeting in the wake of the attempted attack on Northwest Airlines Flight 253.1
  • January 7 - President Obama meets with former President Bill Clinton at the White House.2
  • January 13 - President Obama holds a press conference discussing the operations of US government organizations such as USAID and the Department of Defense in response to the 2010 Haiti earthquake.34
  • January 14 - President Obama commits $100,000,000 to help Haiti recover from the 2010 earthquake, while calling on former American Presidents George W. Bush and Bill Clinton to assist.56
  • January 16 - Secretary of State Hillary Clinton and USAID Director Rajiv Shah travel to Haiti.7
  • January 17 - Obama travels to Boston, Massachusetts to campaign for Martha Coakley in the Senate special election.8
  • January 18 - Obama engaged in community service to honor slain civil rights hero Martin Luther King Jr., while also visiting the American Red Cross headquarters and personally submitting his first Twitter update.910
  • January 19 - Republican Scott Brown wins Massachusetts Senate special election in spite of Obama's personal efforts in support of Martha Coakley.
  • January 21 - Obama announces the "Volcker rule" and a host of other proposals for new banking regulation.1112
  • January 22 - President Obama travels to Elyria, Ohio, visiting the Lorain County Community College and emphasizing his political agenda.13
  • January 25 - President Obama hosts the Los Angeles Lakers at the White House for winning the 2009 NBA Finals.14
  • January 27 - President Barack Obama delivers his first State of the Union address to a joint session of Congress.15
  • January 28 - President Obama travels to MacDill Air Force Base in Tampa, Florida, where he met with crew helping with the humanitarian response to the 2010 Haiti earthquake, before holding a town hall meeting at the University of Tampa.16

February

  • February 1 - Obama announces his proposal for the fiscal year 2011 federal budget.1718
  • February 2 - President Obama travels to Nashua, New Hampshire, touring small businesses before giving a speech at Nashau North High School.19
  • February 3 - President Obama hosts a meeting of Governors in the State Dining Room. Afterwards, he holds a Cabinet-level exercise in crisis-management in preparation for the 2010 Vancouver Olympics.20
  • February 6 - President Obama speaks at a gathering of the Democratic National Committee's Winter Meeting at the Capitol Hilton in Washington, D.C. The president's motorcade encountered trouble due to the blizzard that affected the region when a snow-laden tree branch fell on a vehicle carrying journalists as it returned to the White House; no one was injured.21
