Demetri McCamey, Jr. (born February 21, 1989 in ) is a member of the University of Illinois basketball team who has completed his junior season for the 2009<U+2013>10 team. McCamey, whose nicknames are DMac and Meechi,1 plays the point guard position. He is an All-Big Ten performer. He is the 2009<U+2013>10 Big Ten assists champion (7.06) and the 2009<U+2013>10 NCAA runner-up.

He played high school basketball with Big Ten Conference foe Evan Turner on the St. Joseph High School (in Westchester) basketball team. He was a first-team All-state selections according to numerous publications.

As as true sophomore, he led the 2008<U+2013>09 team in scoring average, and he was chosen as a third team 2008<U+2013>09 All-Big Ten Conference player by both the coaches and the media. During his junior season, he led the big ten in assists per game.2 He holds the Illinois single-season assist/game (7.06) and single-game assists (16) records. Following the 2009<U+2013>10 All-Big Ten Conference regular season, he was named a first-team All-conference selection by both the coaches and the media. He became the first Fighting Illini to average over seven assists per game over the course of a season during the 2009<U+2013>10 NCAA Division I men's basketball season.

Basketball career

High school

He was part of a 2005 Chicago area sophomore class that was considered to be the best in the history of the state of Illinois.3 It included Derrick Rose and his St. Joseph's teammate Turner. Isiah Thomas is St. Joseph's most famous basketball alum, but McCamey was at the time mentioned as the potential next great St. Joseph's product. The class of 2007 was compared to the Chicago area class of 1979 that included Thomas, Terry Cummings, and Darrell Walker as well as the class of 1998 that included Quentin Richardson, Corey Maggette, Frank Williams, Bobby Simmons, Michael Wright.4 McCamey was considered the second best area prospect after Rose.56

As a junior, he and Turner attended 2005 Midnight Madness with the Wisconsin Badgers men's basketball team.7 At the beginning of his junior season, Chicago Tribune named him to its annual top Chicago metropolitan area basketball players list,8 and it ranked St. Joseph number three in the area.9 The team went to the state sectional final before its season ended with a 75<U+2013>72 sectional final loss to Proviso East High School.10 After his junior season, he was considered one of the top 25 prospects in the country in his class according to one scout,11 and he was given special mention by the Chicago Tribune and honorable mention by the Associated Press for all-state honors.12

During his senior season, his team was listed second to Rose's Simeon Career Academy in the preseason Tribune Chicago area high school basketball team rankings.13 That season, he was named to the second-team Associated Press 2006<U+2013>07 Class AA all-state team, a day before Rose's Simeon eliminated Turner's St. Joseph in the Illinois Class AA supersectional.1415 McCamey and Turner finished sixth and third to Rose in the Illinois Mr. Basketball voting,16 and the Chicago Tribune chose both of them as first team All-state selections along with Rose.17 He was also a first team selection by Chicago Sun-Times, Champaign-Urbana News-Gazette and Illinois Basketball Coaches Association.18 McCamey was ranked as the #10, #11 and #10 point guard in the nation as a high school senior by ESPN, rivals.com, and scout.com respectively.192021

College

Freshman season

When McCamey posted 31 points on February 7, 2008 against Indiana,22 it was the season high for the 2007-08 Illinois Fighting Illini men's basketball team and the most by a Illinois Fighting Illini men's basketball freshman in fourteen years.23 During the 2008 Big Ten Conference Men's Basketball Tournament he made six three point shots in six attempts against ,24 which set a Big Ten Conference Men's Basketball Tournament single-game record and tied the Illinois school single-game record for three point field goal percentage (most made with no misses).25

Sophomore season

He was chosen as a third team 2008<U+2013>09 All-Big Ten Conference player by both the Big Ten coaches and the Big Ten media.26 McCamey was also a National Association of Basketball Coaches (NABC) Division I District 7 All<U+2010>District second team choice.2728 Since the Big Ten Conference was its own district, this is equivalent to being named second team All-Big Ten by the NABC.29 He led the 2008<U+2013>09 Illinois Fighting Illini team in scoring average and was second in assists.30 On February 12, 2009, he scored the game-winning bank shot with 2.9 seconds remaining against .31 Although he only ranked fourth in the conference in assists,32 he had the highest assist average in conference games.33

Junior season

McCamey was named co-Big Ten Conference Player of the Week for February 8, 2010.34 On February 20, he recorded 16 assists, which is the fourth highest single-game total in Big Ten history.35 This total set an Illinois Fighting Illini men's basketball single-game record.36

McCamey led the Big Ten in assists (7.06). He was also tenth in scoring and eighth in steals.37 His 254 assists in 36 games resulting in a 7.06 assist per game average eclipsed Deron Williams' 264 in 39 games (6.77 average) posted during the 2004<U+2013>05 NCAA Division I men's basketball season. Thus, McCamey became took over the Fighting Illini single-season average record.38 His total of 254 was the fourth highest in Big Ten history and only 20 shy of Mateen Cleaves' Big Ten single-season record of 274. Williams' 264 is the Fighting Illini record. McCamey's total of 525 after his junior season left him 291 short of Cleaves' career record of 816. Bruce Douglas holds the Fighting Illini record with a total of 765.39

At the conclusion of the regular season, he was a first-team All-Big Ten selection by both the coaches and the media.4041 McCamey was selected by the U.S. Basketball Writers Association to the ten-man All-District V team covering college basketball players in the states of Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Michigan, Minnesota, and Wisconsin.42 McCamey was selected to the 2010 Big Ten Conference Men's Basketball Tournament team.43 He was recognized as an All-District first-team selection by the National Association of Basketball Coaches, making him eligible for the State Farm Division I All<U+2010>America teams.44

Personal

His parents are Sabrina Watson and Demetri McCamey, Sr. He has three brothers and one sister. He was a member of the Jessie White Tumbling Team as a youngster.45
