Karen Farrell is a wheelchair basketball player from Australia. She won two silver medals at the Paralympic Games.

Personal

Farrell is from Sydney, New South Wales.1 She became a paraplegic at the age of fifteen, when a car she was a passenger in slid off the road after trying to pass a truck.2 At the time of the accident, she was not belted into her seat and sustained back, head and neck injuries as a result:3 Other passengers in the car who were belted in had much less severe injuries.4 When she is not competing, she works as a Information Technology Consultant. 5

Basketball

Farrell is a New South Wales Institute of Sport athlete.6 She has been a member of the New South Wales women's state basketball team and competed at the national championships.7 In 2001, she was a scholarship holder at the Australian Institute of Sport.8 In 2000, she was also sponsored by the Motor Accidents Authority in New South Wales.9

National team

Farrell was a member of Australia's national team by 1994. That year and in 1998, she was part of the Australian team that finished third at the Gold Cup tournament.10 She was part of the silver medal winning Australia women's national wheelchair basketball team 11 at the 2004 Summer Paralympics.12 She was part of the silver medal winning Australia women's national wheelchair basketball team 13 at the 2000 Summer Paralympics.14

Club basketball

In 2008, Farrell played her club basketball of the Women's National Wheelchair Basketball League (WNWBL)'s Hills Hornets.15 Her team mates included Liesl Tesch,16 who was on the same team as Farrell when they earned their 2000 and 2004 Paralympic medals.17

Recognition

In 2008, Farrell was named a Basketball Australia Merit Award winner.18
