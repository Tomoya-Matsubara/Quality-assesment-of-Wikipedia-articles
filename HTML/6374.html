The Protestant church of Augustinusga or Saint Augustine<U+2019>s church1 is a religious building in Augustinusga, Netherlands, one of the medieval churches in Friesland.

The church was built in the 15th century and has a tower that dates from the 13th century, built out of brick.2 The building was once a Roman Catholic church dedicated to Saint Augustine, becoming a Protestant church after the Protestant Reformation. It is listed as a Rijksmonument, number 7032.3 The building is located on the Geawei 15.4
