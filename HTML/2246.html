Mi Tierra () is the third studio album by Cuban-American recording artist Gloria Estefan, released on June 14, 1993 by Epic Records. Produced by husband Emilio Estefan, it is her first Spanish-language album and pays homage to her Cuban roots. The album features Cuban musical genres, including boleros, danzan and son music. Recorded at Crescent Moon Studios in Miami, Florida, Mi Tierra features notable Latin musicians such as Arturo Sandoval, Cachao L<U+00F3>pez and Paquito D'Rivera.

The album was an international success, selling eight million copies worldwide as of 2006. In the United States it was the first record to reach number one on the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart, and peaked at number twenty-seven on the Billboard 200 chart. Mi Tierra has sold over one million copies in the US and Spain. The album received favorable reviews from critics, who praised the album's production, songs and Estefan's vocals. Its success won the singer a Grammy Award for Best Tropical Latin Album. Mi Tierra spawned seven singles: "Mi Tierra", "Con Los A<U+00F1>os Que Me Quedan", "Tradici<U+00F3>n", "Montuno", "<U+00A1>S<U+00ED> Se<U+00F1>or!...", "Mi Buen Amor" and "Ayer".

Background

Gloria Estefan had wanted to record a Spanish-language album reflecting her Cuban heritage since the beginning of her musical career.1 Before recording in English, Estefan and her band performed at Latino nightclubs;2 she also remembered her grandmother teaching her old Cuban songs. Music had an important role in Estefan's family; her paternal grandmother was a poet, and an uncle played the flute in a salsa band.3 The singer's desire to record an album in Spanish was also influenced by her son, Nayib; she wanted him to recognize his Cuban heritage.4

Recording and production

Mi Tierra was produced by Estefan's husband, Emilio Estefan, and fellow Miami Sound Machine members Clay Ostwald and Jorge Casas.5 It features notable Latin musicians, including Nestor Torres, Cachao L<U+00F3>pez, Paquito Hechavarr<U+00ED>a, Paquito D'Rivera, Arturo Sandoval, Luis Enrique and Tito Puente.6 Additional performers include Sheila E. and the London Symphony Orchestra.78 The album was recorded at Crescent Moon Studios in Miami, Floria.9 Celia Cruz was invited to perform, but was unable to do so because of her touring schedule.10 The album's cover features Estefan in a black-and-white photo at a Havana nightclub before the Cuban Revolution.11

Musical style and songs

The opening track on the album, "Con los A<U+00F1>os Que Me Quedan" ("With the Years I Have Left"), is a Cuban bolero song. Three other bolero tracks on Mi Tierra are "Mi Buen Amor" ("My True Love"), "Volver<U+00E1>s" ("You'll Be Back"), and "Hablas de M<U+00ED>" ("You're Talking About Me").1213 The title track details the passion of Estefan's homeland in a salsa arrangement.14 In "Ayer" ("Yesterday") the singer finds a flower given to her by a lover and yearns for him to return, since life is short. Its music combines bolero and son music.15 "No Hay Mal Que Por Bien No Bien" ("Out of All Bad, Some Good Things Come") is a danz<U+00F3>n recalling a brief love affair.16

"<U+00A1>S<U+00ED> Se<U+00F1>or" ("Yes Sir!") is another son track featured on the album.17 The bolero "Volver<U+00E1>s" was later covered by Mexican recording artist Alejandro Fern<U+00E1>ndez on his album Me Estoy Enamorando (1997), also produced by Emilio Estefan.18 "Montuno" takes its name from the musical genre of the same name.19 "Hablemos El Mismo Idioma" ("Let's Speak The Same Language") is an anthem, reaching out to other Latino groups, telling them that since they speak the same language they should leave their differences behind.20 The album closes with "Tradici<U+00F3>n" ("Traditional"), which is performed as a guaguanc<U+00F3>.21

Commercial reception

Album

In the United States, Mi Tierra peaked at number twenty-seven on the Billboard 200 chart.22 It was the first number-one album on the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart, established when it was released.23 The album spent a total of fifty-eight weeks atop the chart, until it was displaced by Selena's album Amor Prohibido the week of June 11, 1994.24 It was more successful on the Billboard Tropical Albums chart, where it spent ninety-one weeks on top.25 Mi Tierra debuted at number one on the Billboard Latin Pop Albums chart the week of June 11, 1993, but it was removed from the chart following week since it did not meet its criteria.26

Mi Tierra ended 1994 as the best-selling Latin album of the year in the United States.27 The album was certified 16Ã— platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America by Latin standards, for shipments of 1.6 million copies in the U.S. According to Nielsen SoundScan, it sold 1,198,000 copies in the U.S. by 2007; according to Billboard, most purchasers did not speak Spanish.28 In Latin America, the album was certified double platinum in Argentina and triple platinum in Mexico.29 It sold over 30,000 copies in Chile.30

In Europe, Mi Tierra peaked at number fifty-nine in Germany,31 number nine in the Netherlands,32 number one in Spain,33 number twenty-five in Switzerland and number eleven in the United Kingdom.34 The album was certified 10Ã— platinum in Spain (for shipping one million copies) and certified gold in Switzerland. Mi Tierra was the 60th-best-selling album of the 1990s,35 and sold over eight million copies worldwide by 2006.36

Singles

"Mi Tierra" was the first single released from the album. In the United States, it reached number one on the Billboard Hot Latin Songs chart and number five on the Billboard Hot Dance Club Songs chart.37 In Europe, the song peaked at number seventy-seven on the Media Control charts in Germany, twenty-seventh on the Mega Single Top 100 chart in Netherlands and thirty-sixth on the UK Singles Chart.383940 The second single, "Con Los A<U+00F1>os Que Me Quedan", also reached number one on the Hot Latin Songs chart in the United States.41 An English version, "If We Were Lovers", was released as a single and peaked at number forty on the UK Singles Chart.42 The third single, "Tradici<U+00F3>n", reached number one the Hot Dance Clubs chart in the United States.43

"Montuno" was the fourth single released from the album, peaking at number fifty-five on the UK Singles chart.44 The fifth single, "<U+00A1>S<U+00ED> Se<U+00F1>or!..." peaked at number forty-four on the Mega Single Top 100 chart in the Netherlands.45 The sixth, "Mi Buen Amor", reached number one on the Hot Latin Songs chart in the United States.46 The last single released from the album was "Ayer", which peaked at number five on the Hot Latin Songs chart.47

Critical reception and accolades

Jose F. Promis of Allmusic awarded the album four stars out of five, calling it "one of [Estefan's] most satisfying."48 Mi Tierra was "a breezy, sunny album with moments of melancholy," and "one of her most consistent albums to date."49 Achy Obejas of the Chicago Tribune gave the album four out of four stars, praised it as "dispensing with the brash sound of more contemporary salsa" and lauded Emilio Estefan for the album's production.50 Anne Hurley of Entertainment Weekly said that Mi Tierra "will whirl you through an intoxicating landscape of traditional Cuban rhythms and aromatic flavors," and applauded the guest musicians on the album.51

Parry Gettelman of the Orlando Sentinel gave Mi Tierra four out of five stars, commending the album as "uncompromising, offering up songs and arrangements firmly rooted in Cuban traditions."52 He compared it to Estefan's earlier recordings with the Miami Sound Machine, including her musical style and songwriting: "She ditches the synths and employs the London Symphony Orchestra's strings to graceful effect...abandoning melodrama for real color and emotional shading."53 John Lannert described Mi Tierra for the Sun-Sentinel as "Estefan`s Latin 'unplugged' album" and her "most satisfying effort to date."54

At the 36th Grammy Awards, Mi Tierra was the Best Tropical Latin Album.55 At the 6th Lo Nuestro Awards Estefan received two awards in the tropical category, for Female Artist of the Year and Album of the Year.56 The singer was Female Artist of the Year at the first Billboard Latin Music Awards, and Mi Tierra was Album of the Year in the tropical-salsa category.57 At the 1994 Spanish Ondas Awards, Mi Tierra was the Best International Album and Estefan the Best International Artist. It was recognized as the best-selling Latin album of the year with a 1993<U+2013>94 NARM Best Seller Award.58
