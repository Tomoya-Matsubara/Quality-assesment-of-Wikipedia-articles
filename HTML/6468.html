Boxes () is a 2007 French film and the directorial debut of Jane Birkin. Birkin also stars alongside Geraldine Chaplin and Michel Piccoli. The film is based on Birkin's own family life, chronicling three marriages and the three children she bore from these marriages. The title alludes to the way in which she compartmentalises these relationships and stages of her life.1 The film premiered at the 2007 Cannes Film Festival on 21 May.2 It was released in France on 6 June, 2007.

Plot

In Brittany, Anna shares a rambling home with her sometime dead father (Piccoli), her opinionated mother (Chaplin) and three daughters. As Anna struggles with her mid-life crisis, the posessions and photographs in the home begin to spark her memories of childhood and earlier adulthood.

In particular the memories evoked are of her three husbands and the children she bore with them. Her first marriage to Fanny's English father (Hurt) failed and as a consequence, Fanny (R<U+00E9>gnier) barely knows him. Fanny's half-sister is Camille (Doillon), who Anna had with Camille's now dead father, Max (Benichou). There is also her third husband, Jean (Karyo), with whom she had Lilly (Exarchopoulos), but he left to pursue affairs. 3
