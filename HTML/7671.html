Emily Nasrallah Imil<U+012B> Na<U+1E63>r All<U+0101>h <U+0627><U+0645><U+064A><U+0644><U+064A> <U+0646><U+0635><U+0631><U+0627><U+0644><U+0644><U+0647> n<U+00E9>e Emily Abi Rashed on July 6 1931 in Kfeir, Lebanon) is a Lebanese writer and women's rights activist,.1. She graduated from the Beirut University College (now the Lebanese American University) and the American University of Beirut in 1958 with a degree in Education. As well as journalistic articles, Nasrallah is the author of several novels.2

Early life

Emily Abi Rashed was born in small village of al-Kfayr at the western foot of Mount Hermon in southern Lebanon on 6 July 1931,. She received her formal education in the elementary public school of al-Kfayr.3 Emily left her native town when she was only nine years of age, she wanted to go after her brother who was sent to be schooled away from al-Kfayr. Emily wrote to an uncle of hers who had emigrated to West Virginia, he granted her wish and payed for her tuition at Choueifat National College _a boarding school in the suburbs of Beirut_ where she continued her secondary education. Emily taught for two years at the Choueifat school after attending there as a student, she continued her education at theBeirut College for Women4 before majoring with a BE in education and literature from the American University of Beirut in 1958.5 After graduation, Emily taught at the Ahliah school in Beirut where Hanan al-Shaykh had been a pupil.6 Emily had also contributed, while still a student and during her teaching career to Lebanese periodicals and journals such as as-Sayyad (the hunter) and Sawt al-Mar'a (The voice of Women), sometimes using a pen name.78

Emily married Philip Nasrallah, a chemist from Zahleh in 1957 while still in college. The couple had four children: Ramzi, Maha, Khalil, and Mona.9
