Sammy Saeni (a.k.a. Sharzy) is a musician from Solomon Islands who is of Western Province and Malaita province heritage.

Sharzy was born on the island of Simbo in the Western Province. His mother is from Simbo and his father from Malaita. His musical career began in 1995 when he joined the 2-4-1 band. He produced his first solo album "Aloha" in 2001, which became an instant hit. Since then he has released "Aelan feel'n", "Aelan Wei", and "Hem Stret". He sings in many languages including Simbo, Roviana, and Solomon Islands Pijin. He also has tracks in PNG tokpisin and English. Sharzy's utimate aim is to get a foothold in the Australian music scene and use that as launchpad for further forays afield.

His early songs were mostly love songs, but he described his fifth album, released in May 2008, as "different": "My fifth album has a different taste [...] because I sing about things that have occurred in life."1

Sharzy performed during the official launching of the Melanesian Spearhead Group Trade Directory on May 28, 2008. He sang the official summit theme song, <U+201C>One People, Many Cultures<U+201D>.2

He has been described by The Guardian as one of the Solomon Islands' "living national icons".3
