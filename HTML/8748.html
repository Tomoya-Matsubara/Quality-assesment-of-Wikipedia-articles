The discography of Rick Ross, an American rapper, consists of six studio albums, one compilation album, eighty-five singles, including sixty-five as a featured artist, one soundtrack, and forty music videos.

Ross's debut album, Port of Miami, was released in 2006, which features the singles, "Hustlin'" and "Push It". Ross's second album, Trilla was released in 2008, which spawned the singles, "The Boss" which features T-Pain and "Here I Am" which features Nelly and Avery Storm. Ross's third studio album, Deeper Than Rap was released on April 21, 2009. Deeper Than Rap which included the singles, "Magnificent", which features John Legend and "Maybach Music 2", which features Kanye West, Lil Wayne and T-Pain. On July 20, 2010, Ross released his fourth studio album, Teflon Don, which includes the hit single, "Aston Martin Music", which features Drake and Chrisette Michele. Ross has also appeared in many other artist's singles and tracks, including ""All I Do Is Win by DJ Khaled also featuring Ludacris, Snoop Dogg and T-Pain, and "I'm On One" also by Khaled featuring Drake and Lil Wayne. The latter peaked at number 10 on the Hot 100, becoming Ross' most successful song to date, and was also nominated at the 54th Grammy Awards.
