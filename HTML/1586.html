William Joseph Kayatta Jr. (born October 27, 1953) is a United States Circuit Judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit.

Early life and education

Kayatta was born in Pawtucket, Rhode Island in 1953. He earned a bachelor's degree in 1976 from Amherst College and then earned a law degree in 1979 from Harvard Law School.1 After law school, he served as a law clerk for Judge Frank M. Coffin of the United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit from 1979 to 1980.2

Professional career

In 1980, Kayatta joined the Portland, Maine, law firm Pierce Atwood LLP as an associate.3 He became partner in 1986 and has focused his practice on complex trial and appellate litigation.4 He also has argued two cases before the Supreme Court of the United States.5 In 2010, he served as the lead investigator for the American Bar Association committee that reviewed the qualifications of Supreme Court nominee Elena Kagan.6 In April 2011, the Supreme Court of the United States appointed him to serve as special master in an interstate water-rights dispute, Kansas v. Nebraska and Colorado.7

He has served as president of the Maine Bar Foundation.8 In 2010, the Maine Bar Foundation presented him with the Howard H. Dana Award for his career-long pro bono efforts on behalf of low-income Maine citizens.9 He has also received special recognition awards from the Disability Rights Center of Maine, the Maine Equal Justice Partners, and the Maine Children's Alliance for his pro bono representation of disabled Maine children.10 Kayatta is also a former Chair of the Professional Ethics Commission for Maine lawyers.

Nomination to First Circuit

On January 23, 2012, President Obama nominated Kayatta to serve as a judge on the United States Court of Appeals for the First Circuit.11 He received a hearing before the Senate Judiciary Committee on March 14, 2012,12 and his nomination was reported to the floor on April 19, 2012, by voice vote, with Senator Sessions and Senator Lee recorded as voting no.13

Kayatta's confirmation hearing before the Senate Judiciary Committee was uncontroversial.14 His nomination needed only confirmation by the full Senate, but the process was stalled by GOP filibusters for a series of tactical reasons, such as to block the consideration of another of President Obama's judicial appointments, Robert E. Bacharach.15 With the adjournment of the Senate session on January 2, 2013, the nomination expired.

On January 3, 2013, he was renominated to the same office.16 On February 7, 2013, the Senate Judiciary Committee reported his nomination to the floor by voice vote, with Senator Jeff Sessions recorded as a no vote.17 The Senate confirmed his nomination on February 13, 2013, by a vote of 88 ayes to 12 nays.18 He received his commission on February 14, 2013.19 Kayatta will fill the seat vacated by Judge Kermit Lipez, who took senior status at the end of 2011.20

Personal

Kayatta lives in Cape Elizabeth, Maine. His wife is Anne Swift-Kayatta.
