Victoria Brown (born 1985) is an Australian water polo player who has been chosen to represent Australia at the 2012 Summer Olympics.

Personal

The tall Brown, born in 1985 in Victoria, currently resides in Melbourne.12 One of her parents won a bronze medal for Australia at the Commonwealth Games.3 Both represented Australia in fencing.4 The parents believed that she would win an Olympic gold medal in an equestrian event, as she competed in the sport until she was sixteen years old. At that age, she then switched sports to water polo.5 On 31 December 2010, she broke her leg in a New Year's Eve accident. During her recovery, she had to deal with a post-surgery infection.6 In late 2010, she injured her arm and it took time to recover.7

Water polo

Brown is a goalkeeper.89 When playing, she wears a mouth guard.10 Before every game she plays, she brushes her teeth.11

Brown currently plays her club water polo for the Cronulla Water Polo Club in the National Water Polo League.12 She played for the Brisbane Barracudas in 2008.13 The annual match between Breakers and Barracudas is considered is one the Courier Mail considers a grudge match. She participated in the 2008 edition with her team.14 She played for the Victorian Tigers in 2010, when Cronulla played in the finals tournament; in the tournament, she was named in the league final's All Star team.15 She briefly played with Cronulla in 2012 before taking a break following their 18 February 2012 game against the Balmain Tigers to attend the national team training camp.16

Brown is a member of the Australia women's national water polo team.17 She was named in the team that competed in the preliminary round at the 2008 FINA World League in Tianjin, China.18 In preparation for the Games, her coach Greg McFadden, instructed Brown and every player on the team to sit down for dinner and eat everything on their plate.19She was the last player cut before the 2008 Summer Olympics squad was finalised, right before the team left for Beijing, China.20 She used this to motivate herself to become one of the best goalkeepers in Australia.21 She represented Australia at the 2010 FINA Women's Water Polo World Cup in Christchurch, New Zealand.2223 She was a member of the Australian team that competed at the Pan Pacific Championships in 2010,24 the year she was named the Australian Stinger's Player of the Year. She also secured her position as the team's number one goalkeeper.25

In 2011, Brown missed most of the national team season because of a broken leg and an injured arm.26 Nevertheless, in April 2011, she attended a training camp at the Australian Institute of Sport (AIS) where the coach was "selecting a team for the major championships over winter."27 In February 2012, she was named in the final training squad for the 2012 Summer Olympics, and thus attended a training camp starting on 20 February 2012 at the AIS.28 The team of seventeen players will be cut to thirteen before the team departs for the Olympic games,29 with the announcement being made on June 13.30 She was part of the Stingers squad that competed in a five game test against Great Britain at the AIS in late February 2012. This was the team's first matches against Great Britain's national team in six years.31
