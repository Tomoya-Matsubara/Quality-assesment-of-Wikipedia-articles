Tin City Long Range Radar Station is a military use airport located one nautical mile (2 km) east of the central business district of Tin City, in the Nome Census Area of the U.S. state of Alaska. It is owned by the U.S. Air Force.1

Facilities and aircraft

Tin City Long Range Radar Station has one runway designated 16/34 with a 4,700 x 100 ft (1,433 x 30 m) gravel surface. For the 12-month period ending July 9, 1980, the airport had 350 aircraft operations: 57% air taxi, 29% general aviation and 14% military.2
