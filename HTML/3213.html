Ayd<U+0131>n Karabulut (born 25 January 1988, Berlin) is a Turkish-German footballer. He currently plays for Turkish Super Lig club Sivasspor.

Career

Ayd<U+0131>n had started his career in Hertha Berlin, one of the leading teams in German capital where he was born. After a while, he pulled the attention of Turkish national team scouts as a promising starlet. He had 5 caps for U-19 national team. He joined Be<U+015F>ikta<U+015F> January 2006. His contract came into force on 8 February 2006 which will expire in May 2010. Ayd<U+0131>n had several recent occasions on Turkcell Super League in first season mostly as a substitute player.

On 16 February 2008, he was on the line-up of team up against Ankaraspor where Ayd<U+0131>n assisted Nobre by his corner kick. Subsequently, he tried to find the net by his own again with a corner kick, however he was denied by the opponent keeper Seneck<U+00FD> after his direct curving shoot.1 He scored his first ever goal for Be<U+015F>ikta<U+015F> on 5 January 2008 in Fortis Turkey Cup group match against Diyarbak<U+0131>r B<U+015E>B.2 Ayd<U+0131>n kept playing as a substitute player for his team and scored his very first goal in Turkish League on a crucial encounter against Trabzonspor.3

Ayd<U+0131>n has joined capital city team Ankaraspor in summer 2009, in return of Erhan G<U+00FC>ven, who joined Be<U+015F>ikta<U+015F> as the new defender.4

On 6 September 2013, Karabulut left Elaz<U+0131><U+011F>spor and joined fellow Super Lig side Sivasspor.5
