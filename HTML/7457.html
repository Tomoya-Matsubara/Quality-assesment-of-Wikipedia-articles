    • Leinster won its second European title with a 33<U+2013>22 win over Northampton Saints.

Domestic competitions
  • English Premiership – Final, 28 May at Twickenham: Leicester Tigers vs. Saracens
    • Saracens defeated Leicester Tigers 22<U+2013>18 for their first-ever Premiership title.
  • RFU Championship <U+2014> Worcester Warriors. As the only side among the semifinalists that met the requirements for promotion, they replaced Leeds Carnegie in the 2011<U+2013>12 Premiership.
  • Top 14 – Final, 4 June at Stade de France: Toulouse vs. Montpellier
    • Toulouse won 15<U+2013>10 and lifted the Bouclier de Brennus for the 18th time.
  • Rugby Pro D2 <U+2014> Lyon won the championship and automatic promotion to the Top 14. Bordeaux B<U+00E8>gles won the promotion playoffs. The two clubs will replace La Rochelle and Bourgoin.
  • Celtic League – Grand Final, 28 May in Limerick:
    • In an all-Irish affair, Munster won their third title against Leinster 19<U+2013>9.
  • LV Cup (Anglo-Welsh Cup) – Gloucester
  • ITM Cup:
    • Premiership <U+2013> Final, 3 September in Hamilton: Waikato vs. Canterbury
      • Canterbury won 12<U+2212>3 for their fourth consecutive title in the Air New Zealand/ITM Cup and ninth in the history of New Zealand provincial rugby.
    • Championship: Final, 4 September in Palmerston North: Manawatu vs. Hawke's Bay
      • Hawke's Bay won 35<U+2212>30 and replaced Southland in the 2012 ITM Cup Premiership.
  • Currie Cup: Final, 29 October in Johannesburg: Golden Lions vs. Natal Sharks
    • The Lions won 42<U+2013>16 in the most one-sided Currie Cup final since 1980.
Other major events
  • 26 February: During the England<U+2013>France match in the Six Nations, England's Jonny Wilkinson retakes the all-time lead for career Test points from New Zealand's Dan Carter.
  • 27 February: During the Scotland<U+2013>Ireland match in the Six Nations, Ireland's Ronan O'Gara retakes the all-time lead for career points in the Championship from Wilkinson.
  • 19 March: During the Ireland<U+2013>England Six Nations match, two Irish players reach major career milestones in the Championship:
    • Brian O'Driscoll takes over the all-time lead for career tries in the Championship with his 25th try, breaking the record of Scotland's Ian Smith that had lasted since 1933.
    • O'Gara makes his 56th appearance in the Championship, drawing level with countryman Mike Gibson for the Championship record.
  • 30 July: During New Zealand's Tri Nations opener at home to South Africa, Carter reclaims the all-time lead for career Test points from Wilkinson.
  • December: Owen Sheers is appointed to be writer in residence at the WRU. This seems to be the first such appointment by any national rugby body.
